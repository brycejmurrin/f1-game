# Red Bull Ring — visual reference

Spielberg, Styria, Austria. Austrian Grand Prix. A compact 4.3 km clockwise
circuit set in a natural bowl in the Styrian uplands, ringed by green hills and
distant alpine peaks. Famous for big elevation change, wide green/gravel runoff,
saturated summer-alpine light, and ubiquitous Red Bull branding crowned by the
giant rust-steel charging-bull sculpture.

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- The circuit is "set in an idyllic natural bowl in the Styrian mountains,"
  "nestled in the heart of the Styrian Alps, near Spielberg." It sits **over
  2,000 ft (~660 m) above sea level**, thin enough air to affect engine/aero
  setup. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Bull_Ring),
  [GP Days](https://gpdays.com/blogs/gp-insights/red-bull-ring)
- The setting is widely called "what might be the most picturesque race on the
  Formula 1 calendar… the track high up in the Styrian Hills," with a "stunning
  backdrop of the Styrian mountains." Mountains "surround the track on all
  sides, even to the south beyond the valley floor that stretches off into the
  distance." The mountains act as a "natural amphitheater, amplifying the roar of
  engines." [motorsporttickets](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/austria-f1-grand-prix-seating-guide-best-places-to-sit/),
  [summerstirs](https://summerstirs.com/what-is-the-red-bull-ring/)
- Surrounding landscape: "forest, fields, and hills"; "rolling hills and dense
  forests"; "the steep climbs and the way the corners connect make it a pure
  driving playground in the heart of the Alps." Embedded in natural scenery with
  "significant differences in altitude." [51GT3](https://51gt3.com/en/track/red-bull-ring),
  [redbullring.com](https://www.redbullring.com/en/events-tickets/formula-1/formula-1-circuit/)
- Elevation: **~65 m of total elevation change**, **max incline 12%**, **max
  decline 9.3%** over 10 corners. The lap opens with an uphill charge to Turn 1,
  then climbs again toward Turn 3 (the steepest gradient), then drops downhill
  through the back of the lap. [Yahoo/F1 corner guide](https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/red-bull-ring-track-guide-113431738.html),
  [redbullring.com](https://www.redbullring.com/en/events-tickets/formula-1/formula-1-circuit/)
- Atmosphere: alpine weather is "famously unpredictable — a sunny morning can
  quickly give way to a sudden thunderstorm." Race is a summer day race (late
  June / early July). [GP Days](https://gpdays.com/blogs/gp-insights/red-bull-ring)

## Terrain & vegetation

- **Hilly alpine bowl.** The whole track is built into a hillside: a constant
  3-D rollercoaster. Key grades: **uphill out of Turn 1**, a **steep climb to
  Turn 3** (12% max), then a **long descent** through the mid/back section
  (9.3% max), ~65 m top-to-bottom. [Yahoo/F1 corner guide](https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/red-bull-ring-track-guide-113431738.html)
- **Green valley floor + rolling foothills** immediately around the track, then
  **forested hillsides**, then **grey-blue distant mountains** on all sides
  including south across the valley. A layered near→mid→far silhouette.
  [summerstirs](https://summerstirs.com/what-is-the-red-bull-ring/)
- **Vegetation = Austrian conifer / spruce forest** (dark blue-green, tall,
  pointed) interspersed with **bright green alpine meadows and pasture**. The
  green is lush and saturated — this is high-summer alpine grassland, not the dry
  bleached look of a desert circuit. Forest stands ring the outer runoff and
  climb the surrounding hills. [51GT3](https://51gt3.com/en/track/red-bull-ring)
- **Wide green runoff and gravel traps.** The Red Bull Ring has "comparatively
  wide runoff zones" — much of the immediate verge is **mown green grass**, with
  **beige/grey gravel traps** at heavy braking corners (notably Turn 4, plus T1
  and T3 exits). [Yahoo/F1 corner guide](https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/red-bull-ring-track-guide-113431738.html),
  [motorsport.com kerbs](https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/why-new-perfect-red-bull-ring-kerb-solution-could-banish-f1s-track-limits-problem/10627857/)

## Grandstands, buildings, kerbs, surface, banking

- **Layout:** 4.318 km clockwise, **10 corners**, named — T1 **Niki Lauda Kurve**
  (uphill 90° right, renamed 2019), T2 Münzer, **T3 Remus** (slow right at the
  top of the steep climb, the key overtaking spot), **T4 Rauch** (heavy braking,
  has a gravel trap), T6 Rauch Kurve, **T7 Würth/Graz Kurve**, **T9 Jochen Rindt
  Kurve** (fast, near the bull), T10. [Yahoo/F1 corner guide](https://sports.yahoo.com/articles/red-bull-ring-track-guide-113431738.html),
  [motorsportradar](https://motorsportradar.com/tracks/red-bull-ring/)
- **Grandstands** (named, mostly modern roofed steel stands):
  - **Steiermark / Süd-West (South-West)** — outside the run into Turn 1, the
    **most elevated** stand, overlooks the grid & T1.
  - **Red Bull grandstand (West)** — the **biggest**, 15 sections, runs from
    exit of Turn 1 all the way up the climb to Turn 3.
  - **T3 grandstand** — atop the steepest incline, over the main overtaking zone.
  - **Mitte / Centre grandstand** — in the infield "Yellow Zone," **right next
    to the steel bull**.
  - **Nord grandstand** — outside the old Bosch Kurve, "one of F1's best views."
  - **T8 / T9 / T10 grandstands** — over the fast final turns, near the bull.
  [motorsporttickets](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/austria-f1-grand-prix-seating-guide-best-places-to-sit/),
  [fanamp](https://www.fanamp.com/tr/choosing-your-seats-at-the-austrian-gp),
  [oversteer48](https://oversteer48.com/red-bull-ring-general-admission-views-tips-guide/)
- **Kerbs:** mostly **red-and-white** painted kerbs, plus the notorious
  **yellow sausage kerbs** (rigid rounded blisters, max 12 cm high, behind an
  80 cm flat kerb) on corner exits (e.g. T1, T3). [motorsport.com](https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/why-new-perfect-red-bull-ring-kerb-solution-could-banish-f1s-track-limits-problem/10627857/),
  [racefans](https://www.racefans.net/2016/06/30/new-kerbs-address-track-limits-concerns-austria/)
- **Surface:** standard dark asphalt, smooth and grippy; white track-limit
  lines, with the kerb effectively widened to ~1.8 m before gravel at some
  corners. [motorsport.com](https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/why-new-perfect-red-bull-ring-kerb-solution-could-banish-f1s-track-limits-problem/10627857/)
- **Banking:** the circuit is famous for **gradient (slope), not lateral
  banking** — corners are largely flat/cambered, the drama is the up/down
  elevation. No significant banked corners.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Day race, high-summer European alpine light** (late June / early July). Warm
  but crisp; deep blue sky over saturated green hills (when not interrupted by an
  alpine thunderstorm). Long-ish shadows fit a raking summer sun on a hillside
  bowl. [GP Days](https://gpdays.com/blogs/gp-insights/red-bull-ring)
- **National identity:** Austrian **red-white-red**. Strong Styrian/Austrian
  feel.
- **Sponsor identity = Red Bull palette:** **navy blue, red, silver, and yellow**
  (the bull's gold horns add a metallic gold accent). Branding is everywhere —
  hoardings, pylons, painted infrastructure, the wing, and the bull. Race-day is
  dominated by the **"Orange Army"** (Max Verstappen fans) so **bright Dutch
  orange** is a major crowd colour too. [redbullring.com](https://www.redbullring.com/en/), brand-standard Red Bull colours.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current file: `js/tracks/redbull.js` (theme `green`, `night:false`). It is
already a fairly rich build — most "missing" items from the brief are in fact
present. Real gaps are accuracy/placement, not absence:

1. **Bull sculpture colour is wrong.** Game builds the bull body/head/legs in
   near-black `dark = [0.10,0.10,0.12]` with **white** horns. Reality: body is
   **rusted brown-orange Corten steel**, horns are **gold-leafed**, and it leaps
   through a **steel arch** (game arch is white). Should be warm rust + gold.
2. **Bull is in the wrong place.** Game anchors it at **s≈0.10 (near Turn 1)**.
   Reality: it sits in the **infield by the Mitte/T8 area** (~mid-to-late lap,
   inner side), visible across the bowl. Consider moving/duplicating its visual
   prominence toward the infield rather than the T1 climb.
3. **The Wing is generic, not wing-shaped.** Game pit building is a plain box +
   flat roof blade (`prop` + `addBox`). The real **voestalpine wing** is a
   distinctive **>90 m rear-wing/spoiler silhouette** — an upswept blade form,
   white steel + glass. Current build does not read as a wing.
4. **Mountains may read too tall/snowy.** Game outer ring is `hMin 140 + up to
   100`, snowline 0.50 (lots of snow). The real backdrop is **green forested
   foothills + modest grey-blue peaks**, generally **not heavily snow-capped in
   summer**. Risk of an over-dramatic Swiss-Alps look vs the gentler Styrian
   uplands. Tune peak height down and snowline up (less snow), keep more green.
5. **Kerb colour cue.** Tyre walls use Red Bull colours (good), but there is no
   explicit **yellow sausage-kerb** visual cue — a signature Red Bull Ring
   detail. (Kerbs may be handled by the core road builder; verify.)
6. **Bull horns / arch are present but mis-coloured** — see #1.
7. Branding palette is good (`rbRed/rbNavy/rbYel`), but **silver/grey** and a
   touch of **gold** would better match Red Bull's actual livery; orange is
   already used (Orange Army billboard) — could lean into it more in the crowd.

Items the brief flagged as "likely missing" that are actually **present**:
alpine mountain backdrop (two mountain rings), Red Bull branding/pylons, a bull
sculpture, and a tower/pylon. The grass-bank spectator hillsides and conifer
forests are also present and good.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Recolour the bull (high impact).** Change `dark = [0.10,0.10,0.12]` to a
   **rusted Corten brown-orange**, e.g. `[0.45, 0.22, 0.12]` (warm rust), and
   change the **horns from white to gold** `[0.85, 0.68, 0.22]`. Tint the arch
   from white toward **dark/grey steel** `[0.30, 0.30, 0.33]`. Keep the gold
   pedestal spotlight. This single fix makes the most iconic landmark read true.
2. **Relocate / re-emphasise the bull toward the infield (s≈0.55–0.75, inner
   side).** Either move the `kb = n*0.10` anchor to the late-lap infield, or add
   a second hero placement near the stadium so it's the landmark over the fast
   final sector (matches the real Mitte/T8 position) and is visible from the
   stadium grandstands.
3. **Make the pit building wing-shaped.** Replace the flat roof blade with an
   **upswept spoiler profile**: a long thin **angled blade** (front lower, rear
   raised) over slim pillars, white steel `[0.92,0.93,0.95]` with a glass band,
   ≥70 m long to echo the >90 m real wing. Even a simple wedge/angled-prism roof
   reads far more "Red Bull wing" than a flat box.
4. **Soften the mountains to Styrian uplands.** Lower the outer-ring `hMin`
   (e.g. 140→90) and **raise the snowline** (0.50→0.80 outer, 0.75→0.90 inner)
   so peaks are **mostly forest-green with only light caps**. Keep the layered
   near-green-hills → far-blue-peaks gradient; the brief's "rolling green hills"
   is the dominant note, not snow.
5. **Add yellow sausage-kerb accents** at T1 and T3 exits if kerbs are
   per-track-tintable — short **yellow** (`[0.95,0.80,0.10]`) blister strips on
   the exit kerbs. Signature visual of this circuit.
6. **Keep/boost lush saturated green.** The `pal.grass [0.20,0.48,0.15]` is good;
   ensure the verge/forest stays **vivid blue-green spruce** (dark, cool, pointed
   pines dominate — `pineFrac` already high, good). Avoid any dry/bleached verge
   tint here.
7. **Lean into the Red Bull palette + gold.** Add a couple of **silver/grey**
   and **gold-capped** pylons alongside the navy/red ones, and keep the Orange
   Army orange in crowd/billboards. The gold echoes the bull's horns and the
   "Schlossgold/Rauch" sponsor identity.
8. **Atmosphere:** keep day, warm crisp summer light, deep blue zenith. A
   **raking (lower) summer sun** for long shadows down the hillside suits the
   bowl better than a flat overhead sun.
