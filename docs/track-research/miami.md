# Miami — visual reference

Visual research for the Miami International Autodrome (Miami Grand Prix), the
temporary circuit wrapped around **Hard Rock Stadium** in **Miami Gardens,
Florida** — a flat, land-locked suburb roughly **15–16 miles (26 km) north of
downtown Miami**. This doc captures real-world visual facts for procedural
scenery and flags where the current game build diverges from reality.

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Suburban, NOT downtown.** "The Miami Grand Prix takes place at the Miami
  International Autodrome located around the grounds and private facilities of
  Hard Rock Stadium in Miami Gardens, Florida, a suburb of Miami located 16
  miles (26 km) north of downtown Miami."
  [Miami Grand Prix — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami_Grand_Prix)
- **Built in a parking-lot campus.** "The permanent asphalt pathways of the
  circuit are integrated into the Hard Rock Stadium parking areas… the lap
  threads between perimeter roads and purpose-built sections laid across the
  stadium's parking areas, with the venue's main bowl and grandstand clusters
  forming a constant visual reference."
  [Miami International Autodrome — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami_International_Autodrome)
- **The "fake marina."** "Turns 7 and 8 are one decreasing-radius 150° left,
  going past a number of large corporate suites on the outside of the bend, and
  an area dressed up as a marina (complete with fake boats and blue-painted
  asphalt) on the inside."
  [RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/north-america/usa/miami-international-autodrome.html)
- **Fake marina = real boats on stands + painted plywood/vinyl water.** "The
  marina had 10 real boats on trailers, surrounded by 25,000 feet of plywood
  that came up to their waterlines, and the plywood was covered with a vinyl
  decal to look like turquoise water." The site is a "land-locked neighborhood";
  the boats are real but "the water is just plywood that's been covered in a
  decal."
  [Robb Report](https://robbreport.com/motors/marine/miamis-inaugural-grand-prix-success-but-fake-marina-1234678795/) ·
  [Sports Illustrated](https://www.si.com/formula1/2022/05/07/f1-miami-gps-fake-water-in-its-marina-becomes-talk-of-the-town)
- **"Skyline views" is promotional puffery.** Promo copy claims the circuit
  "offers picturesque views of Miami's skyline," but the venue is 15–16 miles
  north of downtown over flat suburban terrain — there is **no downtown skyline
  backdrop** in the actual sightlines. Reporting consistently calls the site a
  "land-locked neighborhood" surrounded by stadium parking, low-rise suburbia
  and the Florida Turnpike.
  [Miami Grand Prix — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami_Grand_Prix) ·
  [Robb Report](https://robbreport.com/motors/marine/miamis-inaugural-grand-prix-success-but-fake-marina-1234678795/)
- **2026 campus is themed by Miami neighborhoods** (decor/hospitality, not real
  buildings): Wynwood (street-art murals), Miami Beach ("The Beach" — Art Deco,
  lifeguard towers, sand, colorful lounge seating), Little Havana, Brickell, Key
  Biscayne, Coconut Grove, Miami Gardens.
  [F1 Miami GP press release](https://f1miamigp.com/news/press-release/neighborhood-inspired-campus-2026/) ·
  [NBC 6 South Florida](https://www.nbcmiami.com/news/local/miami-grand-prix-neighborhood-campus-food-music-f1-race/3792024/)

## Skyline & architecture

- **There is no dense skyline.** The circuit sits in flat, low-rise suburban
  Miami Gardens. The dominant vertical feature on site is **Hard Rock Stadium
  and its four ~109 m canopy spires** — everything else is parking lots,
  low-rise campus/utility buildings, parking structures, and the Florida
  Turnpike flyover.
- **The "Miami" identity is decoration, not buildings.** The tropical/pastel
  Art-Deco look comes from branding, run-off paint, hospitality structures and
  the 2026 neighborhood-themed campus zones — not from a real waterfront city
  behind the track.
- Downtown Miami's glass-tower skyline (Brickell etc.) is **16 miles away and
  not visible** from the track. Any tall pastel-glass skyscraper ring is
  fictional for this venue.
  [Miami Grand Prix — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami_Grand_Prix) ·
  [F1 Miami GP campus](https://f1miamigp.com/news/press-release/neighborhood-inspired-campus-2026/)

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Signature aqua run-off.** The circuit's most recognizable surface feature is
  the **Miami Dolphins aqua-blue run-off paint with palm-tree patterns** — "the
  now-iconic aqua paint that's become synonymous with the Miami GP," applied as
  FIA-homologated anti-skid paint over sweeping run-off areas, alongside bold
  trackside palm-tree graphics and "Miami" script.
  [Roadgrip — Miami run-off branding](https://roadgrip.co.uk/projects/run-off-branding-and-line-painting-miami-grand-prix-f1/) ·
  [Roadgrip — track painting/grip](https://roadgrip.co.uk/projects/track-painting-grip-improvement-miami-f1/)
- **Barriers**: standard temporary-circuit concrete blocks + TecPro + steel
  debris fencing, dressed with sponsor boards (Crypto.com is title sponsor —
  "Formula 1 Crypto.com Miami Grand Prix"). Branding skews **aqua/teal, pink,
  coral, white** (Miami Vice palette).
  [F1 Miami GP](https://f1miamigp.com/the-circuit/)
- **Grandstands**: temporary scaffold stands clustered around the stadium and
  along the main straight, in Miami's bright pastel branding.
- **Surface**: fresh, smooth, light-to-mid grey asphalt (purpose-built lot
  pathways + perimeter roads); the start/pit area is the permanent stadium
  apron. Notably flat — minimal elevation change except a gentle rise through
  the stadium section.
  [Wikipedia — Miami International Autodrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami_International_Autodrome)

## Vegetation & water

- **Palms everywhere.** Tall palm trees line the boulevards, run-offs and
  hospitality areas — the signature Florida vegetation. Tropical landscaping and
  planters dress the campus. The terrain is dead flat Florida flatland.
- **No natural water.** Despite the "marina," there is **no real water** at the
  venue — it is land-locked. The only "water" is the **turquoise painted
  plywood/vinyl decal** of the fake marina. There are no lagoons, mangroves or
  open water as a natural backdrop.
  [Sports Illustrated — fake water](https://www.si.com/formula1/2022/05/07/f1-miami-gps-fake-water-in-its-marina-becomes-talk-of-the-town) ·
  [Robb Report](https://robbreport.com/motors/marine/miamis-inaugural-grand-prix-success-but-fake-marina-1234678795/)

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Bright tropical daytime.** Race is held in early May (2026: May 1–3) under
  intense Florida sun, deep blue sky, high humidity haze, strong shadows.
  [Formula1.com — Miami 2026](https://www.formula1.com/en/racing/2026/miami)
- **Miami Vice / Art-Deco palette**: cyan/teal (~#0BD2D3), hot pink (~#F990E8),
  white, black, plus coral/aqua. This drives branding, billboards, run-off
  graphics and the 2026 "Beach" zone (Art-Deco aesthetic, lifeguard towers,
  pastel lounge furniture).
  [Miami Vice palette — SchemeColor](https://www.schemecolor.com/miami-vice-logo.php) ·
  [F1 Miami GP campus](https://f1miamigp.com/news/press-release/neighborhood-inspired-campus-2026/)
- **Sponsorship/identity**: **Crypto.com** title sponsor; Dolphins aqua;
  flamingo/palm/sunset motifs in event branding. Hard Rock Stadium's four corner
  spires are dramatically lit at night.
  [F1 Miami GP](https://f1miamigp.com/the-circuit/) ·

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current build = `js/tracks/miami.js`, theme `modern`, day.

1. **WRONG: Downtown skyline backdrop (the big one).** The file builds a
   36-element downtown skyline — `24 tall skyscrapers` up to `bH = 60 + h0*100 +
   60 ≈ 220 m`, plus `12 mid-rise pastel blocks`, all 360–800 m out, ringing the
   main straight / T1 / back straight (`miami.js` lines ~116–157). **This does
   not exist.** Miami Gardens is flat suburb 16 miles from downtown; no skyline
   is visible. This is the single most inaccurate element and should be removed
   or drastically reduced.
2. **WRONG / overbuilt: pastel-glass "cityFront" hospitality towers everywhere.**
   Multiple `cityFront(...)` calls (lines ~293, 321, 416, 431, 469, 492) raise
   tall pastel-glass facades (`maxH` up to 38 m) all around the lap. Real
   surroundings are low-rise campus, parking structures and temporary
   hospitality — not a continuous mid-rise glass cityscape. Keep low-rise/boxy
   only.
3. **PARTIALLY WRONG: marina rendered as real deep water + floating superyacht.**
   Lines ~328–407 build genuine blue/teal `groundPlane` water slabs
   (`WATER_DEEP [0.08,0.32,0.48]`, `WATER [0.13,0.46,0.62]`), 50 moored yachts
   that float, plus a 64 m **floating superyacht with a helipad**. The real
   marina is a **fake**: ~10 boats on **trailers/stands**, on **turquoise painted
   plywood/vinyl**, not floating, no superyacht-with-helipad. The water color is
   also too dark/navy — real decal is bright **turquoise**.
4. **MISSING: Hard Rock Stadium's defining feature — the open-air canopy roof +
   four ~109 m corner spires.** The build does a bowl + a generic curved
   `addFrustum` "roof cap" + 6 short floodlight masts (lines ~159–232). It lacks
   the signature **flat open-air canopy** and the **four tall corner spires**
   that are the stadium's identity. The bowl color uses CORAL/TEAL rim crowns;
   real stadium shell reads white/grey with **aqua interior seats**.
5. **MISSING: signature aqua run-off with palm-tree graphics.** The palette uses
   a tan `runoff: [0.56,0.48,0.34]`. The real run-off's defining trait is **Dolphins
   aqua paint with palm-tree patterns** — currently absent. This is arguably the
   most recognizable surface cue of the whole circuit.
6. **MOSTLY OK: palms.** Heavy palm coverage is correct and well done.
7. **OK-ish: Turnpike overpass.** Two `buildOverpass()` decks on pillars (lines
   ~437–443, labeled "Florida Turnpike") are plausible — but they're labeled in
   comments as the Hard Rock overpass elsewhere; the real elevated highway is the
   Florida Turnpike running along the circuit's western edge.
8. **MISSING: parking-lot ground character.** The circuit literally runs across
   stadium parking lots — wide flat asphalt aprons, light poles, painted lot
   lines. Currently dressed as a generic street city instead.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Delete the downtown skyline.** Remove (or cut to near-zero) the 24-tower +
   12-block `backdrop()` skyline loop (lines ~116–157). Replace the horizon with
   a **low, flat suburban band**: scattered low-rise boxes, a few parking
   structures, treelines, and haze — nothing tall except the stadium.
2. **Make Hard Rock Stadium the hero and get it right.**
   - Keep the elliptical bowl but recolor the shell to **white/light grey**;
     make the visible interior **aqua** (Dolphins seats), not coral/teal rim.
   - Replace the frustum "roof cap" with a **flat open-air canopy ring** (a thin
     wide oversailing roof that shades the bowl, open center).
   - Add **four tall corner spires (~100–109 m)** — slim white masts with
     cable-stay lines, emissive/lit at night. These should be the tallest
     objects on the whole map and visible from most of the lap.
3. **Rebuild the marina as a FAKE marina.**
   - Replace deep-water `groundPlane` slabs with a **bright turquoise painted
     "water" plane** (~`[0.20,0.78,0.74]`–`[0.15,0.70,0.80]`), flat on the
     ground, reading as paint not sea.
   - Put boats **on trailers/cradles sitting on the painted surface** (lift ~0,
     not floating at +1.2 m), ~10 of them, varied white hulls with teal/coral
     trim.
   - **Remove the floating superyacht-with-helipad**; instead add dockside
     hospitality marquees, a "Beach Club" sand patch, and small docks. Optionally
     one large boat on a big cradle, but no open-sea megayacht.
4. **Add the signature aqua run-off.** Where run-off is rendered (or via a
   painted ground decal near corners), use **Dolphins aqua** (`~[0.0,0.78,0.80]`)
   with simple **palm-tree silhouette** billboard/decal accents. This single
   change makes the circuit instantly recognizable.
5. **Demote `cityFront` to low-rise campus.** Cap building heights low
   (`maxH ≈ 12–16`), use boxy white/grey functional + tented hospitality
   structures and **parking decks**, not continuous pastel-glass curtain walls.
   Keep pastel branding on billboards/barriers, not on tower facades.
6. **Lean into branding for the "Miami" feel** (since the real flavor is decor,
   not architecture): more **aqua/pink/coral sponsor boards**, "Miami" script
   billboards, Crypto.com boards, Art-Deco "Beach" zone props (lifeguard towers,
   pastel cabanas, sand), Wynwood-style mural panels — all low to the ground.
7. **Add parking-lot texture** to the verges/infield: flat grey asphalt aprons,
   light poles, painted lot lines, low fencing — reflecting that the track is
   laid across stadium parking.
8. **Keep**: heavy palms, flat terrain, bright tropical day palette, the Turnpike
   overpass (relabel as Florida Turnpike), the gentle stadium-section rise.
