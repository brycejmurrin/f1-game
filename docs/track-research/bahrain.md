# Bahrain International Circuit (Sakhir) — visual reference

Research compiled for Apex 26 procedural scenery. Sources cited inline with real
URLs. The circuit sits in the Sakhir desert ~30 km SW of Manama, designed by
Hermann Tilke, opened April 2004, and run as an F1 **night race since 2014**.

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Pure desert setting, sparse and flat.** The track is "located in the middle
  of sand dunes" in "the heart of the Sakhir desert," surrounded by undulating
  golden sand. The landscape is otherwise barren and very open — the dominant
  surrounding feature is empty desert, not buildings or vegetation. ([Tilke /
  RacingCircuits via search], [Old Stadium Journey](https://oldstadiumjourney.com/bahrain-international-circuit-discovering-the-desert-oasis-of-motorsport/))
- **Sand colour is pale-to-golden, not red.** The Sakhir desert is described as
  "undulating golden sands… against a clear blue sky." Limestone substrate gives
  a pale, almost glowing tone under strong sun (not the reddish dune sand of
  Arabia's Empty Quarter). ([Evendo – Sakhir Desert View Point](https://evendo.com/locations/bahrain/hawar-islands/attraction/sakhir-desert-view-point),
  [Evendo – Jabal ad Dukhan](https://evendo.com/locations/bahrain/jebel-dukhan/landmark/jabal-ad-dukhan))
- **Nearest "mountain" is Jebel Dukhan (Jabal ad Dukhan), 3 km east.** Bahrain's
  highest point at only **134 m (440 ft)** — a low pale **limestone hill**, not a
  dramatic peak. The wider region is "a barren landscape dotted with oil pumps and
  rusty pipelines." So any distant relief should be a single low pale ridge, very
  modest. ([Evendo – Jabal ad Dukhan](https://evendo.com/locations/bahrain/jebel-dukhan/landmark/jabal-ad-dukhan),
  [Mapcarta](https://mapcarta.com/W902491960))
- **Sand-on-track is a defining problem.** Organisers spray adhesive on the sand
  around the circuit and use landscaping/barriers to stop sand blowing onto the
  asphalt — so the immediate verge is managed/sprayed sand, not loose dune.
  ([Old Stadium Journey](https://oldstadiumjourney.com/bahrain-international-circuit-discovering-the-desert-oasis-of-motorsport/),
  [51GT3](https://51gt3.com/en/track/bahrain-international-circuit-oasis))
- **Illuminated palm trees line the track** as part of the floodlighting scheme —
  one of the few green/vegetation elements, and they glow at night. ([Symonds /
  search summary], [Old Stadium Journey](https://oldstadiumjourney.com/bahrain-international-circuit-discovering-the-desert-oasis-of-motorsport/))
- **The "Oasis" name** refers to the BIC's inner/secondary circuit and the
  broader "Bahrain International Circuit Oasis Complex" (drag strip, karting,
  driving school) — it is a complex name, not a literal lake; do not over-build a
  green oasis. ([RacingCircuits via search](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/bahrain/bahrain-international-circuit.html),
  [51GT3](https://51gt3.com/en/track/bahrain-international-circuit-oasis))

## Skyline & architecture / terrain

- **There is no city skyline.** Unlike a street circuit, BIC is isolated desert
  — the backdrop is flat sand to a low far horizon, with the **single Sakhir
  Tower** as the lone tall vertical and a low pale **Jebel Dukhan ridge** ~3 km
  east. Manama's skyline is ~30 km away and effectively **not visible** from the
  track. (Avoid a Manama skyline backdrop — see gap analysis.) ([RacingCircuits via search](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/bahrain/bahrain-international-circuit.html))
- **Architectural language = Tilke "Arabic desert modern":** sand/beige stone,
  **tent-style tensile-membrane roofs** over pit building and grandstands, and the
  sail-canopy tower. "Emphasis was placed on the local architecture… Arabic
  tent-style architecture, integrating indigenous characteristics." ([Autopedia/Wikipedia via search](https://automobile.fandom.com/wiki/Bahrain_International_Circuit))
- **Terrain is essentially flat** (the track has only gentle elevation changes);
  surrounding ground is low rolling sand. Distant relief should be one subtle pale
  hill, not a ring of dunes/mountains. ([Old Stadium Journey](https://oldstadiumjourney.com/bahrain-international-circuit-discovering-the-desert-oasis-of-motorsport/))

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Track surface:** special asphalt of **graywacke aggregate** shipped from
  Bayston Hill quarry, Shropshire (UK), plus **calcined bauxite** for grip — an
  abrasive, dark, high-grip surface. Tone reads dark grey-black asphalt against
  pale sand (high contrast). ([F1 Chronicle](https://f1chronicle.com/is-bahrain-f1-an-asphalt-circuit/),
  [Shropshire Star](https://www.shropshirestar.com/editors-picks/2014/04/05/shropshire-quarrys-rock-builds-bahrain-f1-race-circuit/))
- **Run-off:** very generous — **140,000 m² total run-off**, largely **asphalt**
  with **gravel traps** at heavy-braking points (e.g. the Turn 4 gravel bed was
  extended for 2025). So model wide pale-grey asphalt aprons plus pale-sand gravel
  traps at the slow corners. ([Tracing Insights – 2025 Bahrain GP](https://tracinginsights.com/blog/216484/all-you-need-to-know-about-the-2025-bahrain-grand-prix/))
- **Kerbs:** standard FIA **red-and-white** kerbing (some F1 venues use national
  colours, but Bahrain uses conventional red/white serrated kerbs with flat
  outer "astroturf"/painted run-off strips and occasional sausage kerbs).
  ([TheSportsRush – F1 kerbs](https://thesportsrush.com/f1-news-kerbs-f1-what-is-the-use-of-kerbs-in-f1-what-is-a-sausage-kerb/))
- **Barriers:** concrete walls fronted by **TecPro/tyre barriers** and tall
  **debris fencing**, carrying **Arabesque-patterned national branding** and
  sponsor boards. Roadgrip applied "intricate Arabesque patterns and National
  Branding" trackside for the GP. ([Roadgrip](https://roadgrip.co.uk/projects/track-design-bahrain-f1-grand-prix-circuit/))
- **Grandstands:** open tubular-steel terraces under **white/pale tensile shade
  canopies**, each with a large TV screen; capacity ~45,000 total (main stand
  ~10,000–10,500). ([motorsporttickets](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/bahrain-grand-prix-grandstands/),
  [grandprixgrandtours](https://www.grandprixgrandtours.com/bahrain-circuit-guide/))

## Vegetation & water

- **Date palms** are the signature planting — rows of palms line parts of the
  track and are **floodlit at night**. They are the main green accent in an
  otherwise tan/sand environment. ([Old Stadium Journey](https://oldstadiumjourney.com/bahrain-international-circuit-discovering-the-desert-oasis-of-motorsport/))
- **Sparse desert planting overall** — small irrigated green/landscaped strips
  are used near buildings and as anti-sand landscaping, but the dominant ground
  cover is sprayed/managed sand and gravel, not lawn. Keep vegetation sparse and
  clustered (palms near pit/infield), not a continuous green verge. ([51GT3](https://51gt3.com/en/track/bahrain-international-circuit-oasis))
- **No significant water feature.** The "Oasis" label is a circuit/complex name,
  not a lake. Do not add a literal oasis pond. ([RacingCircuits via search](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/bahrain/bahrain-international-circuit.html))

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Night race since 2014.** The circuit is lit to daylight equivalent by a
  floodlight system of **~495 light posts**, **10–45 m tall**, **~4,500
  luminaires**, ~500 km of cabling. The colour reads as **cool white** (modern
  sports floodlighting), not warm sodium. ([Wikipedia/Eurosport via search](https://www.eurosport.com/formula-1/bahrain-tests-lighting-for-night-race_sto4026348/story.shtml),
  [The National](https://www.thenationalnews.com/sport/bahrain-circuit-to-test-floodlights-for-night-race-1.475017),
  [Crown Prince office](https://www.crownprince.bh/en/news/72273/))
- **Sakhir Tower LED:** 2021 StandardVision upgrade wrapped the tower in **over
  8 million custom LEDs**, a **360° video/illumination** display; LED bands are
  integrated into **handrails and sun louvers on the top seven levels**, and the
  **sail canopy + ground sun-shade** are lit to match the screen's colour and
  tempo. At night the tower is a tall, vivid, colour-cycling beacon (reds, blues,
  golds — often Bahrain red/white). ([AV Magazine / StandardVision](https://www.avinteractive.com/news/displays/standardvision-enveloped-bahrain-international-circuit-with-360-degrees-of-over-8-million-custom-leds-22-04-2021/),
  [bahraingp blog](https://www.bahraingp.com/blog/news/f1-fans-first-to-witness-bahrains-iconic-bic-tower-in-a-whole-new-light/))
- **Dusk → night transition.** Sessions start at desert dusk (orange horizon over
  sand) and run into full dark — the "horizon painted with hues of orange as the
  sun sets behind sand dunes." Floodlit track + dark sky + glowing tower + lit
  palms is the signature look. ([Old Stadium Journey](https://oldstadiumjourney.com/bahrain-international-circuit-discovering-the-desert-oasis-of-motorsport/))
- **National identity / signage:** **Bahrain flag = red + white** with a
  distinctive **five-point white serration** dividing white (hoist) from red
  (fly). Trackside uses **Arabesque geometric patterns** in red/white. Title
  sponsor is **Gulf Air** ("Gulf Air Bahrain Grand Prix") — Gulf Air's golden
  falcon mark on a deep navy/gold palette. ([Flag of Bahrain – Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flag_of_Bahrain),
  [Roadgrip](https://roadgrip.co.uk/projects/track-design-bahrain-f1-grand-prix-circuit/),
  [Gulf Air – Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_Air))

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current game builds: *Bahrain (desert, night): Sakhir Tower (62 m tapered
pale-cream + LED bands + beacon); Manama skyline backdrop; desert dune rings
(`mountain()`); floodlit grandstands + cream hospitality.*

1. **Sakhir Tower height too tall.** Game uses 62 m; real tower is ~9 storeys
   (~40 m class). Lower it to ~40 m and keep the conical/cylindrical taper. **The
   LED bands and beacon are accurate and well-judged** — keep them; ideally bias
   the LED colour to vibrant multi-colour cycling (not just a single band).
2. **Add the sail canopy.** The tower's defining silhouette is the **white sail/
   tent canopy on top** (+ ground-level sun shade) — currently missing. This is
   the most recognisable shape cue.
3. **Manama skyline backdrop is wrong.** The real circuit has **no city skyline**
   — it's isolated desert with Manama ~30 km away and not visible. Replace the
   Manama skyline with empty desert horizon + a **single low pale limestone ridge
   (Jebel Dukhan, ~134 m)** far east. Removing the skyline increases realism.
4. **Dune rings overdone / wrong relief.** Surroundings are **flat** rolling sand,
   not a ring of tall dunes/mountains. Reduce `mountain()` dune ring height/
   frequency to low gentle swells; if any distinct relief, make it one subtle pale
   ridge, not a mountain ring.
5. **Sand colour:** ensure **pale-to-golden limestone tan**, not reddish — match
   the high-contrast pale-sand-vs-dark-asphalt look.
6. **Tent-roof architecture under-represented.** Pit building + grandstands should
   read as **white tensile/tent canopies** over sand-stone bases — the Tilke
   Arabic-tent signature — rather than generic cream blocks.
7. **Palms are a key missing accent.** Add **floodlit date palms** lining the
   track (FURN already supports `palm`) — they're the signature vegetation and
   glow at night.
8. **Floodlight colour** should be **cool LED white** (already the street/modern
   default in `floodColor()`), not desert warm sodium — Bahrain's modern system
   is cool white. Confirm Bahrain isn't mapped to the desert warm-sodium tint.
9. **Branding:** lean on **red/white Bahrain national colours + Arabesque
   geometric patterns** on barriers and sponsor boards (Gulf Air gold/navy as a
   secondary accent).

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

- **Tower:** drop height to ~40 m; keep taper + 360° LED bands + beacon; **add a
  white sail/tent canopy cap** and a ground-level sun-shade ring; bias LED to a
  slow multi-colour cycle (red/white/gold/blue), brightest at night.
- **Backdrop:** remove Manama skyline; render **flat desert horizon**; place a
  **single low pale ridge** (~0.05–0.1 of dune-ring height) far on the east side
  as Jebel Dukhan. Cut dune-ring `mountain()` count/height sharply.
- **Ground:** pale golden-tan sand verge (`buildRoad` sand tint), pale-grey
  **asphalt run-off aprons** wide at corners, **pale-sand gravel traps** at the
  heavy braking zones (T1, T4, T10-ish). Asphalt itself dark/abrasive grey-black.
- **Buildings:** sand-stone bases with **white tensile tent roofs** on pit
  building + grandstands; optionally a couple of **white geodesic hospitality
  domes** in the infield (paddock area).
- **Vegetation:** clustered **date palms** (FURN `palm`) along pit straight and
  infield, floodlit/HDR-glowing at night; keep the rest sparse — no continuous
  green verge, no water.
- **Barriers/signage:** red/white armco livery with **Arabesque geometric
  pattern** accents; tyre stacks; Gulf-Air-style gold/navy boards as secondary.
  Tall debris fencing.
- **Lighting (night):** cool LED-white floodlights (10–45 m masts), dark sky,
  warm dusk-orange band low on the west horizon for the start-of-session dusk
  feel; glowing tower + lit palms as the focal HDR sources.
