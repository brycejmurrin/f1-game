# Hungaroring — visual reference

Visual research for procedural per-track scenery in Apex 26. Track: **Hungaroring**,
near Mogyoród (north-east of Budapest), Hungary. Hot, dry mid-summer day race.

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Natural valley / amphitheatre / bowl.** The circuit is built into a natural
  valley in the Gödöllő Hills, surrounded by ~50 hectares of rolling hillside, so
  roughly **80% of the track is visible from almost any point** — a genuine natural
  amphitheatre. The site was chosen precisely because it sits in a natural bowl
  alongside the M3 motorway, giving spectators views of most of the facility.
  Sources: [RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/hungary/hungaroring.html),
  [Celtic Horizon Tours 2026 guide](https://celtichorizontours.com/is-the-hungaroring-good-for-spectators-best-grandstands-for-first-time-f1-visitors/),
  [EnterF1 where-to-sit](https://www.enterf1.com/races/where-to-sit.php?guide=where-to-sit-at-the-hungaroring).

- **Dusty, sandy soil — and that is the track's defining surface character.** "The
  circuit is normally dusty due to underuse throughout the rest of the year and its
  **sandy soil**." It is built on sandy ground, which makes it dusty and slippery in
  early practice. Source: [Wikipedia via WebSearch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungaroring),
  [pitpass.com](https://www.pitpass.com/circuits/6/budapest).

- **Hot, dry continental summer.** Race is held in late July / early August, "usually
  extremely hot and dry in this region." Air ~25–33 °C, track surface 36–50 °C; one of
  the hottest, most physical races of the year. The "natural" grass banks can turn
  dusty or muddy depending on weather. Sources:
  [Wikipedia/WebSearch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungaroring),
  [Hungarian Grand Prix Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix).

- **Setting in the Gödöllő Hills.** Low, walkable hills with **oak woods, sandy
  meadows, and spring wildflowers** — deer and woodpeckers in the area. This is the key
  vegetation reference: scattered oak woodland and dry/sandy meadow grass, NOT dense
  lush forest. Sources:
  [Panadea Mogyoród guide](https://www.panadea.com/en/travel-guide-guidebook/europe/hungary/budapest-and-surrounding/godollo-hills/mogyorod),
  [Mogyoród Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mogyor%C3%B3d).

- **Elevation.** ~34 m (114 ft) drop from highest to lowest point; uneven, hilly,
  off-camber terrain with blind crests. Source:
  [WebSearch/Formula Timer](https://formula-timer.com/circuit/hungaroring).

- **The "spring/stream" history (relevant to any water feature).** During 1985–86
  construction an underground **spring** forced a temporary chicane after Turn 3. In
  **1989 the stream was culverted (diverted underground)** and the chicane removed.
  So the only natural water was a spring that is now buried — there is no visible
  infield lake or pond. Source:
  [RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/hungary/hungaroring.html),
  [everythingf1 history](https://www.everythingf1.com/history-of-the-hungaroring/).

- **The only real water nearby is OUTSIDE the circuit.** The **Aquaréna** waterpark
  (Hungary's largest, ~11.5 ha) sits *next to* — not inside — the Hungaroring. It is
  not part of the track infield. Source:
  [Aquaréna official](https://www.aquarena.hu/en/),
  [Hello Hungary](https://hellohungary.hu/en/active/greater-budapest/attraction/bath-beach/family/mogyorodi-aquarena-vizi-szorakoztatopark).

## Terrain & vegetation

- **Terrain is dry, dusty and sandy, not lush.** Sandy soil, dusty surface, grass
  banks that bake in the summer heat and can go dusty/muddy. The defining palette is
  **dry yellow-green / straw-coloured grass with dusty bare earth patches**, low
  saturation, sun-bleached — a continental summer look, not a wet green.
  ([RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/hungary/hungaroring.html),
  [pitpass](https://www.pitpass.com/circuits/6/budapest))

- **Rolling hills, ~34 m elevation.** Natural contours form the amphitheatre; grassy
  hillsides are steep in places and uneven.
  ([Celtic Horizon Tours](https://celtichorizontours.com/is-the-hungaroring-good-for-spectators-best-grandstands-for-first-time-f1-visitors/))

- **Vegetation is SPARSE and dry, oak-woodland character.** The surrounding Gödöllő
  Hills are "low, walkable hills with **oak woods, sandy meadows, and spring
  wildflowers**." So: scattered broadleaf (oak) trees and small **wooded patches /
  treelines** on the hill crests and perimeter, with large open dry-grass/sandy meadow
  in between. NOT a dense continuous forest, and NOT manicured lush parkland.
  Tree foliage should read dry-summer (muted/dusty green, some yellowing), not
  vivid spring green. ([Panadea](https://www.panadea.com/en/travel-guide-guidebook/europe/hungary/budapest-and-surrounding/godollo-hills/mogyorod))

- **Infield is grass, kart track and dusty banks — no lake/pond.** The natural spring
  was culverted underground in 1989; the infield holds the venue's own kart circuit
  plus open grass/dirt. Only nearby water is the external Aquaréna.
  ([RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/hungary/hungaroring.html),
  [Aquaréna](https://www.aquarena.hu/en/))

## Grandstands, buildings, kerbs, surface, banking

- **Grandstands.** Around the bowl, mostly open (uncovered) seating plus the open
  general-admission grass banks. The big exception is the **main grandstand**: the only
  fully covered one, rebuilt for 2025 with a **1,125-tonne steel roof**, ~**10,300
  covered seats**, roof shaped "like a rear wing" with a perforated sound-absorbing
  underside. Older stands historically named in a Gold/Silver/Bronze hierarchy, renamed
  in 2025 (Chicane 1/2, Apex, Grand Prix, Fan, Pit Exit, Grid, Turn 1, Hungaroring).
  Sources: [Motorsport Tickets grandstand guide](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/hungarian-grand-prix-grandstand-guide-to-the-formula-1-race-at-the-hungaroring/),
  [hungaroring.hu rename](https://hungaroring.hu/site/en/races/2025-formula-1-magyar-nagydij/new-grandstand-names),
  [Kym Illman upgrade](https://www.kymillman.com/blog/inside-the-hungarian-gps-200-million-upgrade-a-new-era-for-the-hungaroring/).

- **Pit / paddock building.** New main building **>340 m long**, modern faceted
  "aerodynamic" white/grey envelope; 36 racing + 6 technical garages; inaugurated
  June 2025, full handover April 2026. Historically the *smallest* paddock in F1 before
  the rebuild. Sources:
  [International Architecture & Design Awards](https://ad-c.org/winner/hungaroring-gold-winner-mixed-use-development-design-iada-2026/),
  [PlanetF1](https://www.planetf1.com/news/huge-new-pit-building-major-upgrade-hungaroring),
  [F1Technical](https://www.f1technical.net/news/24686).

- **Kerbs & surface.** Standard **red-and-white** kerbing on most corners; the
  **finish straight was resurfaced** in the 2024–25 redevelopment. Notable: **T6 and
  T7 kerbs painted green & yellow** for the 2024 Senna tribute. Surface is grippy
  asphalt but gets a dusty film off the racing line.
  Sources: [RaceFans Senna kerbs](https://www.racefans.net/2024/07/18/senna-kerbs-and-more-bad-luck-for-mclaren-first-hungarian-gp-pictures/),
  [F1.com phase one](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/phase-one-of-hungaroring-redevelopment-complete.1DwwsrWta6cEbScnqxHuDD).

- **Banking.** No significant banking — the elevation comes from the natural valley
  contours (uphill/downhill, off-camber, blind crests), not banked corners.
  ([Formula Timer](https://formula-timer.com/circuit/hungaroring))

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Time of day:** afternoon **day race**, mid-summer (late July / early Aug). Bright,
  hot, **hazy** light — high sun but with a dusty, sun-bleached haze rather than crisp
  air. Expect heat shimmer, washed warm-amber tones near the ground.
  ([Hungarian GP Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Grand_Prix))

- **Light character:** European/continental summer — warm, fairly high sun; long
  shadows are only at the start/end of the day, so a moderately high but raking sun
  with warm colour and a low dusty haze fits the "day" atmosphere bias.

- **National identity:** Hungarian tricolour is **red–white–green** (horizontal bands).
  Trackside branding, banner flags and crowd flags carry this; the green/yellow Senna
  kerbs are Brazilian, not Hungarian — keep that distinction. The venue branding is
  "Hungaroring" / "Magyar Nagydíj." ([Hungaroring official](https://hungaroring.hu/site/en/)).

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current build: theme `"green"`; amphitheatre bowl of **GREEN mounds**; **dense wooded
treelines**; 11 grandstands; **infield pond**; Hungarian tricolour billboards.

1. **Too green / too lush — the single biggest gap.** Real Hungaroring is **dry,
   dusty, sandy** in mid-summer: yellow-green/straw grass with bare dusty patches, not
   vivid green mounds. The `"green"` theme and saturated green hillsides read like a wet
   spring meadow. Should be desaturated, sun-bleached, dusty.

2. **Too many dense trees.** Real vegetation is **sparse oak woodland + open sandy
   meadow** — scattered trees and small wooded *patches/treelines on crests*, with large
   open grass banks (where spectators actually sit). Current dense continuous treelines
   are wrong; they also wall off the open-bank amphitheatre look.

3. **Infield pond is NOT real — remove it.** The natural spring was **culverted
   underground in 1989**; there is no visible lake/pond inside the circuit. The only
   water (Aquaréna) is a separate waterpark *outside* the venue. A blue infield pond is
   a factual error.

4. **Amphitheatre bowl is correct in concept** but should be open grass terraces with
   crowds, not green forested mounds — the whole selling point is open visibility across
   ~80% of the track.

5. **Grandstand count/look:** 11 stands is roughly plausible, but most are **open
   (uncovered)**; the **main grandstand should stand out as the one big covered stand**
   with a distinctive rear-wing-shaped roof, beside a long modern white pit building.

6. **Tricolour billboards** are appropriate (red-white-green) — keep, but don't confuse
   with the green/yellow Senna kerbs.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Recolour the terrain to dry-summer Pannonian.** Shift grass/verge from saturated
   green to **straw / dry yellow-green** with dusty, low-saturation tone and bare-earth
   bald patches. Warm, sun-bleached. Add subtle dust/haze at the ground. This is the
   highest-impact change.

2. **Switch the theme bias away from lush `"green"`** toward a dry-continental palette
   (think dry savanna/meadow), keeping warm floodlight masts if night is ever used but
   default to a hot hazy day.

3. **Thin the trees dramatically and dry them out.** Replace dense continuous treelines
   with **sparse, clustered broadleaf (oak/`broad`) stands** on hill crests and the
   perimeter, plus occasional lone trees — leave the spectator-facing banks open. Use
   muted/dusty-green and some yellowing foliage (dry summer), not vivid green. Keep
   forest only as distant backdrop on the outer hills.

4. **Remove the infield pond.** Replace with open dry grass + dusty banks (and optionally
   the venue's kart-track lines) — no water inside the circuit. If a water nod is wanted,
   place a small bright-blue *external* waterpark splash far outside the track edge as a
   distant prop, never in the infield.

5. **Keep the amphitheatre bowl but make it open grass terraces.** Model the hillsides
   as broad open dry-grass slopes with crowd/spectator dressing on them (general-admission
   banks), especially around the mid-to-late lap and the T4–T5 hill — not forested mounds.

6. **Distinguish the main grandstand + pit building.** Make ONE large **covered** main
   grandstand (pale steel/white, sweeping rear-wing-like roof) along the start/finish,
   backed by a **long, low, modern white pit/paddock building**, with the other stands
   smaller and open. This matches the post-2025 rebuilt look.

7. **Kerbs:** standard **red-and-white**, and optionally a **green-and-yellow** accent
   pair at two corners (Senna-kerb nod). Surface: grippy dark asphalt with a lighter,
   dusty off-line film.

8. **Signage:** Hungarian **red-white-green** tricolour banners/flags trackside and in
   the crowd; "Hungaroring" branding. Bright, hazy daytime lighting with a warm,
   slightly washed look.
