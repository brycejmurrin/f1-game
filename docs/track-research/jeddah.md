# Jeddah (Corniche) — visual reference

Research date: 2026-06-26. Subject: the **Jeddah Corniche Circuit** (Saudi Arabian
Grand Prix), a fast night-run street circuit on the Red Sea coast, ~12 km north of
central Jeddah, Saudi Arabia.

> Headline takeaway for scenery: **Jeddah's trackside surroundings are genuinely
> SPARSE.** The circuit hugs a narrow strip of reclaimed coastal land and a lagoon.
> There is *no* dense Vegas/Singapore-style skyline wall pressing on the track. The
> dominant visual cues are: the **black Red Sea at night**, **floodlight glare**,
> **continuous wall-lined barriers**, a **palm-lined promenade**, low-to-mid-rise
> waterfront blocks set back behind the wall, and a few signature landmarks
> (King Fahd's Fountain, the white Floating Mosque) that read as **distant
> backdrop**, not trackside furniture.

---

## Real-world surroundings (researched facts, with sources/URLs cited inline)

- **Location & form.** The circuit is in the Jeddah Corniche / Jeddah Waterfront area,
  ~12 km north of the city centre. It "skirts the Red Sea and encircles a lagoon,"
  hugging "a narrow strip of reclaimed land along the Red Sea." 6.174 km, 27 corners
  (most of any F1 track), clockwise, designed by Tilke with F1's motorsport group
  (Ross Brawn). Second-longest circuit on the calendar; **fastest street circuit in
  F1** — 2025 pole average 254.6 km/h, long straights over 300 km/h. (RacingCircuits.info:
  https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/saudi-arabia/jeddah-corniche-circuit.html ;
  Formula 1: https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/exclusive-the-inside-story-on-how-the-worlds-fastest-street-track-was.6YxLwaGrdFVsvLkgfyNZoi ;
  Grokipedia: https://grokipedia.com/page/Jeddah_Corniche_Circuit )

- **The Corniche / Waterfront context.** The "Jeddah Corniche" (Jeddah Waterfront) is a
  **30 km coastal resort strip** along the Red Sea: a coastal road, recreation areas,
  pavilions, **large-scale civic sculptures**, palm-lined promenades, piers/jetties,
  beaches, mosques, and King Fahd's Fountain. The renovated central promenade runs
  ~4.2 km; a broad palm-fringed promenade stretches ~11 km ending at City Park.
  (Jeddah Corniche overview via search of en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeddah_Corniche ;
  Visit Saudi: https://www.visitsaudi.com/en/jeddah/attractions/jeddah-corniche ;
  CuddlyNest: https://www.cuddlynest.com/blog/explore-new-jeddah-corniche-on-the-red-sea/ )

- **King Fahd's Fountain — a WATER JET, not a building.** World's tallest fountain
  (Guinness). Central jet reaches **312 m (1,024 ft)**; water leaves the nozzle at up
  to **375 km/h (233 mph)**, airborne mass can exceed **16 tonnes**, uses **Red Sea
  saltwater**. Built 1980–1983, operational 1985. The **base is shaped like a giant
  mabkhara** (traditional Arabian incense burner), reinforced concrete, corrosion-
  resistant stainless nozzles. **Over 500 LED spotlights** light the jet at night
  ("until midnight"). **Visible from ~30 km away / across much of Jeddah.**
  (Search of en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Fahd's_Fountain ;
  House of Saud: https://houseofsaud.com/king-fahd-fountain-jeddah/ ;
  Grokipedia: https://grokipedia.com/page/King_Fahd's_Fountain )

- **Fountain is OFFSHORE and DISTANT from the circuit.** It sits **offshore in the
  Red Sea** — sources give **~800 m to ~1 km offshore** — **midway along the Corniche
  in the Al Hamra district**, which is *south* of the circuit area. So from the track
  the fountain is a **far-field backdrop pillar of glowing water**, not trackside.
  (Grokipedia: https://grokipedia.com/page/King_Fahd's_Fountain ;
  airial.travel: https://airial.travel/attractions/saudi-arabia/jeddah/king-fahd-fountain-jeddah-bh6WzXhU )

- **Al-Rahma "Floating" Mosque.** White mosque built over the water on stilts at the
  edge of the Corniche road; appears to "float / hover over the Red Sea" at high tide.
  **Turquoise main dome + white minaret**, **52 outer domes** plus main dome, **23
  external umbrellas**, 56 windows, Islamic style. Built 1985. The circuit's lagoon /
  high-speed sweeps wind **near the Floating Mosque**. (Welcome Saudi:
  https://welcomesaudi.com/activity/al-rahma-floating-mosque-jeddah ;
  KSA Visa: https://ksavisa.com/floating-mosque/ ;
  Mosqpedia: https://mosqpedia.org/masajid/al-rahmah-mosque/ )

- **Civic sculptures.** The Corniche is famous as an open-air sculpture museum (20+
  restored large-scale works). Named landmarks near the waterfront include the
  **Nawras** sculpture and section, plus the **Tawheed Mosque**. (Visit Saudi /
  Wikipedia Jeddah Corniche; Facebook/VisitSaudi:
  https://www.facebook.com/VisitSaudi/posts/... open-air sculpture museum post )

- **Skyline is modest near the track.** Jeddah's true megatall, **Jeddah Tower**
  (Jeddah Economic City), is **NOT near the circuit** — it's far north of the city,
  unrelated to the Corniche. The waterfront near the track is low-/mid-rise: hotels,
  resort blocks, the Jeddah Corniche Towers project (mixed-use), set back behind the
  promenade. (Jeddah Tower via search of en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeddah_Tower ;
  Jeddah skyline list: https://wanderlog.com/list/geoCategory/113145/ )

## Skyline & architecture

- **Low density.** Unlike Vegas (Strip canyon) or Singapore (Marina Bay wall of towers),
  Jeddah trackside is **open and sparse**. Much is **reclaimed coastal land, lagoon,
  marina and promenade** — water and flat ground dominate the inland-vs-sea framing.
- **Palette: sandy / arid coastal.** Beige and cream stone, sun-bleached concrete,
  warm desert tones, with the **deep blue-black Red Sea** on the seaward side.
  Landscaping uses **date palms and drought-tolerant planting** (the climate is arid).
- **Mid-rise set back.** Hotels and resort blocks (e.g. InterContinental / Al Hamra
  area dining overlooks the fountain) sit behind the promenade — present but **not
  pressing on the wall**. The genuine supertall (Jeddah Tower) is elsewhere and should
  NOT be modelled near the track.
- **Signature silhouettes** that *do* belong: the **fountain's water column** on the
  southern horizon, the **white-and-turquoise Floating Mosque** by the lagoon, and a
  scattering of mosque minarets/domes typical of the Jeddah waterfront.
- Sources: RacingCircuits.info; Grokipedia (circuit + corniche); Visit Saudi;
  Jeddah skyline list (wanderlog).

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Wall-lined, high-speed, low run-off.** Jeddah is defined by **continuous barriers
  hugging the track** with limited run-off — a classic narrow street-circuit feel. Mix
  of **concrete walls + Armco + TecPro** energy-absorbing barriers, plus **SAFER
  barriers** (it has among the most SAFER barriers of any F1 track due to its narrow,
  fast sweeps). Safety revisions over the years moved barriers back at T8, T10, T14, T20
  for sightlines and added TecPro (e.g. T11 after Schumacher's ~270 km/h 2022 crash).
  (RacingCircuits.info; RaceFans: https://www.racefans.net/2023/03/16/new-tecpro-barrier-added-at-scene-of-mick-schumachers-270kph-crash/ ;
  RacingNews365: https://racingnews365.com/five-areas-where-f1-circuits-have-super-charged-safety )
- **Grandstands & pit building.** Purpose-built: garages, paddock and **seven
  grandstands** were built for the circuit; the **Main Grandstand lines the pit
  straight**. Construction used 37,000 t asphalt, 600,000 t cement, 30,000 m² brick,
  1,400 t glass. Modern pit/paddock complex on the main straight.
  (RacingCircuits.info; grandstand guide: https://viptablestulum.com/saudi-arabian-f1-gp-jeddah-corniche-circuit/ )
- **Surface & kerbs.** **Resurfaced, smooth, fast asphalt.** Over **20,000 m² of
  FIA-approved anti-slip paint** on kerbs and run-off. Kerbs keep the original profile
  but the **back edge is flush** with the run-off (no drop). Red/white kerb striping is
  standard F1; many sweeps are flat-out with painted kerb edges.
  (Grokipedia circuit page; RacingCircuits.info)
- **Cabling / scale.** ~**20,000 m of electric cabling** feeds the floodlighting at
  **1,500 lux**. (Formula 1 "by numbers":
  https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/the-saudi-arabian-grand-prix-by-numbers-as-the-worlds-fastest-street-circuit.4ShMwqxs71iPo7DUmm1dux )

## Vegetation & water

- **Palm promenade.** The Corniche is fringed with **date palms** and palm-lined
  walkways for ~11 km. Roadside planting is **palms + drought-tolerant shrubs** in
  green pockets; the climate is arid so vegetation is **sparse and curated**, not lush
  jungle. (Grokipedia Jeddah Corniche; Visit Saudi; CuddlyNest)
- **The Red Sea & lagoon.** The seaward side is **open water** — the Red Sea on the
  outside, an enclosed **lagoon** in the middle of the lap (the track encircles it).
  Beaches, jetties/piers and a **crescent-shaped jetty island** dot the promenade.
  Water is the single biggest scenery element — dark and reflective at night, with
  floodlight, marina and fountain reflections on it.
- No significant forest/woodland; verges are dry/sandy.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Full night race under floodlights.** One of F1's full-night races (with Singapore,
  Bahrain, Sakhir, Qatar). **~1,500 lux** floodlighting all around the circuit;
  temperatures cooler at night. Signature look: **bright track surface under floodlight
  glare, dark surroundings, sparks off the cars, shadows dancing across the barriers**,
  the **Red Sea coastline as a dark backdrop**. (Wikipedia Saudi Arabian Grand Prix via
  search; Formula 1 "by numbers"; House of Saud:
  https://houseofsaud.com/jeddah-f1-grand-prix/ )
- **Lighting colour.** Stadium-style floodlights read as **cool/neutral LED white**
  (FIA broadcast lighting). The **fountain at night = multi-colour LED spray** (500+
  spotlights). Marina/waterfront lights add warm points reflected on the water.
- **National identity / signage.** **Saudi green + white** dominate (flag colours).
  Heavy **Aramco** presence (title-level F1 partner and Saudi state oil company) plus
  STC, Saudia and other Saudi brands on barrier/sponsor boards. Expect green-and-white
  sponsor liveries and Saudi flags around grandstands. (Saudi Arabian Grand Prix search
  results; general F1 sponsorship.)

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific & concrete)

Current game (street_night) builds: King Fahd's Fountain as a **255 m offshore LED
cylinder + cone**; a Corniche skyline of **3 landmarks 115–172 m**; a yacht marina;
seafront water planes; palm promenade.

1. **Fountain is rendered as a solid glowing cylinder + cone — but it is a WATER JET.**
   The real thing is a **slender, feathering column of white/silver spray** (312 m),
   not an opaque LED tower. A solid cylinder+cone reads as a building/lighthouse. It
   should look like **water**: translucent, tapering/plume top, slight wind drift,
   multi-colour LED uplight from the base, soft spray glow — visually distinct from any
   tower. Height in-game (255 m) is reasonable but **slightly short** vs the real 312 m
   max; the base should be a small **mabkhara (incense-burner) plinth**, not a tower.

2. **Fountain offshore distance & bearing.** Game uses 255 m offshore. Real is
   **~800 m–1 km offshore** and **far to the south** (Al Hamra), so it should sit
   **much farther out and off to one side as a distant horizon element**, not a
   mid-distance near-trackside object. Pulling it closer makes it dominate wrongly.

3. **"3 landmarks at 115–172 m" overstates the skyline.** Jeddah trackside is **sparse
   and low-rise** — there is **no trio of ~170 m towers lining the circuit**. Three
   tall hero towers make it read like Singapore/Vegas, which is wrong. The real signature
   silhouettes are the **fountain water column** and the **low white+turquoise Floating
   Mosque**, plus scattered mid-rise hotels set well back. Recommend **fewer, shorter,
   more set-back** buildings + the mosque.

4. **Missing the Floating Mosque.** The white, turquoise-domed, stilted **Al-Rahma
   Mosque by the lagoon** is arguably Jeddah's *most* track-relevant landmark (the lap
   encircles a lagoon near it) and isn't in the current build. High-value, low-cost add.

5. **Barrier livery / wall continuity.** Jeddah's identity is **continuous walls all the
   way round with little run-off**. Ensure the barrier feels **unbroken and close**, in
   **Saudi green/white + Aramco** sponsor cycling, with **TecPro/SAFER** texture at the
   fast corners — more than generic street armco.

6. **Vegetation should be sparser/aridity-correct.** Palms yes, but **drought-tolerant,
   curated, beige/sandy verges** — not a green lush promenade. Keep palms as the hero
   tree; avoid broadleaf/forest stands here.

7. **Colour grade / tone.** Surroundings should read **warm sandy beige stone + dark
   reflective Red Sea + cool LED floodlight glare**, with the multi-colour fountain as
   the one chromatic accent on the southern horizon. Confirm night palette isn't washing
   to a generic neon-city look.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Re-author the fountain as a water jet, not a tower.** Replace the opaque
   cylinder+cone with a **translucent, additive, tapering spray column** (white →
   silver-blue), ~**280–312 m** tall, **feathered/plumed top**, gentle wind sway, and a
   **base ring of 500+ multi-colour LED uplights**. Sit it on a **small mabkhara plinth**
   (squat domed/fluted base, <~15 m). Give it a soft bloom/halo at night.

2. **Move the fountain far offshore and to one side.** Place it **~800–1000 m out** and
   **offset south** so it reads as a **distant horizon landmark** seen across the dark
   Red Sea, reflected on the water — not a near-track object.

3. **Cut the tall-tower trio; add the Floating Mosque.** Reduce the "3 landmarks 115–172 m"
   to **a couple of shorter (~60–100 m), set-back** mid-rise blocks, and **add the
   Al-Rahma Floating Mosque** near the lagoon: **white body, turquoise central dome,
   white minaret, ringed by small white domes/umbrella canopies**, on stilts over the
   water so it "floats." Make it a clear hero silhouette by the lagoon.

4. **Emphasise sparseness + sea.** Lean into **open water (Red Sea + central lagoon)**
   and flat reclaimed land. Keep buildings **low, beige/sandy, set back behind the
   wall**. Avoid a continuous building canyon.

5. **Continuous close walls + Saudi/Aramco livery.** Make the barrier read **unbroken
   and tight** (low run-off), cycling **Saudi green/white + Aramco** sponsor panels;
   add **TecPro/SAFER** texturing at fast sweeps; tall **floodlight masts all the way
   round** (cool LED white pools, ~1,500 lux feel).

6. **Palm promenade, arid verges.** Keep palms as the hero roadside tree; make verges
   **sandy/sun-bleached and sparse** (drought planting), not lush green. Scatter a few
   **abstract civic sculptures** (Nawras-style) on the promenade for waterfront flavour.

7. **Night atmosphere.** Bright floodlit asphalt, dark surroundings, **dark reflective
   Red Sea**, warm marina point-lights on the water, and the **multi-colour fountain
   glow** as the single chromatic accent on the southern horizon. Sparks/shadows on the
   walls are part of the look.
