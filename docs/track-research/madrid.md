# Madrid (Madring) — visual reference

Visual research for the procedural 3D scenery of the Madrid Grand Prix circuit
("Madring"), debuting on the F1 calendar in September 2026. The headline finding:
**the real circuit is at the IFEMA exhibition centre in the Barajas district of
*northeast* Madrid — NOT downtown.** It is a flat, semi-industrial, airport-corridor
setting next to the Valdebebas development and Real Madrid's training ground. The
famous Cuatro Torres skyscraper cluster is ~10 km away in central Madrid and is at
most a small, distant feature on the *southwest* horizon — it is emphatically NOT a
dominant downtown backdrop wrapping the track, which is what the game currently
builds. See the gap analysis section.

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Location: IFEMA exhibition centre, Barajas district, northeast Madrid.** "The
  Madring will be located on the campus of the IFEMA exhibition centre, near
  Madrid-Barajas Airport and the Ciudad Real Madrid training complex." It is in the
  Barajas district, ~16 km from the city centre, a 5-minute drive / 8-minute metro
  ride from Adolfo Suárez Madrid–Barajas Airport. Source: [Formula 1 — Everything you
  need to know about F1's Madrid circuit](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/everything-you-need-to-know-about-f1s-newest-circuit-in-madrid-including.l6oJZDAE9HVa4Ge8LZOZA),
  [Madring — location & access](https://www.madring.com/en/circuit/location-access),
  [Wikipedia — Madring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madring).
- **Hybrid street + permanent circuit, two distinct zones.** 5.416 km, 22 corners
  (some sources cite an earlier 20-turn / 5.474 km figure). "The 20-turn course is a
  part-street circuit, part-permanent facility, with pit and paddock facilities
  located within the halls of the exhibition centre." Source: [The Race — what we
  learned exploring F1's newest track](https://www.the-race.com/formula-1/what-we-learned-exploring-f1-newest-track/),
  [Wikipedia — Madring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madring).
- **Zone 1 — IFEMA "Recinto Ferial" (fairground):** the start/finish straight,
  pit/paddock inside the exhibition halls, and a tight first section that "winds
  around the buildings of the IFEMA exhibition center with 90-degree turns and few
  run-off areas in a dense urban environment." Source: [Formula One History — Circuito
  IFEMA Madrid](https://www.formulaonehistory.com/circuits/circuito-ifema-madrid/).
- **Zone 2 — Valdebebas expansion area:** a permanent ~2.2 km section in the
  neighbouring Valdebebas district "offering a completely different character — fast,
  fluid, and open," running "alongside the Real Madrid Sport City football training
  ground complex." Two tunnels link the zones under the **M-11 motorway**. Source:
  [Formula One History](https://www.formulaonehistory.com/circuits/circuito-ifema-madrid/),
  [The Race](https://www.the-race.com/formula-1/what-we-learned-exploring-f1-newest-track/).
- **Designed by Studio Dromo** (Yas Marina, Zandvoort and Spa renovations). Source:
  [Wikipedia — Madring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madring).
- **Flat-to-rolling terrain, but more elevation than expected.** Madrid sits at ~650 m
  on the Castilian *meseta* (plateau). The circuit's high point is ~697 m at Turn 7
  (reached via an 8% climb gaining ~10 m), low point ~671 m — i.e. ~26 m of total
  relief, concentrated in the Valdebebas section "notable for its inclines and
  descents." The IFEMA fairground portion itself is essentially flat. Source:
  [Formula One History](https://www.formulaonehistory.com/circuits/circuito-ifema-madrid/),
  [Madrid geography — average altitude 650 m, centre of the Meseta](https://www.madridcity.com/v/geography/).
- **Surrounding land use: airport/industrial corridor + new urban development.**
  Barajas/northeast Madrid is "characterized by industrial and airport-related
  infrastructure typical of Madrid's northeast corridor," next to the large
  Valdebebas residential/park expansion. Source: search synthesis of
  [IFEMA venue info](https://expofp.com/ifema-madrid) and Barajas-airport context.

## Skyline & architecture

- **The defining architecture is the IFEMA fairground: huge, LOW, WIDE white
  pavilions, not towers.** Twelve column-free exhibition halls (≈200,000 m² covered),
  glazed entrance fronts, exposed galvanised-steel roof structure, big flat aprons and
  14,000-space car parks. This is the immediate visual envelope around the pit straight
  and paddock. Source: [Wikipedia — IFEMA Exhibition Centre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IFEMA_Exhibition_Centre),
  [IFEMA — halls/spaces](https://www.ifema.es/en/our-spaces/recinto-ferial).
- **The Valdebebas zone reads as a NEW-BUILD urban edge + open parkland**, not a dense
  old-city canyon: contemporary mid-rise residential blocks of the Valdebebas
  development on one side, the Real Madrid training pitches and the 470-ha forest park
  on the other, with the M-11 motorway crossing it. Open, airy, modern. Source:
  [The Race](https://www.the-race.com/formula-1/what-we-learned-exploring-f1-newest-track/),
  [Valdebebas — wooded park](https://valdebebas.es/en/urbanismo/the-wooded-park).
- **No downtown skyline wraps the circuit.** The dense classic Madrid (Gran Vía,
  Castellana towers) is ~16 km southwest. The only tall man-made cluster anywhere on
  the horizon is the Cuatro Torres, ~10.3 km SW — appropriate only as a faint distant
  silhouette in one direction, if at all. Source: distance/direction synthesis from
  [Cuatro Torres location ~10.3 km from IFEMA] and [Wikipedia — Cuatro Torres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuatro_Torres_Business_Area).

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Grandstands / capacity:** opens at **110,000 seats, expandable to 140,000** within
  five years — one of the largest F1 venues. La Monumental is ringed by packed
  grandstands with two hospitality decks on the inside of the corner, within a ~500,000 m²
  plot that will host "the largest Fan Zone of any F1 track" (the "Pelouse" fan area).
  Source: [Formula 1 — La Monumental](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/why-madrids-la-monumental-corner-lives-up-to-its-name.2tbqkIagDDXxFRR48YH4Ih),
  [Wikipedia — Madring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madring).
- **Pit & paddock:** housed *inside* the IFEMA exhibition halls — a distinctive feature;
  the paddock is indoor hall space rather than a conventional outdoor pit-lane building.
  Source: [The Race](https://www.the-race.com/formula-1/what-we-learned-exploring-f1-newest-track/).
- **Surface / sections:** brand-new asphalt throughout (this is a purpose-laid circuit,
  not repurposed public roads, though styled "street" in the fairground section).
  Turn 12 paving was completed as a publicised milestone. The IFEMA section is tight
  with **few run-off areas and concrete walls** (street-style); the Valdebebas section
  is open and permanent with conventional run-off. Source: [Madring — La Monumental
  paved](https://www.madring.com/en/press-releases/monumental-asfaltada),
  [Formula One History](https://www.formulaonehistory.com/circuits/circuito-ifema-madrid/).
- **Kerbs:** no official colour spec confirmed at time of writing; expect standard FIA
  red/white kerbing, with the usual blue/yellow accents and (likely) Spanish national
  red/yellow trackside identity. (Treat current game kerb colours as plausible default,
  not verified.)

## Vegetation & water

- **Dry continental / Mediterranean planting, sparse.** Madrid's climate is "moderately
  continental … hot, sunny summers," low humidity, ~455 mm rain/yr with "rainfall
  hardly noticeable from June to September," and 40 °C-class July/August heat. The
  natural verge is straw-tan dry grass, olive/holm-oak scrub, aromatic Mediterranean
  shrubs. Source: [Climates to Travel — Madrid](https://www.climatestotravel.com/climate/spain/madrid).
- **Valdebebas Forest Park is the one lush element nearby:** 470 ha, **310,000 trees +
  317,000 shrubs**, recreating five central-Iberian ecosystems — Mediterranean scrub
  with holm oaks and aromatic shrubs, high-mountain Scots pine, Manchego *dehesa*
  (scattered oaks over cereal clearings), Iberian juniper/box woods. It contains an
  **artificial river, two lagoons, a natural spring and a wetland** — the only notable
  water near the circuit. Trees there are young (park opened 2015). Source: [Tourism
  Madrid — Parque Forestal Valdebebas](https://www.esmadrid.com/en/tourist-information/parque-forestal-valdebebas-felipe-vi),
  [Valdebebas — wooded park](https://valdebebas.es/en/urbanismo/the-wooded-park).
- **Street planting:** Madrid boulevards typically use London plane trees (plátanos),
  but the IFEMA fairground itself is mostly hard landscaping (aprons, car parks) with
  thin ornamental planting — not a leafy boulevard.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Race date: September** (Spanish GP debut Sept 2026), temperatures ~25 °C, warm and
  dry, very clear. Source: [Formula 1](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/everything-you-need-to-know-about-f1s-newest-circuit-in-madrid-including.l6oJZDAE9HVa4Ge8LZOZA),
  [Climates to Travel](https://www.climatestotravel.com/climate/spain/madrid).
- **High-altitude clear continental light.** At ~650 m on the meseta, "anticyclonic
  conditions … inhibit cloud formation, resulting in predominantly clear skies"; July
  averages 359 sunshine hours (>11 h/day). Expect a **deep saturated blue zenith, hard
  crisp shadows, low humidity haze, warm sunlight vs cool sky** — exactly the
  clear-circuit "chiaroscuro" the engine's `_trackAtmoBias` clear branch targets.
  Source: [Madrid geography/climate](https://www.madridcity.com/v/geography/),
  [Climate of Madrid](https://www.climatestotravel.com/climate/spain/madrid).
- **National / venue identity:** Spanish red-and-yellow; Madrid red ("Madrid" regional
  red); the Madring brand and Carlos Sainz as circuit ambassador. Aircraft on the
  Barajas approach are a genuine ambient element overhead. Source: [Formula 1 — Madrid
  begins construction, Sainz ambassador](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/madrid-begins-construction-of-f1-venue-as-carlos-sainz-becomes-circuit.QNPOPW4X1ADeqPrT0fzRm).

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current file: `js/tracks/madrid.js` (theme `"modern"`, `banked:true`, `street:true`).
It is already more sophisticated than the brief assumed — it *does* build IFEMA halls,
a La Monumental bowl, dry-Castilian filler, a hazy Sierra ring and airport lights. The
real problems are **emphasis and setting accuracy**:

1. **Downtown skyline is massively over-built (the core issue).** Section (2) "MADRID
   CITY SKYLINE" wraps the *entire* track in two dense rings of mid-rise (36 blocks at
   rad+440) and landmark towers (20 towers up to ~220 m at rad+600), plus section (9)
   adds another 24-building ring at rad+290, plus sections (8) `cityFront`/`backdrop`
   put a continuous urban canyon along most street sectors. This paints a dense
   downtown that **does not exist** at flat Barajas/IFEMA. Real surroundings: low IFEMA
   sheds + open aprons + Valdebebas new-build mid-rise on *one* side + park/training
   pitches + airport flatland. → Drastically thin the encircling high-rise; reserve
   tall buildings for a *single* distant SW bearing.

2. **Cuatro Torres rendered too close and too prominent.** Section (2) places the four
   240–268 m towers at only `rad+760` on bearing `a=0.9`, tall and clearly readable.
   In reality they are ~10.3 km SW — they should be a *faint, small, far* cluster on
   one horizon bearing (SW), if shown at all, not a hero landmark. Also the in-code
   heights (240/268/252/224) are slightly inflated vs real (249/248/236/224.5) and the
   tallest is Torre de Cristal (249), not a 268. → Push them far out, shrink screen
   size, fix heights, keep on a single SW bearing only.

3. **Missing the airport as a visual element.** Only 8 tiny runway-edge lights exist
   (section 14). The airport is a signature neighbour: control tower, low terminals,
   **aircraft on approach/landing overhead**. → Add a distant control tower + low
   terminal masses on the NE/airport bearing; consider an occasional aircraft prop on
   the skyline.

4. **Valdebebas character is absent.** No Real Madrid training pitches, no Valdebebas
   Forest Park mass, no M-11 motorway crossing/tunnels, no open new-build-residential
   edge. The permanent half of the circuit is the most distinctive "open, fluid" part
   and is currently dressed like more downtown. → Add an open green park edge + flat
   football-pitch plane + modern low residential blocks on the Valdebebas-side fraction.

5. **IFEMA halls under-scaled relative to their real dominance.** They exist
   (sections 4) but at h≈15–20 m and modest footprints, visually swamped by the towers
   around them. In reality they are the *biggest* built things by footprint and the
   defining backdrop of the pit straight. → Make them larger/wider and the visual
   anchor of the start/finish zone; reduce competing high-rise so they read.

6. **Sierra ridge is reasonable but omnidirectional.** Section (1) rings the *whole*
   horizon with mountains. The Guadarrama is only to the **N/NW**. → Bias the mountain
   ridge to the N/NW arc and keep it low/hazy; leave the other bearings flat (this is
   the meseta).

7. **Vegetation tuning is good but could lean drier + add the park.** The olive/scrub
   filler and dry palette are on-point for the meseta. Missing: the lush, *young*
   Valdebebas park as a distinct dense-but-low green mass with water (lagoons/river),
   contrasting the dry verges elsewhere.

8. **Atmosphere is well-judged.** The clear-blue/warm-sun palette and `banked` La
   Monumental are accurate. Keep.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Cut the encircling high-rise by ~70%.** Remove or heavily thin the two full rings
   in section (2) and the rad+290 ring in section (9). Keep only a *sparse, low*
   modern-block presence and concentrate it on the Valdebebas-side fraction, not all
   360°. The default view should be **flat horizon + low IFEMA sheds**, not a city
   wall.

2. **Relocate + demote the Cuatro Torres.** Push to ~`rad+2000+` on a single SW bearing
   (`a` chosen so the cluster sits low and far on one side only), shrink apparent size
   accordingly, and correct heights to `[249, 248, 236, 224.5]` (Cristal tallest). Make
   them a tiny distant Easter-egg silhouette, not a backdrop. Optionally gate behind a
   "clear day" only.

3. **Make the IFEMA halls the hero backdrop of the pit straight.** Enlarge the section
   (4) pavilions (wider, longer, low — true exhibition-shed proportions), give them the
   glazed-entrance + exposed-steel-roof look, and make sure they dominate the
   start/finish framing now that the towers are gone.

4. **Add the airport on the NE bearing.** A distant control tower (slim, ~60–90 m),
   low horizontal terminal masses, apron lighting, and — for atmosphere — one or two
   static aircraft props on the far skyline / on approach. Reuse the existing runway-
   edge lights but extend them into a recognisable airfield edge.

5. **Build a Valdebebas zone for the permanent half.** On the appropriate track
   fraction (the open/fast section), add: an open flat green plane with goalposts
   (Real Madrid training pitches), a dense-but-low **young forest-park** mass with a
   couple of small water bodies (lagoon/river reflective patches), and a row of modern
   low-rise residential blocks (Valdebebas new-build) on the opposite side. This is the
   "completely different, open, fluid" character the real track has here.

6. **Add an M-11 motorway crossing / underpass cue** between the two zones (a raised
   road deck or tunnel mouth) to reflect the two-tunnel link under the motorway —
   reinforces the hybrid IFEMA↔Valdebebas split.

7. **Bias the Sierra ridge to N/NW only.** Restrict the section (1) mountain ring to a
   ~90–120° arc on the north/northwest bearing; keep it low, blue-grey and hazy with a
   hint of snow only on the highest line. Leave the rest of the horizon flat meseta.

8. **Lean the verges drier and sparser, contrast with the park.** Keep the straw-tan /
   olive scrub for the general meseta verge, but make the Valdebebas park section
   distinctly greener and denser so the two read as different ecosystems. Plane trees
   only along the urban/boulevard street sectors, not the open zones.

9. **Keep the clear continental palette and La Monumental banking.** These are accurate
   — deep blue zenith, hard shadows, warm low sun, the ~24% banked 270° stadium bowl
   with wrapping grandstands and inside-corner hospitality. If anything, push the giant
   Fan Zone / grandstand mass around T12 even larger (110k–140k capacity, largest fan
   zone on the calendar).
