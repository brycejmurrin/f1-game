# Monza — visual reference

Autodromo Nazionale Monza, Italian GP. Built 1922 inside the Royal Park (Parco di
Monza), the third purpose-built circuit in the world and the oldest in mainland
Europe. Nicknamed the "Temple of Speed." A flat, very densely forested circuit
whose single most distinctive (and currently missing) feature is the ruined
**banked oval (Sopraelevata)** weaving through the parkland trees.

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Set inside a royal park, woodland on all sides.** "Built in the Royal Villa of
  Monza park in a woodland setting, the site has three tracks – the 5.793 km Grand
  Prix track, the 2.405 km Junior track, and a 4.250 km high-speed oval track with
  steep bankings." The Parco Reale is ~800 hectares, the largest fenced/enclosed
  green area in Europe. (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monza_Circuit ,
  https://www.in-lombardia.it/en/visiting-lombardy/natural-parks-in-lombardy/monza-park-and-its-green-giants)
- **The abandoned banked oval still stands among the trees.** "With the steadily
  decaying remnants of the high-speed banked circuit providing a backdrop through
  the parkland trees, the atmosphere here is like no other; a mix of speed,
  melodrama and more than a hint of melancholy." Escaped demolition in the 1990s;
  surface repaved 2014; you can walk or rent a bike on the old oval today.
  (https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/italy/monza.html ,
  https://www.circuitsofthepast.com/monza-oval/)
- **The oval geometry (Sopraelevata / Pista di Alta Velocità).** Built 1954–55. Two
  semicircular banked curves (Curva Nord, Curva Sud), radius ~320 m, joined by two
  straights of ~875 m each; total 4.250 km. Banking is a **concrete structure**
  (the original 1922 banking was an earth embankment; the 1955 version is poured
  concrete). Banking angle ~21° average rising to **38.69° at the top**, peaking at
  an 80% gradient at the steepest point. Used for F1 in 1955, 1956, 1960, 1961.
  (https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/high-risk-high-interest-a-brief-history-of-italian-banking.6L8HuEEFfOmwpMc5Y555VJ ,
  https://www.circuitsofthepast.com/monza-oval/)
- **The oval interlaces the GP track.** The high-speed loop "looped in and around
  much of the existing road circuit." Drivers peeled off near the site of today's
  first chicane to tackle Curva Nord. A modern GP-track straight "crosses, with an
  underpass, the North bank curve of the high-speed loop" — i.e. the GP track runs
  under the ruined banking near the Serraglio. The banking "can be seen from many
  grandstands and pathways."
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monza_Circuit ,
  https://www.monzanet.it/en/circuit/)
- **Trees of the Royal Park.** Monumental beeches (Napoleonic era), old **oaks** and
  **hornbeams**; the "Viale dei Carpini" is an avenue lined with two rows of large
  clipped hornbeam topiary. Exotic specimens too: cedar of Lebanon, ginkgo,
  American sequoia. The park edge is bordered by **tall pines**. Reforestation
  (post-July-2023 storm that felled ~10,000 trees) is planting oak, English oak and
  hornbeam — confirming these as the signature native species.
  (https://www.in-lombardia.it/en/visiting-lombardy/natural-parks-in-lombardy/monza-park-and-its-green-giants ,
  https://www.monzanet.it/en/approved-the-monza-reforestation-plan/)
- **Villa Reale di Monza** — neoclassical royal palace (Giuseppe Piermarini,
  1777–80), U-shaped, linear/essential pale facade with simple decoration, no
  colonnades or gables; 700 rooms. Sits at the south end of the park, near the
  circuit but not trackside. A pale, elegant landmark on the skyline.
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Villa_of_Monza ,
  https://www.monzanet.it/en/royal-villa-of-monza-a-neoclassical-palace-to-be-discovered/)
- **Atmosphere — Ferrari Tifosi, September, leafy.** "Monza lies… in leafy parkland
  not far from Milan. The trees cast fantastic shadows on various sections and the
  old banking… reminds you of the history of the track." Each September the
  grandstands become "a sea of red" — Tifosi flags, red smoke.
  (https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/engines-espresso-and-tifosi-passion-the-ultimate-fan-guide-to-monza.1rkB3TtIgupczEs1naB0C8 ,
  https://www.ferrari.com/en-EN/magazine/articles/magical-monza)
- **2026–27 facility upgrade (forward-looking).** €40M restyling begins after the
  2026 season: permanent roof over the Paddock Club terrace, modernized box/pit
  building, a new glazed single-floor race-control with a suspended section over
  the track, new media centre. Suggests a contemporary grey-concrete + glass pit
  complex idiom.
  (https://racingnews365.com/monza-begins-eur21-million-modernisation-project)

## Vegetation & terrain

- **Terrain is essentially FLAT.** The only meaningful elevation is a gentle rise
  through the Lesmo section; otherwise the parkland is level. No hills, no banked
  modern corners. (https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/italy/monza.html)
- **Forest dominant on both sides, full lap.** This is the defining scenery
  characteristic: the track is a clearing cut through continuous mature woodland.
  Canopy presses close to the verge, especially Lesmo 1 & 2 and the Ascari/Serraglio
  area, producing "tree tunnel" shaded sections.
- **Species (use these, not generic):**
  - Dominant native deciduous: **English/common oak**, **hornbeam** (carpinus —
    Monza's signature, even clipped into topiary avenues), beech, plane, maple/ash.
  - **Pines** along the park perimeter and as scattered tall accents (umbrella/
    stone pine silhouette reads as Italian).
  - Occasional monumental exotics (cedar of Lebanon, sequoia, ginkgo) as rare tall
    landmark trees — useful for breaking the canopy line.
  - Mostly **mid-to-deep summer green** (race is September — late summer, leaves
    still full, not yet autumn). A handful of early-autumn yellow/bronze accents are
    plausible but should be sparse.
- **Ground/verge:** grass verge between asphalt and tree line; clipped park hedges
  in places (the manicured royal-park character). Dappled tree shadow across the
  road is a signature look.

## Grandstands, pit buildings, kerbs, surface

- **Grandstands:** large stepped steel/concrete tiers. The tall rear grandstands
  are **roofed**; the front Gradinate Traguardo are uncovered. Dominant in-crowd
  colour is **Ferrari red** (Tifosi). Structure tones are utilitarian grey.
  (https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/italian-grand-prix-grandstand-guide-for-the-f1-race-at-monza/)
- **Pit / paddock building:** historically functional; being modernized (2026–27)
  to a contemporary idiom — grey concrete + glass, permanent tensile/hard roof over
  the Paddock Club, a glazed suspended race-control box overlooking the track. Light
  grey / off-white with glass strips is the right read.
  (https://racingnews365.com/monza-begins-eur21-million-modernisation-project)
- **Kerbs:** classic **red-and-white** kerbs. Monza historically used tall yellow
  "sausage" kerbs at chicane/Parabolica exits (notorious after the 2019 Peroni
  crash); most have since been removed/lowered. Ascari kerbs famously have a drain
  channel that drops ~10 cm. Use standard red/white flat-and-ramp kerbs; sparse
  yellow sausage accents are period-correct but de-emphasized.
  (https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/monza-sausage-kerb-removed-peroni/4533811/ ,
  https://www.racefans.net/2022/09/09/f1-drivers-pleased-to-see-fewer-sausage-kerbs-at-monza/)
- **Run-off:** asphalt run-off at the chicanes and outside Parabolica (gravel
  converted to asphalt — Rettifilo/Roggia in 2007, Parabolica more recently), with
  **narrow gravel strips** reintroduced at the first/second chicane run-offs, plus a
  synthetic-grass carpet between track and asphalt run-off at Parabolica. So: dark
  asphalt run-off + thin pale gravel strip + green synthetic edge.
  (https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/how-the-controversial-kerb-changes-have-really-altered-monza/10649313/)
- **Surface:** standard dark asphalt, recently fully resurfaced; rubbered-in racing
  line. No banking on the modern GP track — the only banking is the ruined oval.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Day race, warm late-summer Italian afternoon.** September. Warm low-ish sun,
  long dappled tree shadows across the track ("the trees cast fantastic shadows").
  Current palette in `monza.js` (warm sun [1.0,0.88,0.58], hazy horizon) is on the
  right track; keep it warm and slightly hazy, not cold.
- **Signature colours / identity:** Italian **green-white-red**, but the in-context
  dominant is **Ferrari red** — Tifosi flags, red smoke, red caps fill the stands.
  Autodromo's own identity leans red/white. National green/white/red works for
  trackside banners and the start gantry trim.
- **Signage:** sponsor billboards and hoardings line the straights; "AUTODROMO
  NAZIONALE MONZA" branding; Pirelli/F1 boards. A sea of red in the grandstands is
  the defining crowd texture.
  (https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/engines-espresso-and-tifosi-passion-the-ultimate-fan-guide-to-monza.1rkB3TtIgupczEs1naB0C8)

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

What `js/tracks/monza.js` currently builds (good, keep):
- Dense multi-rank **royal-park forest** (Ranks A–D: front pines, broadleaf,
  set-back pines, outer broadleaf) plus extra close canopy at **Lesmo 1 & 2** — this
  correctly captures the woodland-tunnel character.
- Clipped **park hedges** through several sweeps — correct manicured-park detail.
- Grandstands (Tribuna Centrale + facing stand) with red Italian trim band, podium/
  timing tower, modernized pit building with permanent roof canopy, start gantry,
  sponsor billboards, lamp posts. Warm afternoon palette.
- Gentle Lesmo rise via `elevations` — correct (track is otherwise flat).

Gaps / inaccuracies:
1. **The banked oval ruins (Sopraelevata) are entirely missing.** This is Monza's
   single most iconic and unique scenery feature and the brief's top priority.
   Nothing in the file evokes the crumbling concrete banking through the trees.
2. **Tree species lean too pine-heavy.** Ranks A and C are pine-dominant; reality is
   **deciduous-dominant** (oak, hornbeam, beech, plane) with pines as perimeter/
   accent. The umbrella-pine read is fine as an accent but should not be the front
   rank everywhere.
3. **No distant Villa Reale landmark** — a pale neoclassical palace on the skyline
   would anchor the "royal park" identity (optional, lower priority since it's not
   strictly trackside).
4. **Run-off detail is generic.** Real Monza has a specific layered run-off look at
   chicanes/Parabolica: dark asphalt run-off + thin pale gravel strip + green
   synthetic-grass edge. Current build references only generic GRAVEL and Lesmo
   runoff mounds (the brief notes Lesmo runoff mounds exist).
5. **Crowd colour:** stands read as grey with a thin red trim; the real signature is
   a *sea of Ferrari red* filling the tiers. More red mass in the grandstand fill
   would land the Tifosi identity.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Add the banked oval ruins (highest impact).** Emit a curved, ruined concrete
   banking running through the back/outer treeline — partially visible behind the
   forest, not on the racing line. Two candidate placements that match reality:
   (a) a Curva-Nord-style banked arc near Turn 1 / the first chicane, and (b) a
   long straight ruin behind the Parabolica/paddock outer field. Model it as a
   tilted concrete ramp section (use `addPrism`/`addFrustum` for the banked
   wedge profile, ~20–38° tilt), weathered grey ([0.55,0.55,0.52]-ish) with darker
   moss/stain streaks, partly screened by trees growing in front of and through it.
   Even a short 60–120 m segment visible past the trees delivers the "decaying
   banking glimpsed through parkland" signature. Optionally an **underpass** where
   the GP track passes beneath a banking arch near the Serraglio.
2. **Rebalance tree mix to deciduous-dominant.** Make broadleaf (`tree`) the front
   and majority rank; demote `pine` to perimeter Rank C/D and scattered accents.
   Add clipped **hornbeam topiary** balls/avenues as a distinctive Monza detail
   (small dense rounded crowns, already have `hedge`/`bush` primitives to riff on).
3. **Keep late-summer green, add sparse early-autumn accents.** Mostly full summer
   green; a few bronze/yellow trees (5–10%) for September warmth — do not overdo it.
4. **Layer the run-off at chicanes + Parabolica:** dark asphalt apron, a thin pale
   gravel strip, and a green synthetic-grass edge band between track and run-off.
   Add red/white flat kerbs; sparse yellow sausage-kerb accents at chicane exits.
5. **Fill grandstands with Ferrari red.** Increase the red mass in the stand fill
   (crowd/seat tone) on the start/finish straight so it reads as a Tifosi sea, not
   just a red trim band. Keep the rear stands roofed, front Gradinate open.
6. **Optional distant Villa Reale:** a pale, low U-shaped neoclassical block with a
   terracotta roof on the far skyline behind the start/finish, as a backdrop
   landmark reinforcing the royal-park setting.
