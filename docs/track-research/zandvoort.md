# Circuit Zandvoort — visual reference

Research for procedural 3D scenery accuracy. Track: Circuit Zandvoort,
Dutch Grand Prix, Netherlands. Coastal dune circuit on the North Sea, ~35 km
west of Amsterdam. The single most distinctive feature is that the track is
built **inside the coastal sand dunes** — a "rollercoaster in the dunes". The
second is its two **steeply banked corners** (unusual for modern F1).

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Built in the dunes, on the North Sea coast.** Circuit Zandvoort is a
  4.259 km track "located in the dunes north of Zandvoort, Netherlands, near the
  North Sea coast line." It "lies at the centre of a dune-filled landscape" and
  sits "between the dunes and the sea on North Holland province's North Sea
  coast." Nicknamed the **"rollercoaster in the dunes."**
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort ,
  https://www.globalhighways.com/feature/rollercoaster-dunes-circuit-park-zandvoort)
- **The whole site is a coastal dune landscape, ~40 km².** "The area is
  characterised by sand dunes and is almost 40 square kilometres in size."
  (https://visitzandvoort.com/zandvoort-dunes/)
- **Flat coastal terrain — NO mountains.** The Netherlands coast is flat; the
  only relief is the dunes themselves (rolling sand hills, not peaks). Elevation
  on circuit comes from the dune undulation, not distant mountains.
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort)
- **The sea is genuinely visible from the circuit.** From the upper grandstands
  "you can actually catch a glimpse of the Zandvoort beach view — a surreal
  contrast with the screech of Pirelli tyres on one side and the rolling waves
  of the North Sea on the other." The Eastside grandstands "place you right in
  the middle of the dunes."
  (https://www.grandprixgrandtours.com/blog/where-to-sit-at-the-dutch-grand-prix/)
- **Sand blows onto the track.** "The sand from the dunes, which is continuously
  blown up and over the track surface by the coastal wind, presents an additional
  challenge." The bespoke asphalt ("FlyingDutch", Norwegian stone + Shell
  bitumen) was engineered specifically "to meet the characteristics of the
  Zandvoort's sand, the cold temperatures, the strong winter winds."
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort ,
  https://www.circuitsofthepast.com/layout-zandvoort-circuit/)
- **Sand colour is GOLDEN/BLOND, not green.** "The coast is adorned with golden,
  fine sand" and the dune landscape is described as "a shifting world of gold and
  green" — i.e. **bare gold sand interspersed with grey-green grass**, not a
  green forest. Bare "sand hills 'wander' through the landscape" where grass
  doesn't grow.
  (https://visitzandvoort.com/zandvoort-dunes/ ,
  https://www.pietschy-photography.com/blogs/photography/to-build-a-home-amsterdamse-waterleidingduinen-dunes)

## Terrain & vegetation

- **Dunes = bare golden/blond sand + sparse grass, NOT forest.** The dominant
  ground texture is open, rolling sand. "In places where marram grass or other
  crops do not grow, sand hills 'wander' through the landscape." Landscape reads
  as "gold and green" — gold sand with scattered grey-green grass, not a green
  canopy. (https://visitzandvoort.com/zandvoort-dunes/)
- **Marram grass (Ammophila arenaria) is the signature plant.** It "grows to
  approximately one metre tall with dense tussocks of grey-green spiky leaves"
  and is "visible at all times of the year." It binds the sand and grows in
  clumps/tussocks, leaving bare sand between. This is **grey-green / blue-green
  and slightly silvery**, not a vivid forest green.
  (https://www.wildlifetrusts.org/wildlife-explorer/grasses-sedges-and-rushes/marram-grass ,
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammophila_arenaria)
- **Vegetation is a "varied mosaic."** "The nature in the Zandvoort dunes
  consists of a varied mosaic of grasslands, pools, some trees, marram
  grasslands and paths." Key word: **some** trees — sparse, scattered low scrub
  and the occasional stand, NOT continuous dense forest.
  (https://visitzandvoort.com/zandvoort-dunes/)
- **Height & form.** Dunes are low, rolling hills (tens of metres at most), wind-
  sculpted, with smooth windward slopes and patchy vegetation cover. Bare sand
  faces are common where the wind scours them. No tall trees breaking the
  silhouette — the horizon is low, undulating sand against sky/sea.

## Grandstands, buildings, kerbs, surface, banking

- **Banking — the iconic feature.** Turn 3 (Hugenholtz) and Turn 14 (Arie
  Luyendyk) "will have corners banked at around 19 degrees, whereas the
  Indianapolis Motor Speedway has nine-degree turns... Turn 3 (Hugenholtz)
  features a 19-degree angle, whilst Turn 14 (Arie Luyendyk) is slightly less
  steep at 18-degrees." Banking is **double Indy's**. Turn 14 has a **maximum
  gradient of 32%** in the banked section.
  (https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/first-look-images-of-zandvoorts-new-banked-corners-revealed.5BXXjRKQadkAX35ZOIWR2F ,
  https://www.essentiallysports.com/f1-news-dutch-gp-how-zandvoorts-banking-beats-the-iconic-indianapolis-track-that-once-hosted-the-us-gp/)
- **Variable (Fibonacci) banking on Hugenholtz.** "Hugenholtz uses the Fibonacci
  Sequence to progressively increase the angle of the tarmac, with the inside of
  the turn at just 4.5 degrees, but on the outside, it's 19." So the bank is a
  ramped bowl — inside nearly flat, outside lip steep.
  (https://www.essentiallysports.com/f1-news-dutch-gp-how-zandvoorts-banking-beats-the-iconic-indianapolis-track-that-once-hosted-the-us-gp/)
- **Surface.** Bespoke "FlyingDutch" asphalt — Norwegian aggregate + Shell
  bitumen, dark and grippy, engineered for sand/cold/wind. Resurfaced for the
  2021 return. (https://www.circuitsofthepast.com/layout-zandvoort-circuit/)
- **Kerbs.** Standard FIA red/white serrated kerbs; painted asphalt run-off in
  places (notably Tarzan and the banked corners) rather than gravel everywhere.
- **Grandstands.** Main Grandstand is the **only covered/roofed stand**, on the
  pit straight over pit lane and podium. Arena grandstands form the densest,
  loudest block (final corner / pit straight). Eastside stands sit out in the
  dunes on the back half. Steel-frame open stands; crowd reads as a solid wall of
  orange. (https://oversteer48.com/zandvoort-arena-grandstand/ ,
  https://www.grandprixgrandtours.com/dutch-circuit-guide/)
- **Pit building.** Modern low pit/paddock complex along the start/finish
  straight.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Day race, North Sea coastal light.** The Dutch GP is a daytime race. Light is
  **soft North Sea coastal light — frequently hazy/overcast**, with bright blue
  spells; the maritime climate means rapidly changing conditions and sea haze.
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_Zandvoort)
- **ORANGE is the identity.** The crowd is the Dutch "Orange Army." "Zandvoort
  transforms into a sea of orange." "With 99% of the crowd rooting for the home
  hero Max Verstappen, you'll be sat in a sea of orange." The Arena is "a sea of
  orange smoke come race day" (orange flares/smoke bombs). Orange shirts, wigs,
  flags, choreographed flag waves.
  (https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/how-max-verstappen-captivated-and-transformed-f1-fan-culture-in-the.AFysFZC9m6zQY0T0mqUbo ,
  https://oversteer48.com/zandvoort-arena-grandstand/)
- **Signage / national identity.** Dutch orange dominates banners, smoke, fan
  zones; Netherlands flag (red/white/blue) present but the visual signature the
  game should hit is **saturated Verstappen-orange** across the stands.
- **Coastal extras.** Beach atmosphere on the seaward side; pale beach + blue-
  grey sea visible from high stands. (Note: there is no large offshore wind farm
  directly defining the Zandvoort skyline in the way the current game implies —
  the dominant horizon is dunes, beach and sea, see gap analysis.)

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current file: `js/tracks/zandvoort.js`. It is already fairly dune-aware (sandy
palette constants, marram tufts/hedges, golden dune mounds), which is good. But
several elements actively fight the real look:

1. **Theme/grass palette skews green.** `theme: "green"` and `pal.grass:
   [0.42,0.50,0.25]` push the verge/terrain toward a meadow green. The real
   ground is golden sand with grey-green grass *patches*. The base terrain tint
   should read as pale sand, not green grass. **FLAG: dune ground should be
   sandy/beige, not green.**
2. **Dark coastal PINE FOREST is wrong and over-used.** Five `forestEdge()`
   calls plant dense dark conifers (`pineCol [0.16,0.34,0.12]`,
   `pineFrac 0.80–0.88`) along most of the lap. Real Zandvoort dunes are open
   sand with only *some* sparse, low scrub/trees — **not** a continuous dark
   green pine belt. This is the biggest single inaccuracy: it reads as a forest
   circuit (Spielberg/Hockenheim), not a North Sea dune circuit. The pines also
   visually re-introduce the "green" look the dune palette is trying to avoid.
3. **Banking is data-correct but worth verifying it reads visually.** The def
   sets `banked: true`, `pal`-driven engine banks highest-curvature corners, and
   the geometry segs flag the two corners (`b: 0.31`/`0.30`). Confirm the
   rendered bank on Hugenholtz (T3) and Arie Luyendyk (T14) is *visible and
   steep* (target ~18–19°), since this is THE iconic visual. Real T14 reaches
   32% gradient — the in-game bank should look dramatic, not a subtle camber.
4. **Marram grass colour OK but could lean more grey/silver.** `marramG
   [0.33,0.49,0.24]` is fairly saturated green; real marram is grey-green /
   blue-green. Slightly desaturating toward `[0.40,0.50,0.36]` would read more
   like dune grass than lawn.
5. **Wind turbines on the seaward horizon are a questionable landmark.** Five
   80 m turbines are placed at `dist 300` on the seaward side. The defining
   seaward horizon is dunes → beach → North Sea, not a wind farm. This is not a
   strong Zandvoort signature and competes with the real (flat sea + dunes)
   silhouette. Consider removing or heavily reducing.
6. **Beach huts are a minor invention.** Pastel beach-hut rows (s≈0.3–0.7) are a
   plausible coastal touch but not a circuit landmark; low priority, fine to keep
   sparse.
7. **Good things to keep:** sandy mound rings (inner/mid/far), marram tufts +
   hedges, orange grandstands + orange bunting, red/white armco, multicolour
   tyre stacks, pit building, sea/beach horizon bands, sea-glimpse slivers.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Drop the dark pine forest.** Remove or drastically thin the five
   `forestEdge()` pine belts. Replace with: (a) more bare golden dune mounds, and
   (b) occasional **sparse, low scrub clumps** (use `bush`/short scrub, not tall
   `pine`/`tree`). If any trees remain, make them few, low, and muted — scattered
   wind-stunted scrub, never a continuous canopy.
2. **Push the whole palette toward sand.** Re-tint base terrain/verge to pale
   golden sand rather than green grass. Consider `theme` handling so the ground
   plane and verge read beige `~[0.84,0.78,0.60]` with grass only as patches.
   The dominant ground colour the player sees should be SAND.
3. **Make the dune grass grey-green, not lawn-green.** Desaturate `marramG`
   toward a silvery grey-green (e.g. `~[0.42,0.50,0.36]`) and keep `marramT`
   tan; intersperse plenty of *bare sand* gaps between tufts (the dune look is
   patchy, not continuous turf).
4. **Sell the banking.** Verify Hugenholtz (T3) and Arie Luyendyk (T14) render a
   steep, *visible* banked wall (target ~18–19°, T14 up to 32% gradient). If the
   engine bank looks subtle, raise the banking for these two corners so the outer
   lip is clearly elevated — this is the circuit's signature shot. Add a few
   spectators/grandstands looking *down into* the bowl.
5. **Lead with the dune silhouette on the horizon.** Keep dune mounds as the
   primary far backdrop on all sides; keep beach + flat blue-grey sea on the
   seaward arc. **Reconsider/remove the wind turbines** — they aren't a Zandvoort
   signature and clutter the authentic dune+sea horizon.
6. **Lean into orange.** Already good (orange crowds + bunting). Optionally add
   orange smoke/flare accents and a dense, fully-orange Arena block near the
   final corner; ensure the Main Grandstand is the one with a roof/canopy.
7. **Atmosphere preset: hazy coastal day.** Bias the `day` atmosphere toward the
   humid/overcast end (soft, slightly hazy North Sea light), not a deep clear
   saturated sky — Zandvoort is often grey/hazy. Keep low haze near the sea band.
8. **Surface:** keep dark asphalt; ensure run-off at Tarzan and the banked
   corners reads as painted asphalt (not gravel everywhere), with red/white FIA
   kerbs.
