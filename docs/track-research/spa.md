# Spa-Francorchamps — visual reference

Visual research for the procedural scenery of CIRCUIT DE SPA-FRANCORCHAMPS
(Belgian GP) in Apex 26. Goal: make the per-track dressing read unmistakably as
Spa — the dense Ardennes conifer forest, the dramatic Eau Rouge/Raidillon
hillside, the painted Belgian-flag run-offs, and the modern pit/tower complex.

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Setting.** A 7.004 km circuit in Francorchamps, Stavelot, Wallonia, Belgium,
  about 8 km southeast of the town of Spa. It sits in the **Belgian Ardennes at
  ~500 m altitude, surrounded by dense forest that creates its own
  microclimate.** It borders the High Fens / Fagnes uplands.
  ([Wikipedia via search](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_de_Spa-Francorchamps),
  [carreracafe.ca](https://carreracafe.ca/en/blogs/culture-course/spa-francorchamps-le-temple-de-la-pluie-du-brouillard-et-du-genie-automobile),
  [visitwallonia.com](https://visitwallonia.com/en-gb/3/where-to-go-in-wallonia/walloon-towns-and-cities/spa/surroundings))
- **Highest-elevation circuit in F1.** Spa has **the greatest difference between
  its high and low points of any F1 track — 102.2 m.** Nicknamed the "Ardennes
  roller coaster"; speeds exceed 330 km/h.
  ([f1-fansite.com](https://www.f1-fansite.com/f1-circuits/spa-francorchamps-circuit/),
  [BRM](https://brm-chronographes.com/en/blog/spa-francorchamps-circuit-n28))
- **Eau Rouge / Raidillon hillside** — the most dramatic elevation feature in F1.
  "Raidillon" = "steep path"; the corner has a **17% gradient and 40.8 m
  (134 ft) of elevation change.** The car drops into the Eau Rouge creek valley,
  then sweeps hard left-right-left UP the hillside.
  ([medium / Formula One Forever](https://medium.com/formula-one-forever/the-iconic-corners-chicanes-and-stretches-of-spa-francorchamps-7e853b5f85f7),
  [circuitsofthepast.com](https://www.circuitsofthepast.com/eau-rouge-or-raidillon/))
- **Forest comes right up to the track.** "The circuit is surrounded by the
  trees of the Ardennes forest… spectators are perched precariously on the
  hillside amongst the trees." General-admission banks are natural grass/mud
  slopes among the pines, not paved terraces.
  ([motorsporttickets.com](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/belgium-f1-seating-guide-spa/),
  [spa-francorchamps.be](https://www.spa-francorchamps.be/en/the-circuit))
- **Microclimate / weather.** Belgian GP runs in **late July/August, 18–25 °C**,
  the **highest rain probability (~55%) of any European round**, 65–85%
  humidity, and **morning mist/fog in the forested valley.** The 7 km lap is so
  long it can be **wet at one end and dry at the other** (rain at La Source while
  the sun shines at the Bus Stop).
  ([gpweather.com](https://gpweather.com/blog/circuit-de-spa-francorchamps-weather-guide),
  [alpinef1.com](https://www.alpinef1.com/news/mastering-the-wet-weather-at-spa))

## Vegetation & terrain

- **Dominant cover: dense Ardennes conifer forest** — spruce/pine plantation
  ("beautiful pine woodland inside the track") mixed with deciduous beech. The
  forest is a near-continuous dark-green wall on both sides of much of the lap
  and **comes right up to the trackside.**
  ([motorsporttickets.com](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/belgium-f1-seating-guide-spa/),
  [spagrandprix.com](https://www.spagrandprix.com/en/the-circuit))
- **Terrain is extremely hilly** — 102.2 m total elevation change, a forested
  valley (the Eau Rouge creek / Amblève river valley) with the track climbing,
  cresting and plunging continuously. Burnenville/Stavelot drop into the Amblève
  valley.
  ([motorsporttickets.com](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/belgium-f1-seating-guide-spa/))
- Spectator areas are **natural grass/mud hillside banks among the trees**, not
  engineered terraces — a key "wild forest" character cue.
- Microclimate keeps the forest lush and often misty; **morning fog in the
  valley** is characteristic.
  ([gpweather.com](https://gpweather.com/blog/circuit-de-spa-francorchamps-weather-guide))

## Grandstands, pit buildings, kerbs, surface

- **Kerbs: yellow-and-red rumble strips in the colours of Wallonia** are the
  modern signature (historically white-and-red, a far older tradition). Both are
  authentic to Spa.
  ([spa-francorchamps.be — new colours](https://www.spa-francorchamps.be/en/spa-francorchamps-track-decked-out-new-colours),
  [gtplanet forum](https://www.gtplanet.net/forum/threads/how-hard-can-it-be-to-update-spa-francorchamps.315791/))
- **Belgian-flag painted run-offs.** The enlarged run-offs at the **Raidillon of
  Eau Rouge and at the Bus Stop chicane exit are painted black-yellow-red** (the
  Belgian flag) — a highly distinctive, photographable visual unique to Spa.
  ([spa-francorchamps.be](https://www.spa-francorchamps.be/en/spa-francorchamps-track-decked-out-new-colours),
  [coliseum-online.com](https://www.coliseum-online.com/spa-to-bloom-in-belgian-flag-colors/))
- **Gravel traps reinstated 2022** (light grey-brown) at La Source, Les Combes,
  Speaker's Corner, Pouhon, Stavelot and Blanchimont — replacing earlier asphalt
  run-off and Astroturf. Mix of **gravel + asphalt run-off** now coexists.
  ([motorsport.com](https://www.motorsport.com/f1/news/spa-80million-euro-revamp-gravel-traps/4888499/),
  [racefans.net](https://www.racefans.net/2021/12/23/new-spa-photos-show-gravel-replacing-asphalt-run-offs/))
- **2022 €80 m revamp** added ~13,000 grandstand seats: a **4,600-seat
  grandstand atop the Raidillon**, a new Endurance grandstand + terraces, plus a
  later new grandstand at La Source. Resurfaced from exit T17→entry T2 (incl. the
  pit lane fast lane), T4→T8, and T14→T15.
  ([racefans.net](https://www.racefans.net/2022/03/22/new-look-spa-francorchamps-for-2022-revealed-after-completion-of-renovation-work/),
  [motorsportmagazine.com](https://www.motorsportmagazine.com/articles/motorcycles/new-grandstands-gravel-traps-in-e80m-spa-francorchamps-revamp/),
  [racefans round-up — La Source](https://www.racefans.net/2022/12/06/racefans-round-up-06-12-5/))
- **Towers:** the classic **Uniroyal Tower (1985–Oct 2025)** over the pit lane —
  the iconic Spa silhouette for decades — replaced by the modern **~30 m glass
  Uhoda Tower (inaugurated 12 June 2026)** in the same location.
  ([belganewsagency.eu](https://www.belganewsagency.eu/spa-francorchamps-unveils-plans-for-successor-of-uniroyal-tower),
  [spa-francorchamps.be — Uhoda](https://www.spa-francorchamps.be/en/news/572_spa-francorchamps-unveils-the-uhoda-tower-a-new-beacon-of-excellence-and-innovation))

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **DAY race, temperate, frequently overcast/wet.** Spa is the wettest European
  round — overcast skies, soft diffuse light, and morning valley fog are the
  defining mood. No floodlit night racing; lean into cool, slightly hazy,
  green-tinted daylight rather than bright blue clear-sky.
  ([gpweather.com](https://gpweather.com/blog/circuit-de-spa-francorchamps-weather-guide))
- **National / circuit identity: Belgian flag black-yellow-red**, plus the
  **Wallonia yellow-and-red** kerbs/rumble strips. These are the signature
  on-track colour accents.
  ([spa-francorchamps.be](https://www.spa-francorchamps.be/en/spa-francorchamps-track-decked-out-new-colours))
- **Signage:** sponsor hoardings line the trackside walls; grandstands carry
  sponsor banners. Marketed officially as "the most beautiful circuit in the
  world" — the brand leans on the natural forest scenery.
  ([spa-francorchamps.be](https://www.spa-francorchamps.be/en/the-circuit))

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

What the game currently builds for Spa (per the brief):
Ardennes forest treelines; sparse grandstands; minimal infra; highest-elevation
circuit; Eau Rouge on natural hillside.

Gaps:

1. **Forest density & proximity.** Real Spa forest is a near-continuous DENSE
   conifer wall (spruce/pine) right at trackside, with mixed beech. If current
   treelines are sparse/scattered, they under-sell Spa's defining "walled-in by
   forest" look — especially along the **Kemmel straight, Pouhon, Blanchimont
   and Stavelot.**
2. **Conifer dominance.** Verify the tree mix is conifer-dominant (fir/`conifer`
   in the FURN system), not broadleaf/palm. Spa should read dark blue-green, not
   light deciduous.
3. **Belgian-flag run-off paint is missing.** The black-yellow-red painted
   run-off at Raidillon and the Bus Stop is one of Spa's most recognisable visual
   signatures and is presumably absent.
4. **Kerb colours.** Should be **yellow-and-red (Wallonia)** rumble strips, not a
   generic red/white — confirm Spa's kerb livery.
5. **Pit/tower landmark.** No mention of a control-tower landmark. Spa needs a
   tall pit-straight tower silhouette (classic Uniroyal slab, or the new
   ~30 m Uhoda glass tower) to read as Spa, plus a modern pit/garage frontage.
6. **Grandstands too sparse.** Post-2022 Spa has substantial grandstands at the
   **Raidillon top, La Source, and pit straight** — the brief flags these as
   currently sparse.
7. **Gravel traps.** Light grey-brown gravel run-off at the named corners is a
   strong post-2022 visual; confirm it's represented vs flat asphalt/grass.
8. **Atmosphere.** Ensure Spa's default day atmosphere is cool/overcast/hazy
   (humid-overcast bias in `_trackAtmoBias`), not a clear saturated-blue sky.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Max-density conifer treeline.** Set Spa's FURN tree kind to `fir`
   (conifer/spruce) at high density and short stride so the forest forms a
   near-continuous dark blue-green wall close to the verge — heaviest along
   Kemmel, Pouhon, Blanchimont, Stavelot. Add a minority of broadleaf (beech)
   and a few autumn-accent trees for variation; allow clustered forest stands.
2. **Conifer colour palette.** Tune tree tint cool dark blue-green (spruce), not
   bright/olive, to match the Ardennes plantation look.
3. **Paint the Belgian-flag run-offs.** Add black-yellow-red painted run-off
   stripes at **Raidillon (Eau Rouge exit, both sides)** and the **Bus Stop
   chicane exit / start line** — a distinctive, cheap, high-impact decal-style
   surface tint in `buildRoad`.
4. **Wallonia kerbs.** Set kerb livery to **yellow-and-red** rumble strips for
   Spa specifically.
5. **Add a pit-straight tower landmark.** Emit one tall slab/glass tower over the
   pit straight (classic Uniroyal silhouette or the modern ~30 m Uhoda glass
   tower) as the Spa hero structure, with light/white tones + sponsor signage.
6. **Modern pit/garage frontage + grandstands.** Place a light-toned pit/garage
   building run along the main straight, and grandstands at the **Raidillon top
   (the big 2022 stand), La Source, and pit straight** with sponsor banners.
7. **Gravel traps.** Render light grey-brown gravel run-off at La Source, Les
   Combes, Pouhon, Stavelot and Blanchimont (vs flat asphalt/grass).
8. **Elevation.** Keep/strengthen Spa as the most-graded circuit — ensure the
   Eau Rouge/Raidillon section has the steep ~17% climb (~41 m) and the lap reads
   as a forested valley roller-coaster (~100 m total range).
9. **Overcast day atmosphere.** Bias Spa toward humid/overcast in
   `_trackAtmoBias` (pale, hazy, cool green-tinted daylight), optionally with a
   touch of low valley haze/mist — never a clear deep-blue zenith.
10. **Natural grass banks.** Keep spectator-side terrain as natural grass slopes
    among trees rather than hard paved terraces, to preserve Spa's "wild forest"
    character.
