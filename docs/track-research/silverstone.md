# Silverstone — visual reference

Visual-reference research for the procedural per-track scenery of the British GP
circuit (Silverstone, Northamptonshire, UK). Goal: make the game's surroundings
match the real circuit's distinctive identity — a flat former WWII airfield in
open English parkland, dominated by the modern silver "Wing" pit complex.

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Former WWII RAF bomber airfield.** RAF Silverstone was built 1942, operational
  1943–1947 as the base of No. 17 Operational Training Unit (Vickers Wellington
  night-bomber crew training). It was sited on **flat, open farmland**, with
  **three runways in the classic WWII triangular pattern**, whose outline still
  lies within the present track. The first British Grand Prix was run here in 1948.
  ([RAF Silverstone — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RAF_Silverstone),
  [Development history of Silverstone Circuit — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Development_history_of_Silverstone_Circuit))

- **The terrain is airfield-flat.** The whole circuit sits on the old airfield, so
  it is "relatively flat" with only gentle undulation — roughly **~22 m of total
  elevation change** over the 5.71 km lap. The most notable drop is **out of Stowe
  (T15) down into Vale and Club (T16–18)**. Views from Becketts and Club are
  described as "unobstructed from natural elevation" because the site is airfield-flat.
  ([Highs and lows — which F1 track has the most elevation changes? — Formula1.com](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/highs-and-lows-which-f1-track-has-the-most-elevation-changes-.7I9JEcBw3R2AqXbnJ6hyvc),
  [Silverstone Circuit — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit))

- **Surroundings are open English parkland / mixed farmland, NOT dense forest.**
  The circuit lies in the **Yardley-Whittlewood Ridge National Character Area**: a
  mixed agricultural landscape of **medium-sized arable fields and pasture,
  interspersed with blocks of woodland and bounded by mature hedgerows** (hedgerow
  trees usually **oak or ash**, with suckering elm). Small dense woodland pockets
  are called **"copses"** — and several Silverstone corners are named for them
  (Copse corner cuts along the edge of the old **Chapel Copse** woodland).
  ([Yardley-Whittlewood Ridge — National Character Area Profiles](https://nationalcharacterareas.co.uk/yardley-whittlewood-ridge/analysis-landscape-attributes-opportunities/),
  [What's in a name? — Formula1.com](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/whats-in-a-name-the-history-behind-silverstones-iconic-corners.4Q3miKVCXBkz5rKtyYnL5z))

- **The Silverstone Wing — the signature landmark.** A **390 m long, up to 30 m
  high** pit & paddock complex by architect **Populous**, opened **18 May 2011**
  (cost ~£27 m). Houses **41 pit garages**, race control, the **podium**, a media
  centre and hospitality. The **roofline continuously rises and falls like an
  aerofoil/wing** to evoke both aviation (the RAF heritage) and speed, pointing to
  the sky at the last corner. Cladding is **polyester powder-coated steel in three
  finishes — Metallic Silver (RAL 9006), Anthracite Grey (RAL 7016) and Graphite
  Grey (RAL 2074)** — an angular silver/grey frontispiece running the full length
  of the pits, forming the backdrop to the winner's podium.
  ([Silverstone Wing — WikiArquitectura](https://en.wikiarquitectura.com/building/silverstone-pit-lane-paddock-silverstone-wing/),
  [The Silverstone Wing Building — Ash & Lacy](https://www.ashandlacy.com/case-studies/the-silverstone-wing-building/),
  [Silverstone Circuit — Populous](https://populous.com/showcases/silverstone-circuit))

- **The BRDC Clubhouse** (British Racing Drivers' Club) opened **1999**, a landmark
  set in the infield with a restaurant, roof terrace and garden overlooking the
  circuit. The BRDC also has a members' campsite/motorhome paddock in the **infield**.
  ([Clubhouse — BRDC](https://brdc.co.uk/about/clubhouse/),
  [British Racing Drivers' Club — Silverstone](https://www.silverstone.co.uk/about/british-racing-drivers-club))

- **Two pit/start complexes.** F1 starts/finishes at the **Wing** (between Club and
  Abbey, on the "Hamilton Straight"); most other series use the **original pits**
  (the "International" start/finish on the Old Pit Straight near Woodcote). The
  southern half can run independently as the **International Circuit**.
  ([Silverstone — RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/united-kingdom/silverstone.html))

- **Kerbs.** Currently **red and white**. (History: black/white in the Shell era,
  red/white for Marlboro, blue/white during the Santander sponsorship, then back to
  red/white.) Kerbs here are **flat** — a high-speed circuit, drivers run wide over
  them. ([Silverstone Circuit — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit),
  [Red and white kerbs — Autosport Forums](https://forums.autosport.com/topic/138544-red-and-white-kerbs/))

- **Run-off & resurfacing.** The 2010/2011 Arena layout used **~58,000 m² of asphalt
  run-off** and **~56,000 m² of gravel** run-off, **3,000 m of new kerbs**,
  **4,000 m² of astroturf** and **2,700 m³ of concrete**. Fully resurfaced again in
  early 2022 with a high-grip compound and microgrooves for water dispersion; a new
  camber was shaped so water runs off (anti-aquaplaning).
  ([Silverstone facts — Sky Sports](https://www.skysports.com/f1/news/12538/6246046/silverstone-facts),
  [Silverstone Circuit — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silverstone_Circuit))

## Vegetation & terrain

- **Open, FLAT, parkland/agricultural — not forest.** The dominant ground texture is
  **grass and arable fields**, a legacy of the airfield's wide open expanses. Avoid a
  continuous wall of dense trees.
- **Hedgerows are the signature vegetation grid.** Medium fields **bounded by mature
  hedgerows**; hedgerow trees are **oak and ash**. This patchwork of hedge-lined
  fields is the defining English-farmland look around the circuit.
- **Copses = small dense woodland pockets,** scattered (not continuous), some named
  (Chapel Copse / "Cheese Copse" by Copse corner). Mostly **broadleaf (oak, ash,
  elm)**; some mixed conifer windbreak rows exist as farm shelterbelts.
- **Terrain is airfield-flat,** ~22 m total elevation change across the lap; only
  gentle undulation. The one notable feature is the **drop out of Stowe into
  Vale/Club**. Backdrops should be **low and distant** (open horizon), with **NO
  mountains, hills, or snow** — the land is genuinely flat.

## Grandstands, pit buildings, kerbs, surface

- **The Wing pit building:** long, low-to-mid-rise, **silver/anthracite/graphite-grey
  metallic cladding** with a continuously **undulating (rising/falling) aerofoil
  roof**, large glazed track-facing facade, ~390 m long. The defining modern landmark
  — must read as **silver/metallic**, not white masonry.
- **Grandstands:** numerous large **covered** stands with steel/tensile roofs
  (white/silver/grey), tiered seating, sponsor/brand boards along the front. Major
  ones at Copse, Becketts, Stowe, Club, Luffield, Woodcote, Village, Abbey, Hamilton
  Straight. British-event branding (Union-flag accents, sponsor colours).
- **Old pits / International stand:** older neutral/white concrete buildings on the
  Old Pit Straight — a contrast to the modern Wing.
- **Kerbs:** **red and white** (flat, high-speed kerbs). Some yellow/astroturf
  edging exists at apex run-offs.
- **Surface:** dark high-grip **asphalt** (resurfaced 2022), with a rubbered-in
  racing line. **Run-off is predominantly asphalt** (paved) with **gravel traps** at
  some corners and astroturf strips at the edges.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Daytime race** (British GP, early July). Temperate maritime climate — **often
  grey/overcast, soft diffuse light**, occasionally bright. Skies should lean
  **cool/pale British summer**, not deep-blue clear desert skies. The game's `day`
  atmosphere should bias toward the **overcast/humid** end (pale zenith, low haze).
- **No floodlights needed for racing** (day event); the circuit is not a night venue,
  though general infrastructure lighting (lamp posts around the Wing/paddock) exists.
- **Signage:** large **advertising hoardings / sponsor boards** around every major
  corner and along the pit straight; trackside sponsor banners; sector/timing boards;
  British identity (Union-flag motifs, "SILVERSTONE" branding). Brand colours are
  varied and bright against the green/grey backdrop.
- **National / circuit identity:** **British** — Union-flag red/white/blue; the
  circuit's own brand is the **silver of the Wing** plus the green of the parkland.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current build (`js/tracks/silverstone.js`) is already fairly rich. Findings:

- **GOOD — already present:** the Wing as a long building with cantilevered roof
  blade, glazing strip and lit windows; a Wing control tower; BRDC clubhouse;
  hedgerow grid; named oak copses via `forestEdge`; conifer/broadleaf windbreaks;
  farm-shed/hangar outbuildings; flat low treeline backdrop; many grandstands;
  red/white kerbs; start gantry; pit garages; billboards. Conceptually well-aligned.

- **GAP 1 — The Wing colour is WHITE, should be SILVER/METALLIC.** The building wall
  is `[0.86, 0.86, 0.88]` (near-white) and the roof blade `[0.90, 0.92, 0.96]`. The
  real Wing is **metallic silver (RAL 9006) + anthracite/graphite grey** — it should
  read as a cooler, more metallic silver-grey, ideally with low roughness so it
  catches the sky (use the `glass`/reflective path for the facade if available).

- **GAP 2 — The Wing roof is a flat blade, not the undulating aerofoil.** The single
  `addBox` roof blade misses the building's most iconic feature: the **continuously
  rising/falling wing-shaped roofline**. A stepped/segmented roof (several boxes at
  varying heights, or a `addPrism`/`addFrustum` profile) would capture the silhouette.

- **GAP 3 — Backdrop uses `mountain()` rings (up to 26 m tall green rises).** The site
  is **airfield-FLAT**. Three concentric rings of `mountain()` create hills that
  shouldn't exist. Replace with **low flat treeline/hedgerow backdrops** only (the
  `backdrop()` flat treeline is correct; the mountain rings are not).

- **GAP 4 — Forest density may be too high in places.** Several `forestEdge` belts at
  density 0.4–0.8 risk a "wall of trees" look. Real Silverstone is **open** — copses
  should be **discrete clumps separated by open grass/fields**, with hedgerows doing
  most of the boundary work. Keep copses, thin the continuous belts.

- **GAP 5 — No explicit Old Pits / International pit complex.** Only the Wing and a
  small pit control tower exist. The **original pit straight grandstand/buildings**
  (a second, older neutral-white complex) are a distinct landmark worth adding.

- **GAP 6 — Atmosphere not explicitly biased overcast.** Palette is a reasonable
  cool blue (`zenith [0.28,0.38,0.58]`), but real British GP light is frequently
  **grey/overcast/diffuse**; the `day` atmosphere should bias toward overcast/humid
  (pale, hazy) rather than deep saturated clear-sky.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Recolour the Wing to metallic silver-grey.** Change wall to ~`[0.62,0.64,0.68]`
   (RAL 9006 metallic silver) with darker anthracite accents `[0.30,0.31,0.34]`
   (RAL 7016) on lower bands; route the facade glazing through the reflective
   `glassBuf` path (low roughness) so it mirrors the (often grey) sky. Keep warm
   amber lit-window band for depth.

2. **Make the Wing roof undulate.** Replace the single flat roof blade with **3–5
   segmented roof sections at stepping heights** (e.g. peaks ~11–16 m rising toward
   one end) to suggest the aerofoil silhouette pointing skyward at the last corner.

3. **Remove the `mountain()` backdrop rings; keep it FLAT.** Delete the three
   concentric `mountain()` loops. Rely on the low flat `backdrop()` treelines plus a
   distant hedgerow/copse silhouette so the horizon reads as open airfield farmland.

4. **Convert continuous forest belts into discrete copses + open gaps.** Reduce the
   density of the long `forestEdge` windbreak belts (e.g. 0.45 → ~0.25) and keep the
   named copses as **tight clumps** with clear grass/field gaps between them. Lean on
   `hedge()` for field boundaries (already good — keep the grid).

5. **Add the Old Pits / International complex** as a second, older neutral-white
   building cluster + a modest grandstand on the **Old Pit Straight (near Woodcote)**,
   distinct from the silver Wing, to capture Silverstone's two-paddock identity.

6. **Bias the day atmosphere overcast.** Pale the zenith slightly and raise low haze
   for the British-summer grey look (or ensure `_trackAtmoBias` treats Silverstone as
   humid/overcast rather than clear). Soft diffuse light, cool-pale sky.

7. **Keep red/white kerbs** (already correct); ensure **asphalt-dominant run-off**
   framing at apexes (paved `CONC` aprons, not large gravel) to match the modern
   layout, with small astroturf/yellow edge accents.

8. **Reinforce British signage identity:** Union-flag-accented hoardings and
   "SILVERSTONE" branding among the existing billboards; sponsor boards along the
   Hamilton Straight in front of the Wing.
