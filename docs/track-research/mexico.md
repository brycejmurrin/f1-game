# Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez (Mexico City) — visual reference

Research compiled for Apex 26 procedural scenery. The circuit's signature is that
it is **not a desert and not a typical street track** — it is a flat motor-racing
circuit set inside a large green urban sports **park** (Ciudad Deportiva Magdalena
Mixhuca) in eastern Mexico City, at extreme altitude, and the final third of the
lap runs **through the middle of an enclosed concert/baseball stadium** (Foro Sol /
Estadio GNP Seguros) packed with fans on all sides. The atmosphere is the loudest,
most festive fiesta on the F1 calendar, late-October (around Día de Muertos).

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Setting: a public sports park, not desert.** The track sits inside the
  Magdalena Mixhuca Sports City (Ciudad Deportiva), a public park in southeast /
  east Mexico City (borough of Iztacalco), which was the official Olympic park for
  the **1968 Summer Olympics**. Flat terrain, green areas, trees, surrounded by
  dense Mexico City urban sprawl.
  [racingcircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/north-america/mexico/mexico-city.html),
  [aroundus.com](https://aroundus.com/p/5130560-autodromo-hermanos-rodriguez),
  [cdmx.gob.mx](https://mexicocity.cdmx.gob.mx/venues/hermanos-rodriguez-race-track/?lang=en)
- **Altitude: ~2,240 m (≈7,350 ft) above sea level — the highest circuit ever to
  host an FIA-sanctioned event.** Thin air = reduced downforce/cooling, long
  full-throttle straight, and a slightly hazy, high-UV daylight look. (Some sources
  cite 2,285 m / 7,500 ft.)
  [racingcircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/north-america/mexico/mexico-city.html),
  [planetf1.com](https://www.planetf1.com/tracks/autodromo-hermanos-rodriguez)
- **Track: 4.304 km, 17 corners, ~1.2 km start/finish straight** (one of the longest
  full-throttle sections on the calendar). Surface is famously **bumpy** because
  Mexico City sits on a geologically active (former lakebed) region; largely
  re-laid for the 2015 F1 return after a Hermann Tilke 2014–15 redesign.
  [racefans.net](https://www.racefans.net/f1-information/going-to-a-race/autodromo-hermanos-rodriguez-circuit-information/),
  [grandprixgrandtours.com](https://www.grandprixgrandtours.com/mexico-circuit-guide/)
- **Day race, late October.** Falls around the Día de Muertos (Day of the Dead)
  season — the event leans hard into that cultural imagery.
  [formula1.com fan guide](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/colour-culture-and-corners-the-ultimate-fan-guide-to-mexico-city.6t1hYHDy2vilos7khojsyf)
- **Record crowds:** 2024 weekend drew 404,958 spectators over three days (record),
  ninth consecutive sell-out; circuit race-day capacity is around 100,000+.
  [formula1.com](https://www.formula1.com/en/racing/2025/mexico)

## Skyline & architecture / terrain

- **Flat.** The circuit and park are essentially flat (modest elevation changes
  only); no hills, no mountains immediately bordering the track, no banked sweeps
  anymore. The historic **Peraltada** — once a monstrous 180-degree high-speed
  banked final corner — was bypassed and built over by the Foro Sol stadium after
  the 1992 GP; the modern layout slices through where it used to be, retaining only
  a fast, slightly banked final corner ("Peraltada" T17) onto the main straight.
  [planetf1 history](https://www.planetf1.com/features/autodromo-hermanos-rodriguez-mexican-gp-history),
  [racingcircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/north-america/mexico/mexico-city.html)
- **Signature skyline element: the Palacio de los Deportes copper dome.** A
  116 m-diameter dome of copper-clad aluminium hyperbolic-paraboloid panels on a
  steel-arch lattice — a warm, oxidised copper/orange colour that reads sharply
  against the grey-blue sky. It is in the same Magdalena Mixhuca sports park and is
  visible from most of the track — the single most distinctive built landmark of
  the setting.
  [archdaily.com](https://www.archdaily.com/1041169/beyond-the-shell-felix-candelas-palacio-de-los-deportes-for-the-1968-mexico-olympics),
  [racingcircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/north-america/mexico/mexico-city.html)
- **City backdrop:** beyond the green park, the horizon is a dense, flat, low-to-mid
  rise Mexico City sprawl, not a glamorous high-rise skyline — utilitarian painted
  concrete blocks, the airport nearby. High-altitude light tends toward a hazy,
  bright, slightly washed daytime sky.

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Grandstands dominate the look.** Enormous, very tall, very densely packed
  grandstands — the crowd is the scenery. The **Foro Sol stadium bowl** in the final
  sector is the defining feature: tiered concrete grandstands wrapping **fully
  around** the cars so the slow esses run between two solid walls of fans, capped by
  the **new 2024 white tensile roof canopy** sweeping around the rim.
  [stadiumdb.com](https://stadiumdb.com/news/2024/08/mexico_legendary_foro_sol_reopens_in_a_new_look),
  [motorsporttickets.com](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/mexico-grand-prix-grandstand-guide-where-to-watch-the-race-in-mexico-city/)
- **Stadium capacity / enclosure:** Foro Sol holds ~25,000–30,000 for the F1 award
  ceremony front-on; "the only circuit where 30,000 people can watch the podium up
  front." The two banks are Foro Sol Sur (GS14) and Foro Sol Norte (GS15).
  [thestadiumbusiness.com](https://www.thestadiumbusiness.com/2024/06/19/foro-sol-enters-new-era-as-estadio-gnp-seguros/),
  [oversteer48.com GS14](https://oversteer48.com/grandstand-14-f1-mexico/)
- **Main grandstand:** long, tall, **mostly covered/roofed** grandstand facing the
  pit building across the 1.2 km straight.
  [oversteer48.com main GS](https://oversteer48.com/main-grandstand-1-f1-mexic/)
- **Sponsor boards & branding:** heavy trackside advertising hoardings and
  colour-coded spectator zones (Green/Blue zones, "Blue Grandstands"). Big screens
  in the stadium section.
  [motorsporttickets.com](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/mexico-grand-prix-grandstand-guide-where-to-watch-the-race-in-mexico-city/)
- **Barriers:** conventional FIA armco/concrete walls with TecPro/tyre barriers at
  braking zones — a modern permanent circuit, not a street armco look. The final
  corner historically had an unforgiving concrete wall (the reason the old Peraltada
  was removed).
  [planetf1 history](https://www.planetf1.com/features/autodromo-hermanos-rodriguez-mexican-gp-history)
- **Kerbs & surface:** standard F1 **red/white** painted kerbs; grey asphalt;
  notably **bumpy** surface; generous modern asphalt/gravel run-off. Drivers must
  ration how much kerb they take or the car gets unsettled.
  [racefans.net](https://www.racefans.net/f1-information/going-to-a-race/autodromo-hermanos-rodriguez-circuit-information/)

## Vegetation & water

- **Green park, NOT sand.** The defining ground cover is **grass and trees** of a
  city park, plus landscaped green areas of the sports city. Explicitly a "public
  park" / "park environment."
  [cdmx.gob.mx](https://mexicocity.cdmx.gob.mx/venues/hermanos-rodriguez-race-track/?lang=en),
  [aroundus.com](https://aroundus.com/p/5130560-autodromo-hermanos-rodriguez)
- **Trees inside and around the track.** Documented stands of trees inside the
  infield (e.g. inside the old oval Turns 3–4) and treelines around the park —
  broadleaf park trees, with some palms typical of central-Mexico landscaping.
  [racingcircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/north-america/mexico/mexico-city.html)
- **No significant water feature.** It is a dry park (Mexico City sits on a drained
  lakebed but there's no lake at the circuit). Vegetation is the green dressing —
  treat verges as lush-to-medium grass, not dusty sand.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Day race, festive fiesta.** Widely described as F1's "ultimate fiesta" — feels
  more like a festival than a race, with mariachi bands, dancers, DJ sets, street-
  food stalls, live music, and "bursts of colour and sound around every corner."
  The stadium section roars.
  [formula1.com fiesta](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/how-the-mexico-city-grand-prix-became-formula-1s-ultimate-fiesta.6pheuHLRusNVvyXOPOD63S),
  [formula1.com 2025 whats-on](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/a-fiesta-of-speed-whats-on-at-the-2025-mexico-city-grand-prix-full-race.5rMgCugvOsYZS0HDS3U8XQ)
- **Día de Muertos imagery.** Vibrant **Day of the Dead** displays fill the stadium
  section; **La Catrina** (elegant skeleton figure) face paint and floral headbands,
  **calaveras** (skulls), **alebrijes** (colourful spirit animals), and
  **papel picado** (rows of cut-paper flag bunting) everywhere. Marigold-orange,
  magenta, purple, and white are the signature palette.
  [formula1.com fiesta](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/how-the-mexico-city-grand-prix-became-formula-1s-ultimate-fiesta.6pheuHLRusNVvyXOPOD63S),
  [mexicotravel.blog](https://mexicotravel.blog/day-of-the-dead-in-mexico-city/)
- **Lucha libre & mariachi.** Masked lucha libre wrestlers mingle in the crowd;
  mariachi sombreros and traditional dress are part of the spectacle (Hamilton
  famously donned a sombrero / climbed a lucha ring).
  [espn.com](https://www.espn.com/blog/onenacion/print?id=8080)
- **National identity:** Mexican flag green/white/red is omnipresent; huge support
  historically for local hero Sergio "Checo" Pérez. Crowd brings "wild passion."
- **Post-race ritual:** the podium is inside Foro Sol, fans flood onto the track in
  front of the winner, and DJs play to the packed bowl long after the chequered
  flag — a unique, stadium-concert finish.
  [oversteer48.com GS14](https://oversteer48.com/grandstand-14-f1-mexico/)
- **Lighting:** bright high-altitude daylight, slightly hazy/UV-bright; this is a
  day race so floodlights are decorative/secondary, but the new white stadium roof
  catches and bounces the daylight.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current game builds (Mexico, modern theme): baseball stadium (Foro Sol) infield;
Chapultepec park treelines; palms; festive papel-picado banners; floodlit
grandstands.

What's right:
- Foro Sol baseball stadium concept ✔
- Park treelines + palms ✔ (good — confirms the green-park, not-desert direction)
- Papel-picado banners ✔ (authentic — keep and expand)
- Floodlit grandstands ✔ as structures, but…

Gaps / corrections:
1. **It's a DAY race, not floodlit/night.** Floodlights should be off/decorative;
   prioritise bright hazy high-altitude daylight, not a night palette. (Night
   floodlight masts can stay per the engine's universal-mast rule but shouldn't
   dominate the look.)
2. **The stadium must fully ENCLOSE the track, not just be an infield prop.** The
   real signature is the cars threading the slow esses **between two solid walls of
   tiered, packed grandstands** with a continuous roof canopy overhead-ish around
   the rim. Currently it reads as a stadium beside/inside, not a tunnel of crowd.
3. **Missing the Palacio de los Deportes copper dome** — the one truly iconic
   built landmark on the skyline. A large copper/orange geodesic dome would be the
   highest-impact single addition.
4. **"Chapultepec park" is the wrong park** — the circuit is in **Ciudad Deportiva
   Magdalena Mixhuca**, not Chapultepec. The vibe (green city park) is right; the
   name/reference should be corrected. Add the **velodrome** and generic Olympic
   sports-park buildings rather than Chapultepec-specific dressing.
5. **No new white Foro Sol roof canopy.** Since 2024 the bowl has a sweeping pale
   tensile roof — a strong silhouette cue currently absent.
6. **Under-using Día de Muertos crowd colour.** Beyond papel-picado, the crowd and
   props should carry Catrina skulls, marigold-orange/magenta, alebrijes — the
   single most distinctive cultural signature.
7. **Crowd density is the scenery.** Mexico's grandstands are exceptionally tall and
   packed; make stadium grandstands taller and denser than a generic track.
8. **Mexican flag green/white/red** should tint barriers/banners/kerb accents.
9. **Surface should read bumpy/used grey asphalt** with red/white kerbs — already
   plausible via the engine's wear tinting; keep light, not desert-dusty.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

- **Time of day:** set/bias Mexico to **bright day** (high-altitude hazy daylight),
  not night. Reduce floodlight contribution to ambient; let the sun dominate.
  Slightly raise haze for thin-air look. Keep masts (engine rule) but unlit.
- **Foro Sol as an enclosing bowl:** in the final-sector stadium section, place
  **tall tiered grandstand walls on BOTH sides** of the slow esses so the cars run
  through a corridor of crowd. Add a **pale/white sweeping roof canopy** ring around
  the rim (low-roughness light material so it catches daylight). Pack the stands
  with dense multicoloured crowd billboards/sprites. Add **big screens** and a
  **podium/DJ stage** structure at the bowl.
- **Palacio de los Deportes dome:** add a large **copper/orange geodesic dome**
  (hyperbolic-paraboloid faceted look, ~wide low dome) on the skyline just outside
  the circuit, visible from much of the lap — the hero landmark. Warm oxidised
  copper colour (orange-bronze), faceted metallic panels.
- **Sports-park dressing:** add a **velodrome** (low oval building) and generic
  1968-Olympic-era concrete sports buildings near the dome; flat green park ground.
- **Vegetation:** lush-to-medium **green grass verges**, broadleaf **park trees** in
  clustered stands (especially infield around T2–4), plus scattered **palms**. No
  sand/dune dressing. Some autumn/marigold accent colour fits the late-October date.
- **Día de Muertos signage:** keep **papel-picado** bunting (rows of cut-paper flags
  in marigold-orange/magenta/purple/white); add **Catrina skull** motifs, **calavera**
  / **alebrije** colour accents on banners and crowd; a giant Catrina or skull
  installation as a hero prop near the start or stadium would be on-theme.
- **National colours:** tint barriers, banners and kerb caps with **green/white/red**;
  add Mexican-flag bunting along the main straight.
- **Barriers/kerbs:** standard **red/white kerbs**, grey FIA armco/TecPro/tyre
  barriers (modern permanent circuit, not street armco). Grey bumpy asphalt with
  generous run-off.
- **Main straight:** long covered **main grandstand** opposite a low modern **pit
  building** with team/sponsor branding; heavy sponsor hoardings throughout.
- **Crowd:** make Mexico's grandstands noticeably **taller and denser** than the
  generic track to convey the record, passionate crowds.
