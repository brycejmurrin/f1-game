# Suzuka — visual reference

Visual-reference research for the procedural scenery of **Suzuka International
Racing Course** (Japanese Grand Prix), Ino, Suzuka City, Mie Prefecture, Japan.
Honda-owned, opened 1962. Figure-of-eight layout, 5.807 km, 18 corners.

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Figure-8 with a crossover overpass.** Suzuka is the only FIA Grade 1 track
  with a figure-of-eight layout. The ~1.2 km back straight passes **over** the
  front section by means of a **bridge/overpass** — the track does not actually
  intersect itself. After the Degner Curves (sector 2) the track runs through a
  **tunnel under the main straight**, then climbs through the Crossover section,
  passes **above itself**, and feeds into 130R before the final chicane.
  https://www.crash.net/track/18/suzuka-circuit ,
  https://f1chronicle.substack.com/p/suzuka-international-racing-course
- **Adjacent amusement park "Motopia" / Suzuka Circuit Park.** A family theme
  park sits immediately beside the circuit with go-kart tracks, vehicle-themed
  rides, a racing theater and a giant Ferris wheel. A miniature replica of the
  Suzuka track is one of the rides. https://www.worldtripdiaries.com/motopia-the-suzuka-circuit-park/ ,
  https://livejapan.com/en/in-kansai/in-pref-mie/in-suzuka_yokkaichi/article-a2001013/
- **The Ferris wheel ("Circuit Wheel", formerly "Jupiter").** ~**50 m tall**
  (≈100 m above sea level), **bright red**, located **behind the finishing
  straight** / **to the right of the V grandstands** (which line the
  start/finish straight and pit lane). From the top you overlook Ise Bay and the
  whole circuit — paddock, main straight, grandstand and final turn. It is the
  single most iconic visual landmark of the venue and is plainly visible from the
  pit-straight / first-corner area on TV.
  https://www.suzukacircuit.jp/eng/park/attraction/circuit-wheel/ ,
  https://www.snaplap.net/suzuka-circuit/ ,
  https://www.gpfans.com/en/f1-news/1079854/f1-japanese-grand-prix-10-wild-facts-suzuka/
- **Significant elevation change.** Suzuka is one of F1's most undulating tracks
  (grouped with Spa, Red Bull Ring, Interlagos, Monaco). Cited **vertical gain
  ≈ 71 m** total across the lap; the start/finish straight sits on a gradual
  **downhill slope** (grid on the slope). https://www.tracktitan.io/post/suzuka-track-guide ,
  https://f1blast.com/which-f1-tracks-have-the-biggest-elevation-changes/
- **Spring race, daytime, mild.** Since 2024 the Japanese GP moved from October
  to **spring (late March)**; the 2026 race was 29 March. Race-day air ≈ 9–21 °C,
  cool clear temperate light. Historically an autumn race, so either palette is
  defensible, but current canon is a crisp spring day.
  https://www.total-motorsport.com/japanese-gp-2026-weather-forecast/ ,
  https://www.espn.com/f1/story/_/id/48229237/

## Vegetation & terrain

- Suzuka sits in **hilly, forested countryside** in Mie Prefecture. The circuit
  is wrapped by **dense mixed Japanese woodland** (broadleaf + conifer), with the
  **Suzuka Mountains** visible on the horizon and **Ise Bay** to the east (seen
  from the Ferris wheel). https://www.worldtripdiaries.com/motopia-the-suzuka-circuit-park/
- **Rolling, undulating terrain** is the circuit's defining physical trait
  (≈71 m vertical gain): sector 1 runs along **higher ground** up the esses; the
  track **drops** through the Degners and the tunnel to the **low point between
  T1–T2**; sector 3 **climbs back up** through Spoon, with 130R at a different
  elevation from the start/finish. The grid sits on a **downhill** start straight.
  https://www.tracktitan.io/post/suzuka-track-guide ,
  https://www.lightsoutblog.com/suzuka-ultimate-track-guide/
- Vegetation density is **high on both sides**, especially the outfield of the
  esses, Degners, Spoon and 130R — green forest banks rather than city. This is a
  **countryside/parkland circuit**, not a street/cityscape track.

## Grandstands, pit buildings, kerbs, surface

- **Main grandstand "V"** lines the start/finish straight opposite the pits,
  split V1 (lower) / V2 (upper, **roofed/covered**); large steel/grey multi-tier
  structure. Other big stands ring the esses, Degner, hairpin, Spoon and the
  final chicane (Q/R stands near the last corner, by the Ferris wheel).
  https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/suzuka-grandstand-guide/ ,
  https://www.japan.gp/en/map-of-the-grandstands-27
- **Pit building / paddock**: modern grey-and-white complex with glass frontage
  inside the start/finish straight. https://www.re-thinkingthefuture.com/articles/suzuka-circuit/
- **Kerbs**: classic FIA **red/white** kerbing, with **blue/white** accents at
  the chicane and some corner exits — standard Japanese-GP palette.
- **Gravel traps**: Suzuka is notable for **real gravel run-off**, not all
  asphalt — the **Degners (T8/T9)** and several other corners back onto **beige
  gravel traps** that regularly catch cars. Dunlop run-off was enlarged
  (12→25 m) in 2002. https://www.lightsoutblog.com/suzuka-ultimate-track-guide/
- **Surface tone**: dark-grey aged asphalt with a rubbered-in racing line;
  green grass verges between track and gravel/barriers.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Daytime race**, **late-March spring** light: cool, clear, temperate; air
  ≈ 9–21 °C, soft blue sky with the Suzuka mountains in haze. (Historically an
  October/autumn race — autumn-foliage accents are also period-authentic.)
  https://www.total-motorsport.com/japanese-gp-2026-weather-forecast/
- **National / venue identity**: Japanese — Honda-owned circuit; expect **red &
  white** accents (national colours, and Honda branding) on barriers, gantries
  and signage. The bright-red Ferris wheel reinforces the red signature.
- **Signage / sponsor boards**: corner-named sponsor branding — **Dunlop**
  (Dunlop curve), **Casio** (Casio Triangle chicane, now Hitachi Astemo),
  plus general F1/title-sponsor gantry advertising along the straights.
- No permanent floodlighting culture — it is a day venue (unlike the street
  night races). Keep it bright-day default.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

What the game already builds for Suzuka:
- Japanese tree forests on both sides, grandstands, the figure-8 layout, minimal
  cityscape. Good base — the forest + figure-8 already match reality.

Missing / wrong, in priority order:
1. **The bright-red Ferris wheel is absent.** This is THE single most iconic
   visual cue of Suzuka and is currently not placed — despite a ready-made
   `ferrisWheel(k, side, dist, radius)` helper existing in `js/tracks.js`
   (line ~2502). Highest-impact gap.
2. **The figure-8 crossover bridge/overpass is not modelled** as a visible
   structure. The layout crosses, but there's no concrete deck/piers where the
   back straight passes over the front section — the most distinctive piece of
   Suzuka architecture.
3. **No amusement-park dressing** behind the main grandstand (rides, the on-site
   hotel) — Motopia is a defining backdrop on the pit-straight side.
4. **Gravel traps**: confirm beige gravel run-off is rendered at the Degners and
   other corners rather than uniform asphalt/grass run-off.
5. **Terrain undulation**: ensure the ≈71 m vertical profile reads (uphill
   esses, downhill Degners to the T1–2 low point, climb through Spoon) — Suzuka's
   rolling character should be visible, not flat.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

In the track's `scenery(api)` (`js/tracks/suzuka.js`) and/or `buildProps`:

1. **Place the red Ferris wheel.** Use the existing helper:
   `api.ferrisWheel(k, side, dist, radius)` where `k` is the spline index near
   the **start/finish straight / final-chicane area** (behind the finishing
   straight, V-grandstand side). Suggested `radius ≈ 22–25 m` (real ≈50 m
   diameter scaled to game), `dist` placing it clearly behind the grandstand
   line. Override the rim/leg colours toward **bright red** (`[0.85, 0.12, 0.12]`
   structure) with white/multicolour cabins to match the real Circuit Wheel.
   The helper already calls `blockAt` for a footprint, so it won't be drivable.
2. **Build the crossover overpass.** Add a **grey concrete bridge deck** with two
   piers spanning the front section where the back straight crosses over (Degner-
   exit → 130R region). Use `addBox`/`addPrism` for the deck + `addCyl`/`addBox`
   piers; keep it off the driving boundary. This is the second-highest-impact
   addition after the wheel.
3. **Add a theme-park cluster** behind the main grandstand on the pit-straight
   side: a few bright primary-coloured low buildings + a neutral **hotel** block
   (`building()`/`addBox`), so the Ferris wheel reads as part of a park, not
   floating.
4. **Kerbs**: red/white standard with **blue/white** at the chicane; rubbered
   dark racing line on aged-grey asphalt.
5. **Gravel traps**: beige gravel run-off patches at the **Degners** and other
   classic corners; green grass verges elsewhere.
6. **Vegetation**: keep dense mixed Japanese forest both sides (broadleaf +
   conifer / `fir`), with the **Suzuka Mountains** as a hazy backdrop ridge and
   optional autumn-foliage accents (period-authentic for the old October date).
7. **Atmosphere**: bright temperate **spring day**, clear cool-blue sky, low haze
   on the mountain backdrop; no floodlights (day venue). Red/white Honda/Japanese
   accents on gantries and barriers. **Dunlop** and **Casio** sponsor boards at
   their namesake corners.
8. **Terrain**: make sure the elevation profile expresses the uphill esses /
   downhill Degners / Spoon climb so the ~71 m undulation is legible.
