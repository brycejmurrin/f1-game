# Singapore (Marina Bay) — visual reference

Research compiled June 2026 for procedural scenery accuracy. The Marina Bay
Street Circuit is the original F1 night race (debut 2008) and one of the most
landmark-dense circuits on the calendar — almost every corner frames a named
building. This document captures concrete visual facts (heights, colours,
shapes, positions) plus a gap analysis against the current game's
`js/tracks/singapore.js` scenery.

---

## Real-world surroundings (researched facts, with sources/URLs cited inline)

**The circuit.** A ~4.928 km street circuit (was 5.063 km until the 2023 layout
change) around Marina Bay, in the Downtown Core (Turns 4–19) and Kallang (Turns
1–3). The pit/paddock sits on reclaimed land off Republic Boulevard **right
beside the Singapore Flyer**. The lap runs under the Benjamin Sheares Bridge,
onto Raffles Boulevard, along Nicoll Highway, Stamford Road and St Andrew's Road
**around the Padang** (the open colonial cricket green) past City Hall, onto the
**Anderson Bridge past the Fullerton Hotel**, then a tight left to Esplanade
Drive **beside Merlion Park**, and back around the bay to the Flyer.
([Wikipedia: Marina Bay Street Circuit — via WebSearch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit),
[RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/asia/singapore/marina-bay-singapore.html),
[F1 fan guide](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/lights-skyscrapers-and-midnight-magic-the-ultimate-fan-guide-to-singapore.2uxSgT4DurbA3306cddca8))

**2023 layout change (important for what is/isn't trackside now).** Old Turns
16–19 were removed and replaced with a ~398 m straight; the track **no longer
runs under the Bay Grandstand / The Float**, because The Float was demolished in
2023 and is being rebuilt as **NS Square** (a 30,000-seat venue, due 2027).
([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit),
[gpblog](https://www.gpblog.com/en/news/official-revised-layout-for-f1-circuit-singapore-from-2023.html),
[NS Square — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NS_Square),
[The Float @ Marina Bay — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Float_@_Marina_Bay))

**Night race.** Run entirely under artificial light since 2008 — the first F1
race to do so. Over **1,600 floodlights** (Philips/Signify ArenaVision LEDs on
low trusses), ~3,000 lux, ~4× a typical stadium, custom-made to minimise glare
and surface reflection; >108 km / 220 km of cabling, ~3 MW.
([Signify](https://www.signify.com/global/our-company/blog/innovation/track-lighting),
[The Peak](https://www.thepeakmagazine.com.sg/lifestyle/f1-lights-signify),
[F1 fan guide](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/lights-skyscrapers-and-midnight-magic-the-ultimate-fan-guide-to-singapore.2uxSgT4DurbA3306cddca8))

**Geography to keep straight.** Marina Bay Sands, the ArtScience Museum, the
Helix Bridge and the Gardens-by-the-Bay Supertrees are on the **Marina South /
east side of the bay** — they are the dramatic *backdrop seen across the water*
from the pit straight and the bay-side turns, **not** buildings the track
threads between. The track itself runs through the colonial/CBD core (Fullerton,
Anderson Bridge, Padang, Esplanade) on the **north/west shore**. Getting that
near-vs-far separation right is what makes the skyline read correctly.

## Skyline & architecture

Two distinct architectural worlds meet at the bay, and the track sits between
them:

- **The CBD / Raffles Place skyline (inland, north-west):** a dense wall of
  **280 m grey-and-silver glass office towers** — UOB Plaza One, One Raffles
  Place (OUB), Republic Plaza — plus Guoco Tower (290 m, tallest in Singapore)
  further back. Cool-toned reflective glass, granite bases, triangular/octagonal
  crowns and antenna masts. This is the *dense canyon* backdrop behind the Padang
  and Anderson Bridge section.
  ([List of tallest buildings in Singapore — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_tallest_buildings_in_Singapore))
- **The Marina South icon cluster (across the water, east):** Marina Bay Sands'
  three towers + boat SkyPark, the white ArtScience lotus, the silver Helix
  Bridge arc, and the coloured Supertrees. These read as a **distinctive
  silhouette across the bay water**, smaller in apparent size and reflected in
  the bay.
- **Colonial low-rise (trackside, north shore):** the grey-granite Fullerton, the
  white Padang pavilions/City Hall/Supreme Court, and the spiky bronze Esplanade
  domes — a *human-scale, warm-stone* layer right at the barriers, in front of
  the tall glass behind.

Materials overall: lots of **reflective glass** (cool grey/blue), granite and
white stone on the heritage buildings, and steel (Flyer, Helix, Anderson
Bridge). Density is high and the skyline is tall and continuous — Singapore reads
as a modern megacity wrapping a bay.

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Surface:** notoriously **bumpy** — it's public street tarmac (Raffles
  Boulevard between Turns 5–7 is the worst), with manholes, paint and seams. Dark
  grey asphalt with a used, slightly uneven sheen; resurfacing never fully
  smooths it.
  ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit))
- **Kerbs:** historically **high and aggressive** — Massa likened them to
  "little tortoises". The standard F1 red/white kerbing, with extra-tall
  sausage/exit kerbs; the Turn 10 chicane kerbs were lowered after driver
  complaints. Painted run-off and white edge lines are visible.
  ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit))
- **Barriers:** classic street-circuit **concrete walls + steel Armco** lining
  almost the whole lap with very little room for error; debris fencing on top.
  **TecPro** barrier banks and tyre walls at the higher-speed run-offs (an extra
  TecPro layer was added at the Turn 10 run-off).
  ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marina_Bay_Street_Circuit))
- **Grandstands:** large temporary grandstands ring the bay-side and the pit
  straight (Pit, Promenade, Padang, Bay grandstands). The **Float/Bay Grandstand
  underpass** existed pre-2023 (cars passed *under* the grandstand) but is gone
  with the NS Square works.
  ([Wikipedia: The Float](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Float_@_Marina_Bay),
  [motorsporttickets grandstand guide](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/singapore-grand-prix-grandstand-guide-marina-bay-circuit-f1-race/))
- **Pit building:** a permanent ~350 m, three-storey aerodynamic **F1 Pit
  Building** with 36 garages, beside the Singapore Flyer; media centre, race
  control, podium and roof terrace above.
  ([myguidesingapore](https://www.myguidesingapore.com/business-and-mice/f1-pit-building))
- **Anderson Bridge / Fullerton area:** the track threads *across* the 1910
  steel-arch Anderson Bridge (Turns 11–13) directly in front of the floodlit
  grey-granite Fullerton — a signature TV shot, low colonial buildings under tall
  glass towers.

## Vegetation & water

- **The bay itself** is the central feature — Marina Bay is a large enclosed
  reservoir of dark water that **mirrors the floodlit skyline and Sands**.
  Superyachts moor in it during the GP. Water planes on the pit-straight/bay side
  are essential.
- **Roadside greenery:** Singapore is "the Garden City" — manicured **roadside
  trees, palms and planters** line the boulevards (Raffles Boulevard, Esplanade
  Drive), plus the **Padang's open green cricket field** and Esplanade Park /
  promenade lawns.
- **Supertrees (across the bay):** 18 tree-shaped vertical gardens, 25–50 m,
  with green planted trunks and flared steel canopies that **glow in shifting
  colours at night** (OCBC Garden Rhapsody). Distant backdrop, not trackside.
  ([Greenroofs.com](https://www.greenroofs.com/projects/gardens-by-the-bay-supertrees/))
- **Palms** are the dominant tree silhouette around the bay promenades; broadleaf
  rain trees line the inland boulevards.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **It is THE night race.** Warm twilight-to-black sky, the whole circuit lit by
  >1,600 cool-white LED floodlights at ~3,000 lux — bright, even, low-glare. The
  tarmac reads bright and slightly reflective; cars cast multiple soft shadows.
  ([Signify](https://www.signify.com/global/our-company/blog/innovation/track-lighting))
- **Skyline as a light show:** illuminated skyscrapers, the colour-cycling Helix
  Bridge (red/green DNA letters), the warm-lit Sands SkyPark, the multicolour
  Supertrees and frequent fireworks — all **reflected in the bay water**. Neon
  and LED building signage on the CBD towers and the Sands.
  ([F1 fan guide](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/lights-skyscrapers-and-midnight-magic-the-ultimate-fan-guide-to-singapore.2uxSgT4DurbA3306cddca8))
- **Mood:** "urban racing theatre" — concrete walls and aggressive kerbs in the
  foreground, a glittering megacity skyline and bay behind, hot/humid haze.
- **Signage/sponsors:** large illuminated trackside sponsor boards and gantry
  branding; building-mounted corporate logos on the CBD towers (banks: UOB, OCBC,
  Maybank, etc.) and the Marina Bay Sands lettering. Singapore GP branding and
  the start gantry over the pit straight.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific & concrete)

The current `js/tracks/singapore.js` is **already substantially developed** and
well past the brief's stale "24 generic towers + ion sky tree" description. It
already builds, by arc position: Marina Bay Sands (3 towers + boat skypark slab,
`s 0.18 R`), ArtScience lotus (5 petal cones, `s 0.22 R`), Supertrees (11,
`s 0.26 R`), Fullerton neoclassical block (`s 0.55 L`), Esplanade twin durian
domes (`s 0.66 L`), Helix Bridge arc (`s 0.80 R`), Singapore Flyer ferris wheel
(`s 0.86 R`, R=44 m / H=138 m), CBD hero towers, `cityFront` street canyons, a
timing tower, and Float-grandstands on the main straight. So the gaps are
**refinements and accuracy fixes**, not "it's all missing":

1. **Marina Bay Sands towers are straight, not fanned/curved.** The code uses
   three equal upright boxes spaced 36 m. Real Sands towers **lean apart from a
   narrow base and curve** — the splayed silhouette is the icon. The SkyPark slab
   is present but is a flat slab; the real one is a **boat hull that cantilevers
   well past the north tower**. Height in code (read the `H` constant) should land
   ~191–200 m to scale.
2. **ArtScience uses 5 petals; the real lotus has 10 "fingers."** And it should
   be **pure gleaming white** with a rounded central base, not cone-like.
3. **Supertrees count/placement.** Code places 11; real grove is 18 and they're a
   *distant across-the-bay* cluster, not roadside. They should sit further out
   laterally and glow in **shifting colours**, not static.
4. **Near-vs-far placement.** Sands/ArtScience/Helix/Supertrees are on the **east
   side across the water** — they should sit beyond a **bay water plane** at
   greater lateral distance and smaller apparent scale, with reflections, rather
   than as near street-canyon facades. Verify they're not pulled in too close.
5. **Singapore Flyer scale.** Code uses WHEEL_R 44 m (=88 m dia). Real diameter
   is **150 m**, total height 165 m. It currently reads roughly half-size; the
   wheel should be much larger relative to surrounding towers.
6. **Esplanade durian texture.** Two domes + 8 fins is a good start, but the
   signature is **dense spiky triangular cladding** (7,000+ panels) in
   **gold/bronze**, not smooth shells with a few ribs.
7. **Merlion is absent.** The white water-spouting Merlion at the Esplanade-Drive
   turn (near `s ~0.6`) is a famous TV landmark and is missing.
8. **Anderson Bridge not represented.** Turns 11–13 cross a **triple-arch steel
   bridge** over the river in front of the Fullerton — currently the Fullerton is
   there but the bridge structure / river crossing isn't called out.
9. **CBD skyline isn't tall/dense enough vs reality.** Real backdrop is a wall of
   **280 m** glass towers; check the hero-tower `H` reaches ~200–280 m and that
   there are enough of them to read as a continuous dense canyon behind the
   Padang/Anderson section.
10. **Float/NS Square is dated.** Code references "Float@Marina Bay grandstands"
    on the main straight; post-2023 The Float is demolished and the track no
    longer runs under a grandstand there. Either keep as period flavour or model
    open NS Square works — but don't run the track *under* it.
11. **Kerbs/surface character.** Ensure aggressive **red/white high kerbs** and a
    visibly **bumpy, used dark-tarmac** surface, with concrete walls + TecPro/tyre
    banks at run-offs — the street-circuit foreground.
12. **Bay water reflections.** The single biggest atmosphere lever: a dark bay
    plane reflecting the floodlit skyline/Sands. Confirm fountain/bay planes are
    present and read as mirror-like at night.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Reshape Marina Bay Sands:** lean the three towers outward from a narrow
   base (taper width with height, small splay angle), set H ≈ 191–200 m to scale,
   and make the SkyPark a **boat-hull slab that overhangs one end** with a warm
   underside light strip and a thin blue infinity-pool top edge.
2. **ArtScience → 10 white petals** around a rounded central drum, pure white
   low-roughness material so the floodlights catch it; place it tight beside Sands
   on the far (east) shore.
3. **Bay water plane first.** Put a dark, low-roughness **bay water plane**
   between the pit-straight / bay turns and the east-shore icons so Sands,
   ArtScience, Helix and Supertrees sit *across water* at greater lateral distance
   and slightly smaller scale, with skyline reflections at night.
4. **Scale up the Flyer:** wheel diameter ~150 m (R≈75 m), total height ~165 m,
   lit rim + 28 capsule blocks + single A-frame straddle support; keep it beside
   the pit/start area.
5. **Add the Merlion:** small white statue + spout + plinth near the
   Esplanade-Drive turn (~`s 0.6`, bay side), warm-uplit — cheap, iconic.
6. **Esplanade durian upgrade:** clad each dome in a denser field of small
   **gold/bronze triangular spikes** (many short angled prisms) rather than 8
   ribs; warm-lit glass beneath.
7. **Helix Bridge accents:** keep the double-helix tube but add the
   **red/green DNA letter lights** and stainless-steel silver material; arc it
   across the bay water near the Flyer/Sands.
8. **Anderson Bridge:** add a low **triple steel-arch bridge** deck the track
   crosses at Turns 11–13, with the grey-granite Fullerton + warm colonnade
   uplighting directly beside it.
9. **Denser, taller CBD canyon:** raise hero-tower heights toward **200–280 m**,
   add more of them in a continuous cool-grey reflective-glass wall behind the
   Padang/Anderson section, with antenna masts and a few lit corporate logos
   (bank-style signage).
10. **Supertrees → 18, colour-cycling, far.** Move the grove further out across
    the bay, increase count, and drive their canopy emissive through a slow
    colour cycle at night.
11. **Foreground street-circuit kit:** aggressive **red/white high kerbs**,
    concrete walls with debris fence, **TecPro/tyre** banks at run-offs, bumpy
    dark used tarmac with paint lines — the unforgiving Monaco-like wall
    proximity.
12. **Night atmosphere:** lean into >1,600-floodlight cool-white even lighting,
    floodlit reflective skyline, warm Sands deck + colour Helix/Supertrees, and
    moored superyacht silhouettes + neon signage reflected in the bay. Roadside
    **palms and planters** along the boulevards; the open green **Padang** lawn
    beside St Andrew's Road.
