# Yas Marina Circuit (Abu Dhabi) — visual reference

Research compiled for Apex 26 procedural scenery. The defining visual icon of this
circuit is the **W Abu Dhabi (formerly Yas Hotel / Yas Viceroy)** — a colour-shifting
LED grid-shell canopy that literally arches over the track between two hotel towers,
one inside the circuit and one in the marina. Second icon is **Ferrari World's** vast
red GT-curve roof. The race is F1's signature **twilight (day→night) race**.

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **The hotel spans the track.** The W Abu Dhabi is "the only hotel in the world that
  spans a Formula 1 racetrack." Two **12-storey** towers — one set *inside* the race
  circuit, one in the *marina* — linked by a **monocoque steel-and-glass bridge** and a
  **Grid Shell** that both cross the F1 track. The circuit literally "passes by the
  marina and under the W Abu Dhabi hotel."
  ([Wikipedia/UNIQ Hotels](https://www.uniqhotels.com/yas-viceroy-hotel-super-grand-prix-hotel/),
  [RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/united-arab-emirates/yas-marina.html))
- **Grid Shell dimensions & panels.** A **217 m** curvilinear glass-and-steel canopy —
  "the world's largest LED lighting system." Panel counts vary by source:
  **5,096 diamond-shaped glass panels** (most-cited), or **5,389 glass elements**, with
  **~5,800 LED luminaires** each containing 144 bulbs mounted at the lattice vertices.
  The diamond mesh evokes tessellated Islamic-architecture patterns.
  ([Architect Magazine](https://www.architectmagazine.com/project-gallery/yas-hotel-grid-shell),
  [parametric-architecture](https://parametric-architecture.com/yas-viceroy-hotel/),
  [Eaton](https://www.eaton.com/us/en-us/markets/success-stories/yas-hotel.html))
- **Colour-shifting light show.** Panels are **white by day**; at sunset the LEDs switch
  on and the facade **continuously changes colour**, acting "as a wraparound screen,
  turning the hotel into something of a lava lamp." Asymptote/Arup designed **seven
  digital lighting scripts** based on "ambient/ethereal" phenomena like slow-moving
  ocean waves; the system integrates a video feed with the pivoting colour-changing
  panes. Lighting design by **Rogier van der Heide (Arup Lighting)**.
  ([parametric-architecture](https://parametric-architecture.com/yas-viceroy-hotel/),
  [Rogier van der Heide](https://www.rogiervanderheide.com/lighting-design-for-the-w-hotel-yas-abu-dhabi/))
- **Architect & opening.** Designed by **Hani Rashid & Lise Anne Couture of Asymptote
  Architecture** (New York). Opened **November 2009** (with the inaugural Grand Prix).
  **499 rooms**, 7 restaurants.
  ([Dezeen](https://www.dezeen.com/2009/05/14/the-yas-hotel-by-asymptote/),
  [The National](https://www.thenationalnews.com/lifestyle/travel/yas-viceroy-the-latest-abu-dhabi-hotel-to-get-a-new-name-1.742625))
- **Rebrand history.** Opened as **Yas Hotel** (2009) → **Yas Viceroy** (2013, Viceroy
  Hotel Group) → **W Abu Dhabi – Yas Island** (rebranded under Marriott, **2018**).
  ([whatson.ae](https://whatson.ae/2018/06/viceroy-abu-dhabi-set-rebranded-w-hotel/),
  [gulfbusiness](https://gulfbusiness.com/marriott-takeover-management-abu-dhabis-yas-viceroy-hotel/))
- **Ferrari World.** Adjacent on Yas Island. A **red roof of ~200,000 m²** (largest
  roofed park in the world; total roof perimeter 2,200 m), shape directly inspired by
  the **double-curve side profile of a Ferrari GT** — "like a red sand dune peeling up
  from the landscape." Highest point **45 m**; structure length **~700 m**. Carries the
  **largest Ferrari logo ever made — 65 m × 48 m**.
  ([WikiArquitectura](https://en.wikiarquitectura.com/building/ferrari-world-abu-dhabi/))
- **The marina & superyachts.** Circuit wraps the marina on three sides. **227 serviced
  wet berths** for superyachts **8–90 m**, plus 93 dry berths. Like Monaco, **yachts moor
  right against the circuit**; select berths give true trackside viewing.
  ([Yachtway](https://yachtway.com/news/the-superyacht-insiders-guide-to-the-abu-dhabi-grand-prix/),
  [f1experiences](https://f1experiences.com/ja/blog/yas-marina-circuit-most-expensive-f1-circuit-ever-built))
- **Etihad branding.** Race title is the **Formula 1 Etihad Airways Abu Dhabi Grand
  Prix** (naming-rights sponsor). Etihad Park (concert venue) sits on the island.
  ([Etihad](https://www.etihad.com/en-us/abu-dhabi/formula-1/your-guide-to-yas-marina-circuit))
- **Yas Island terrain.** An **artificial, reclaimed island** built from desert/Gulf —
  "rough tracks over reclaimed sand and coral," now heavily developed and **flat**.
  Lined with **dark-green mangroves** and turquoise water channels; **white-sand**
  beaches. Yas Links golf reshapes reclaimed land into dunes, mangroves and water
  channels — but around the circuit the land is developed and flat, not dune sea.
  ([Yas Island/Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yas_Island),
  [Yas Beach](https://alike.io/blog/a-beach-day-at-yas-beach))

## Skyline & architecture / terrain

- **Flat, reclaimed, developed island** — no natural dune sea right at the circuit. The
  ground reads as engineered: tarmac, concrete aprons, landscaped verges, and water,
  not open desert. Sand where exposed is **pale/white**, not deep orange Sakhir sand.
- **Skyline is built, not natural:** the grid-shell hotel + twin towers dominate one
  side; **Ferrari World's red roof** is the other landmark mass; Yas Mall and other Yas
  Island resort blocks fill the mid-distance. Mangrove channels and the open Gulf form
  the horizon on the marina side.
- **Water is integral.** The marina basin sits *inside* the track loop; the hotel sits
  "half on land, half on water." Yachts moored trackside are a defining sight.
- Architecture language: **smooth, curvilinear, parametric** (Asymptote sweeps),
  **mashrabiya / Islamic tessellation motifs** (grid-shell diamonds; the new West
  Entrance paddock shade uses Arabic mashrabiya patterns), white tensile membranes.
  ([MakMax tensile structures](https://www.makmax.com/applications/sports/yas-marina-circuit/),
  [BDP Pattern](https://www.bdp.com/ca/news/bdp-pattern-delivers-second-phase-of-yas-marina-circuit-pit-and-paddock-expansion))

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Grandstands:** **five fully-covered** grandstands (Main, West, North, South, Marina)
  — curved **white/grey tensile fabric membrane** roofs. The Main Grandstand fronts the
  start/finish straight and pit building. One run-off area runs **beneath the West
  Grandstand**; pit-lane exit runs **under the track**.
  ([abudhabi-tickets](https://www.abudhabi-tickets.com/yas-marina-circuit/about/))
- **Surface:** **black, very smooth, low-degradation asphalt** made from **graywacke
  aggregate** shipped from Bayston Hill quarry, Shropshire, England. Prized for grip.
  ([Wikipedia via search](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yas_Marina_Circuit))
- **Kerbs:** standard FIA **red-and-white** alternating kerbs; aggressive **double-width
  kerbs** at some corners (50 mm followed by 75 mm). White edge lines.
- **Run-off:** **generous asphalt and concrete run-off** (modern Tilke design) — large
  paved aprons rather than gravel at most corners, giving a clean grey apron look around
  the track edges.
- **Pit & paddock building:** large modern block behind the pits; recent (2023+) West
  Entrance adds a dramatic **mashrabiya-patterned tensioned-fabric shade** structure.
- **Sponsor boards:** **Etihad Airways** title branding prominent on trackside boards
  and grandstand fascias; Abu Dhabi / Yas Island identity throughout.

## Vegetation & water

- **Landscaped date palms** line approach roads and verges — a managed, irrigated resort
  planting, not wild desert scrub. Trimmed green lawns/turf in places.
- **Dark-green mangroves** fringe Yas Island channels nearby (distinctive low dense
  green mass at the waterline).
- **Marina water:** calm, dark/turquoise; the basin sits within the circuit loop with
  **white-hulled superyachts** moored against the quays trackside.
- Beaches on the island are **white sand**; the Gulf horizon is flat and bright.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Twilight day→night race — the signature.** F1's **first day–night race** (since
  2009). Starts **~17:00 local (GST)** in daylight, runs ~2 hours, **finishes after
  sunset under floodlights**. The famous **sunset-to-night transition**: warm low sun →
  orange/pink dusk sky → deep blue/black night, with the LED hotel glow taking over.
  ([GP Explained](https://gpexplained.com/f1-night-races-explained/),
  [Abu Dhabi GP / The National](https://www.thenationalnews.com/sport/other-sport/day-night-gp-for-abu-dhabi-1.512155))
- **Floodlighting:** permanent system by **Musco Lighting** — described as the
  **largest permanent sports-venue lighting project in the world**. **Cool-white LED**
  light pools; lights on from the start so the day→night transition is seamless.
  ([Musco](https://www.musco.com/yas/))
- **Hotel LED glow** is the dominant artificial light source on the skyline at night —
  slow colour waves (blues, purples, magentas, ambers) reflected in the marina water.
- **Signage:** Etihad Airways (title sponsor), Abu Dhabi / Yas branding; FIA red/white
  kerbs and edge lines.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current build: *"Abu Dhabi (desert, night): Yas Viceroy Hotel twin towers (105m) with LED
gridshell arch; yacht marina; desert dune rings; floodlit hotels cityFront; Sakhir-like
tower."*

- **Grid-shell colour show missing.** Game has an "LED gridshell arch" but the icon is
  the **continuous colour-cycling** wraparound (blue→purple→magenta→amber slow waves)
  reflected in the water. If it's a static glow, it should animate/shift hue. This is the
  single most recognisable feature of the circuit — prioritise it.
- **Naming/era:** "Yas Viceroy" was the 2013–2018 name; current real name is **W Abu
  Dhabi**. Cosmetic but worth noting for any label.
- **Tower height likely overstated.** Towers are **12 storeys** (~85–105 m is the upper
  bound). Two curved towers flanking the arch is correct; keep them, but the arch+bridge
  geometry (canopy spanning *between* towers, *over* the track) matters more than height.
- **"Desert dune rings" is wrong for this island.** Yas is **flat, reclaimed, developed**
  land — pale sand, mangroves, water, resort landscaping. Concentric desert dunes read as
  Sakhir/Jeddah, not Abu Dhabi. Replace with **flat ground + marina water + mangrove
  green + pale sand fringe**.
- **Sand colour:** should be **pale/white**, not deep orange Sakhir sodium-sand tone.
- **Ferrari World absent.** The **giant red double-curve roof** with the huge Ferrari
  logo is a top-three landmark on the skyline and currently not represented.
- **Marina is present (good)** but could be enhanced with **white superyachts moored
  trackside** against the quay, and the hotel sitting half-over-water.
- **Generic "Sakhir-like tower"** — Yas Marina's identity is the grid-shell + Ferrari
  World, not a Sakhir-style spire. Consider swapping for the grandstand membrane canopies
  or de-emphasising.
- **Floodlight tone:** should be **cool-white LED** (Musco), matching the CLAUDE.md
  "street/modern = cool LED white" — confirm it's not warm sodium here.
- **Twilight/dusk option:** the real race *starts in daylight and ends at night*. The
  signature look is **dusk** (orange/pink sky, low warm sun, hotel just lighting up), not
  only full night. A dusk variant would be very on-brand.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Animate the grid-shell LED.** Drive the hotel arch/facade emissive through a slow
   hue cycle (e.g. period ~10–20 s sweeping blue→purple→magenta→amber). White/neutral by
   day, saturated colour at dusk/night. This is the circuit's #1 signature.
2. **Geometry: arch over the track between two towers.** Keep two curved 12-storey
   towers (one circuit-side, one marina-side) joined by a **diamond-mesh canopy + bridge
   that spans across the racing surface** near the marina/pit-exit area. Diamond lattice
   = the readable texture.
3. **Add Ferrari World.** A large, low **red double-curve "dune" roof** (peak ~45 m,
   long ~700 m footprint, ground-hugging) with a **central funnel** and a big circular
   logo motif, placed on the skyline NE of the circuit. Ferrari red is the cue.
4. **Replace desert-dune rings with flat reclaimed island.** Flat ground plane; **pale
   white sand** fringe; **dark-green mangrove** strips at the waterline; landscaped
   **date palms** along verges; marina **water basin inside the loop**.
5. **Moor white superyachts** along the marina quay trackside (white hulls, dark water),
   with the hotel reading as sitting half over the water + the water reflecting the LED
   colour wash at night.
6. **Grandstands as white/grey tensile membrane canopies** (curved fabric roofs), five
   of them, fully covered — distinct from boxy concrete stands.
7. **Surface & kerbs:** very smooth **black asphalt**, FIA **red/white** kerbs (some
   double-width), white edge lines, **large grey asphalt/concrete run-off aprons** at
   corners (not gravel).
8. **Lighting:** **cool-white LED** floodlights on slim masts ringing the circuit;
   support a **dusk/twilight palette** (low warm sun, orange-pink sky fading to deep
   blue) as well as full night, to capture the day→night transition.
9. **Signage:** Etihad Airways trackside boards / grandstand fascia branding; Yas / Abu
   Dhabi identity. Mashrabiya/Islamic-tessellation motif for paddock shade structures.
