# Shanghai — visual reference

Visual research for procedural 3D scenery of the **Shanghai International Circuit**
(Shanghai International Circuit, Anting town, Jiading District, Shanghai, China —
host of the Chinese Grand Prix). Designed by Hermann Tilke, opened 2004.

> **HEADLINE CORRECTION:** The circuit is in **suburban Jiading / Anting, ~30 km
> from Shanghai's city centre and roughly an hour's drive from Pudong**. The
> **Pudong/Lujiazui skyline — Oriental Pearl Tower, Jin Mao, Shanghai World
> Financial Center, Shanghai Tower — is NOT visible from the circuit**, nor is the
> Huangpu River. The circuit sits on flat reclaimed marshland surrounded by the
> auto-industry zone and low-rise suburb. The game's current "Oriental Pearl Tower
> + Pudong CBD + Huangpu water" model is **geographically wrong** and should be
> removed. See Gap analysis.

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Location: Anting town, Jiading District, ~30 km (18.6 mi) NW of the city
  centre.** "located in the northeast of Anting Town, Jiading District, 30
  kilometers (18.6 miles) from the city center, and Pudong is approximately an
  hour's drive from the circuit."
  (https://www.travelchinaguide.com/attraction/shanghai/international-circuit.htm)
  This alone confirms the Pudong skyline is far out of sight.
- **Built on reclaimed swamp / rice-paddy marshland.** "The site was actually a
  swampland, previously used as rice paddy fields, and extensive groundworks had
  to be completed to construct the circuit… for 18 months some 3,000 workers were
  on site daily."
  (https://motorsportguides.com/shanghai-international-circuit/) Cost ~$450–500 M.
- **Marshland foundation → ongoing subsidence.** "Subsidence is normal because of
  the soft soil in Shanghai… areas around turns 1, 8 and 14 have been sinking";
  the circuit was stabilised by driving thousands of concrete piles / steel beams
  into the soft waterlogged soil.
  (https://www.crash.net/f1/news/166103/1/shanghai-international-works-to-cure-sinking-feeling)
- **5.3 km² mixed-use complex, not just a track.** "Covering an area of 5.3 square
  kilometers (1,310 acres), it comprises the circuit area itself, a business
  exhibition area with shopping mall and racing car exhibition hall, leisure zone
  including fitness facilities, cinema, pub, and hotel, and an area reserved for
  future development."
  (https://www.racingcircuits.info/asia/china/shanghai-international-circuit.html)
- **Surrounded by the auto-industry zone (Shanghai International Automobile City /
  Anting), flat suburban land** — not a dense CBD. Anting is China's "auto city"
  (Volkswagen-Shanghai plants, auto expo). (search corroboration across
  travelchinaguide, racingcircuits, motorsportguides above)
- **Layout traces the Chinese character "上" (shàng = "above/ascend").** "the track
  is based on the Chinese 'Shang' character (meaning 'above')."
  (https://motorsportguides.com/shanghai-international-circuit/)

## Skyline & architecture

- **There is no city skyline behind this circuit.** Horizon is **flat, low-rise
  suburban/industrial** (auto-industry warehouses, the Automobile City exhibition
  buildings, low apartment blocks, sheds, trees, electricity pylons). The site is
  reclaimed flat marshland — no hills, no high-rise cluster.
- The **only tall/iconic verticals are the circuit's own structures**: the
  cantilever-roofed main grandstand, the two winged overhead platforms ("Moon
  Gate") spanning the track, the control/media tower over the pits, and the
  floodlight masts. These read as **modern steel-grey + white + glass**, with the
  Chinese-garden pavilion idiom at the lake.
- Architectural language (Tilke): "combines the grandeur and tradition-
  consciousness of China with… modernity" — i.e. modern aluminium/glass/steel
  forms with curved, garden-pavilion and lotus references, **not** a generic glass
  CBD. (https://www.re-thinkingthefuture.com/articles/shanghai-international-circuit/)
- **DO NOT render Oriental Pearl Tower / Jin Mao / Shanghai Tower / a dense
  Lujiazui glass cluster** — they are ~30+ km away across the Huangpu in Pudong
  and are not visible from Jiading.

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Kerbs:** standard FIA red-and-white serrated kerbs at apexes/exits; flat blue-
  and-white or red-and-white run-off kerbs and "sausage" kerbs at the chicane/
  esses. (Generic modern-Tilke kerbing — no exotic colour scheme reported.)
- **Run-off:** very generous **asphalt run-off** (paved tarmac aprons) typical of a
  Tilke circuit — large grey paved areas outside kerbs, especially Turn 1's long
  decreasing-radius right and the Turn 14 hairpin, with gravel traps and tyre
  barriers beyond. Turns 1, 8 and 14 are the known subsidence/repaved zones.
  (https://www.crash.net/f1/news/166103/1/shanghai-international-works-to-cure-sinking-feeling)
- **Grandstands:** main grandstand is steel-grey/white with the dramatic cantilever
  roof and glass frontage; outer grandstands wear the **26 white lotus-umbrella
  membrane canopies**. Seating banks are large (total venue capacity up to
  ~200,000: ~50,000 covered permanent + ~150,000 temporary).
  (https://www.re-thinkingthefuture.com/articles/shanghai-international-circuit/)
- **Barriers/walls:** white concrete walls + steel Armco with **debris fencing**
  (catch-fence) on poles around the spectator areas; tyre-stack barriers (banded
  red/white) at high-impact corners. Pit wall along the main straight.
- **The long back straight:** the ~**1.2 km straight between Turn 13 and Turn 14**
  (one of the longest in F1, cars reaching ~270–340 km/h) ending in the heavy-
  braking Turn 14 hairpin — a flat, open, fast section flanked by paved run-off
  and low grandstands/advertising hoardings.
  (search: formula1.com circuit guide; racingnews365)

## Vegetation & water

- **Artificial lake(s) in the paddock** are the circuit's signature water feature —
  the **team pavilions sit in/around a lake** evoking Shanghai's Yu Garden. This is
  the *only* legitimate water to render, and it is **inside the venue (paddock/
  infield), not a river behind the city.**
  (https://www.racingcircuits.info/asia/china/shanghai-international-circuit.html)
- Built on former **swamp/rice-paddy marshland** — surrounding land is flat, with
  **drainage ditches, ponds and reclaimed wetland**; landscaped greenery, lawns and
  young/medium **broadleaf trees** planted across the infield and approaches
  (typical Tilke landscaped berms and tree lines).
- Vegetation idiom: **temperate/subtropical broadleaf** (planes/poplars/willows
  near water, ornamental shrubs, mown grass banks), not palms and not arid. Lush
  green in the wet spring race window.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Climate: humid subtropical; race held late March / April.** "April features
  mild and cozy temperature with rain falling a bit frequently… precipitation… 130
  mm on average in April, with around 15 rainy days… **Humidity averages 69% in
  April**." Highs ~20–27 °C. Frequently **overcast / hazy with drizzle**, mixed
  cloud and sun.
  (https://www.travelchinaguide.com/cityguides/shanghai/weather-april.htm)
  → Lighting target: **soft, diffuse, slightly hazy overcast** (low contrast, pale
  cool-grey sky, damp tarmac sheen), with sunny-but-humid as the bright variant.
  Avoid hard, clear, deep-blue desert skies as the default.
- **Terrain: dead flat.** No elevation change of note (reclaimed marshland) — keep
  the ground plane essentially level.
- **National identity / signage:** **Chinese red** is the dominant accent;
  bilingual **Chinese-character + English signage** (中国大奖赛 / "Chinese Grand
  Prix"), red corner-marker boards, sponsor hoardings (e.g. **Heineken**, **Rolex**,
  **DHL**, **Pirelli**, **MSC/Emirates** depending on year, Chinese brands). Big
  trackside LED ribbon boards along the main straight and back straight. Plenty of
  **red/white** banding and Chinese flags.
- **Floodlights:** modern cool-white LED masts ring the circuit (street/modern
  theme) — Shanghai has run at twilight/under-lights for support events; treat as
  cool LED white at night.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current game (modern theme) builds for Shanghai: a **CBD skyline cityFront with
tall 50–150 m towers, Oriental Pearl Tower prominence, Huangpu water, tree-lined
streets.** Problems:

1. **WRONG — Oriental Pearl Tower & Pudong skyline.** The Pearl Tower / Lujiazui
   cluster is **~30+ km away in Pudong and not visible** from Jiading. **Remove the
   Pearl Tower landmark and the dense CBD skyline entirely.** This is the single
   biggest inaccuracy. The circuit's skyline is its **own** grandstand + winged
   overhead structures + control tower, against a **flat low-rise suburban/auto-
   industry horizon.**
2. **WRONG — Huangpu River water.** The Huangpu is in central Shanghai, not here.
   The water that exists is the **artificial paddock lake with team pavilions.**
   Replace "Huangpu river-front" with an **infield/paddock lake** feature.
3. **WRONG — tall 50–150 m glass towers as the backdrop.** Replace with **low-rise
   suburban + industrial (auto city) sheds/warehouses/expo halls and mid-rise
   apartment blocks**, plus electricity pylons and tree lines. Keep heights modest
   (mostly <30 m) so nothing competes with the grandstand.
4. **MISSING — the circuit's signature architecture.** No representation of the
   **cantilever-roof main grandstand**, the **two winged "Moon Gate" overhead
   platforms** crossing the start/finish, the **26 white lotus-umbrella membrane
   canopies**, or the **garden-pavilion team buildings on the lake.** These are the
   visual identity of Shanghai and should be the hero props.
5. **MISSING — flat marshland terrain + generous asphalt run-off.** Ensure the
   terrain is flat and run-off aprons are large paved grey areas.
6. **ATMOSPHERE — likely too clear/sunny.** Bias the default toward **humid
   overcast / hazy spring** (pale cool-grey sky, low contrast, damp sheen), not a
   clear saturated sky.
7. **VEGETATION — "tree-lined streets" is partly right but wrong species feel.**
   Use **temperate broadleaf** trees, lawns and waterside planting, not palms; add
   reclaimed-wetland ponds/ditches.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

For `js/tracks/shanghai*.js` `scenery(api)` and the `STYLES`/`FURN`/`BARRIER`
entries in `js/tracks.js` (city/furniture generators), plus theme/atmo in
`game.js`:

1. **Delete the Oriental Pearl Tower + Pudong CBD skyline + Huangpu river** for
   Shanghai. Remove any `cityFront` tall-tower band tagged to this track.
2. **Hero structures (build as track props):**
   - **Main grandstand** along the front straight: long, low, multi-tier seating
     bank with a single huge **cantilever roof** sweeping out over the seats
     (steel-grey/white, glass front). ~40 m cantilever, no support columns at the
     front edge.
   - **Two winged overhead "Moon Gate" platforms** spanning the track at each end
     of the pit straight — a curved/ovoid white-steel bridge deck over the racing
     surface (do NOT call `blockAt`/`markBarrier` on the span, it's above the
     road). These are the iconic gateway look.
   - **Control/media tower** rising from the pit building mid-straight.
   - **Lotus-umbrella canopy field:** a row of ~20+ white **tensile umbrella
     canopies** over the outer grandstands (cluster of pale fabric "umbrella"
     props).
   - **Team pavilion buildings on a lake** in the infield/paddock: a small cluster
     of low Chinese-garden pavilions (light stone, glass, dark curved roofs) sitting
     in/around a **water polygon**, linked by walkways.
3. **Backdrop city:** swap to **suburban/industrial Jiading**: low-rise
   warehouses/expo halls, sheds, mid-rise apartment slabs (mostly 10–30 m),
   electricity pylons, occasional construction crane — flat skyline, nothing taller
   than the grandstand. Cool, muted facade palette (grey concrete, pale render,
   some VW/auto-industry blue/silver), with **Chinese red** accent signage.
4. **Water:** one **infield/paddock lake** polygon (calm, low-roughness reflective)
   beside the team pavilions; plus small reclaimed-wetland ponds/ditches near the
   verges. No river.
5. **Vegetation (`FURN`):** **broadleaf** trees (`broad`) with green spring foliage,
   willows/poplars by the water, mown grass banks; low ornamental shrubs. No palms,
   no autumn/arid jitter.
6. **Barriers/kerbs (`BARRIER`):** white concrete walls + Armco + tall debris
   catch-fencing; red/white tyre stacks at Turn 1 and the Turn 14 hairpin; FIA
   red-and-white apex kerbs, large **paved grey asphalt run-off** aprons.
7. **Signage:** **Chinese red** as primary accent; bilingual Chinese-character +
   English boards; trackside LED ribbon boards on the main and back straights;
   Heineken/Rolex/DHL/Pirelli-style hoardings and Chinese flags.
8. **Atmosphere (`_trackAtmoBias` / time-of-day):** bias toward **overcast/humid
   haze** — pale cool-grey low-saturation sky, soft diffuse light, low contrast,
   damp-tarmac sheen, low haze; flat terrain (no elevation bias). Provide a
   "hazy-bright humid" variant rather than a clear deep-blue default.
