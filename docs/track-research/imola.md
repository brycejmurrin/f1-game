# Imola (Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari) — visual reference

Research target: **Autodromo Internazionale Enzo e Dino Ferrari**, Imola, Emilia-Romagna,
Italy. ~4.909 km, run **anti-clockwise**, ~40 km east of Bologna. A semi-permanent road
course threaded along the **Santerno river** at the foot of the Apennine foothills, wrapping
the wooded **Parco delle Acque Minerali**. Day race, spring (mid/late May). Heavy Ferrari /
Italian-tricolour identity. Iconic for the **Ayrton Senna memorial** at Tamburello.

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Santerno river runs right alongside the circuit.** The track "meanders through the
  picturesque Santerno River valley" and was "built as a semi-permanent road course along
  the Santerno River and at the foot of the Apennine hills." At **Tamburello** the river is
  separated from the track only by a **concrete wall with no tyre barrier** — this is where
  Senna's car speared into the wall in 1994. The river sits on the **outside of Tamburello /
  the pit-straight side of the loop** (the fast left-hander "runs alongside the river").
  Sources: [Wikipedia: Imola Circuit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imola_Circuit),
  [The Playoffs — Tamburello explained](https://theplayoffs.news/en/what-is-tamburello-corner-at-the-imola-circuit-2025-emilia-romagna-grand-prix-circuit-explained/),
  [RacingCircuits.info — Imola](https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/italy/imola.html).
- **Parco delle Acque Minerali — the wooded park the circuit encircles.** "The historic
  green lung of Imola … completely surrounded by the Autodromo Enzo e Dino Ferrari." ~11
  hectares, "rich collection of tree species, native and exotic": **cedars, pines, ginkgo,
  liquidambar, koelreuteria, and holm oaks**, "some of which have already reached considerable
  sizes." The pedestrian park path "is parallel to the track and has the same shape as the
  original circuit," with "an unpaved road between a river and the circuit," "immersed in the
  greenery of the park along the Santerno." Source:
  [Parco Acque Minerali (search summary, visitareimola.it / comune.imola)](https://visitareimola.it/eng/living/free-time/parks-and-reserves/parco-acque-minerali/),
  [F1 Imola — Acque Minerali ticket pages](https://www.imolaf1.com/en/ticket-info/acque-minerali-1).
- **Wooded hill inside the loop: Monte Castellaccio.** Inside the park is "a wooded hill
  called Monte Castellaccio" (site of prehistoric settlement), "covered with a dense forest of
  **downy oaks and hornbeams**" and "bordered at its base by the Giandolino stream." So the
  interior of the circuit is a forested rise, not flat infield. Source: park search summary
  (above).
- **The town of Imola is a historic stone/terracotta Emilia-Romagna town.** Dominated by the
  14th-century **Rocca Sforzesca** fortress ("towers, drawbridges and stone courtyards");
  from its keep the view is "the Imola hills and the historic city, punctuated by the profile
  of the **cathedral and church bell towers**." Sources:
  [Bologna Welcome — Rocca Sforzesca](https://www.bolognawelcome.com/en/places/towers-historic-buildings/rocca-sforzesca-di-imola-eng),
  [Wikipedia: Imola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imola).
- **Spring day race, "ideal" Italian light.** Imola in May: lows ~13°C, highs ~24°C; the
  2025 race day was "completely clear from early hours … anticyclonic pressure," sunny.
  Source:
  [Formula1.com weather](https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/what-is-the-weather-forecast-for-the-2025-emilia-romagna-grand-prix.1Jv8wWRNSE4erxEItNUxay),
  [Motorsinside — ideal conditions](https://www.motorsinside.com/en/f1/news/36585-emilia-romagna-gp-weather-ideal-conditions-imola-kick-off-european-triple-header.html).

## Terrain & vegetation

- **River-valley basin ringed by wooded foothills.** The circuit sits in the Santerno valley
  at the foot of the Apennines; **wooded ridges/hills surround it** and the town hills rise
  beyond. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imola_Circuit))
- **~30–40 m total elevation change per lap.** "Total drop of approximately 30 metres across
  the lap, most notably in the descent to Rivazza." Tosa is uphill, Piratella/Variante Alta
  sit on the high ground, Acque Minerali dips then climbs, then a long descent into Rivazza
  (the low point). ([RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/europe/italy/imola.html))
- **Genuinely tree-lined, park character — this is the defining look.** The Acque Minerali
  section is famously enclosed by **mature, tall trees** that screen the track from the park.
  Species to evoke: **cedars, pines, holm oaks (evergreen, dark), downy oaks & hornbeams
  (deciduous, mid-green), ginkgo, liquidambar** (autumn-tinged accents acceptable). Temperate
  Mediterranean mixed woodland — dense, mature, varied heights, NOT sparse scrub.
- **Monte Castellaccio** = a forested hill **inside** the circuit loop (downy oak + hornbeam),
  so the infield reads as a green wooded rise, not open tarmac.
- **Streams**: the Santerno (main river, outside Tamburello) and the small **Giandolino**
  stream at the base of Monte Castellaccio.

## Grandstands, buildings, kerbs, surface, banking

- **Pit / start-finish straight** is short, lined by **Partenza (Start) grandstands 1–4** plus
  the modern pit complex (current GP layout since 2008; pit buildings rebuilt over the 1980s–
  2000s). Older cantilever pit-roof character survives in places.
  ([ImolaF1 grandstand map](https://www.imolaf1.com/en/map-of-the-grandstands),
  [grandprixgrandtours](https://www.grandprixgrandtours.com/imola-circuit-guide/))
- **Named grandstands**: Start/Partenza 1–4 (main straight), **Villeneuve** (inside, exit of
  Tamburello chicane), **Tosa** (T7), **Acque Minerali 1/3** (elevated, over the curve),
  **Rivazza (Grandstand R)** — fan favourite. ([motorsporttickets guide](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/imola-grandstand-guide-where-to-sit-at-autodromo-di-enzo-e-dino-ferrari/))
- **Kerbs**: "aggressive kerbs … narrow margins that punish mistakes"; classic **red/white**
  serrated kerbs, lowered at the chicanes in 2006. Sausage/exit kerbs at chicanes.
  ([Tracing Insights](https://tracinginsights.com/blog/321894/all-you-need-to-know-2025-emilia-romagna-grand-prix/))
- **Run-off**: a mix of **gravel traps** (Tamburello/Villeneuve outside, Tosa, Rivazza) and
  grass banks — Imola retains more gravel than most modern tracks.
- **Banking**: essentially flat road course (no big banked turns); the drama is gradient, not
  banking. Spectator banking (earth terraces) at Tosa.
- **Surface**: dark asphalt; the layout is tight and old-school, with walls close.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Daytime spring race**, clear "ideal" Italian light, warm low-haze; lush green parkland at
  peak foliage in May. Long shadows from low morning sun are plausible; midday is bright but
  the tree canopy throws strong dappled shade over the park sections.
- **Italian national identity is strong**: green-white-red **tricolour**, and above all a
  **heavy Ferrari / Tifosi red** presence (flags, banners, grandstand crowds). Sponsor/
  signage palette skews red, with national-colour kerbing and barriers.
- **Town backdrop**: warm **terracotta roofs and pale stone**, the Rocca fortress and
  **cathedral/church bell towers** on the hillside skyline.

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current `js/tracks/imola.js` is already rich (theme `green`, encircling wooded hills, section
forestEdges, Santerno river planes, a "campanile," an Italian hillside town with church +
tower). Gaps:

1. **Senna memorial is a generic flat box, not the seated bronze statue.** Lines 175–178 place
   a `3×2.5×0.4` white-grey box at `K(0.07)` labelled "Senna memorial." The real icon is a
   **2 m seated bronze figure on a marble plinth** in a tree clearing. This is the single most
   recognisable landmark at Imola and is effectively absent.
2. **No Ratzenberger memorial.** Real one sits at **Tosa** (T7); currently nothing there.
3. **Campanile placement is generic, not town-anchored.** The standalone "Imola campanile"
   at `K(0.64)` (lines 148–161) floats above the Variante Alta treeline. Imola's real towers
   are the **Rocca Sforzesca** and **cathedral/church bell towers** clustered in the town on
   the hill — better read as part of the distant town silhouette than a lone tower at a corner.
   (The hillside-town block at lines 249–280 already builds a church + campanile near T1 — that
   is the more accurate home for a bell tower; the lone `K(0.64)` tower is redundant/misplaced.)
4. **River is modelled as flat ground-plane "basins," not a continuous channel hugging
   Tamburello.** Real Santerno is a defined river right behind the **concrete wall at
   Tamburello** with vegetated banks. Current planes are at `K(0.00/0.08/0.15)` and `K(0.92)`
   but don't read as a riverbank against the wall, and there's no tree-lined bank.
5. **Tree species/character is uniform dark conifer-heavy forest.** Real Acque Minerali park is
   **mixed mature broadleaf + evergreen** (cedars, holm oak, downy oak, hornbeam, ginkgo,
   liquidambar) with tall, varied, manicured "park" trees — not a wild pine ridge. `pineFrac`
   is 0.45–0.65 across most sections; should lean broadleaf in the park.
6. **Infield (Monte Castellaccio) not represented as a wooded hill** inside the loop.
7. **Tifosi/Ferrari-red identity is under-played.** Some red billboards/kerbs exist, but the
   overwhelming red crowd + flag character isn't emphasised (crowd colours are muted).
