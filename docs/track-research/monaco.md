## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- The circuit is a **3.337 km (2.074 mi) street circuit** laid out on the public
  streets of Monte-Carlo and La Condamine, wrapping around the harbour
  (**Port Hercule**) of the Principality. It is the slowest, tightest, and most
  enclosed track on the calendar — "narrow, bumpy and bordered almost entirely by
  barriers," with the Armco "inches from the racing line for almost the entire
  3.337 kilometres." (https://www.gpfans.com/us/f1-news/1050518/, https://www.total-motorsport.com/monaco-gp-corner-names-circuit-de-monaco/)
- Corner sequence and what borders each: **Sainte Devote (T1)** — sharp uphill
  right after a short sprint up Boulevard Albert Ier, with the small **Sainte-
  Dévote Chapel** "just beyond the barriers"; then the steep climb up **Beau
  Rivage** to **Casino Square**; **Mirabeau** (high/low, "Haute"/"Bas"); the
  **Fairmont (Grand Hotel) Hairpin**, "the slowest corner in Formula 1," unchanged
  since 1929; downhill to **Portier** (double right-hander); the **Tunnel**; the
  harbour-side **Nouvelle Chicane**; **Tabac**; the **Swimming Pool** complex
  (Louis Chiron / La Piscine) around the **Rainier III Nautical Stadium**;
  **La Rascasse**; and **Anthony Noghès** onto the start/finish.
  (https://www.formula1.com/en/latest/article/explained-how-every-monaco-corner-got-its-name.5ClYqWmfpeUpsRJA6mDNaX,
  https://www.f1oversteer.com/features/how-every-corner-at-the-monaco-grand-prix-got-its-name-including-sainte-devote-and-rascasse/)
- **Casino Square**: "The famous Monte-Carlo Casino stands on one side of the
  square, flanked by the Hôtel de Paris to its left and the Café de Paris on its
  right." Probably "the most expensive patch of land in sport."
  (https://www.monacograndprixticket.com/formula1monaco/the-circuit-of-monaco)
- **The Tunnel** is a unique F1 feature: the track "dives under the Fairmont Hotel
  into a ~500-metre curved section where cars reach approximately 280 km/h in
  near-darkness before exploding back into Mediterranean sunlight," past the
  **Yacht Club de Monaco** on exit. Cars lose ~20–30% downforce inside.
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circuit_de_Monaco — via search synthesis;
  https://f1chronicle.com/circuit-de-monaco-technical-guide-every-corner-of-f1s-tightest-track-explained/)
- **Port Hercule** during the GP is "one of the most impressive floating shows of
  large yachts in the world." In 2025, **115 yachts above 24 m** were docked in
  Port Hercule (incl. the 90 m Benetti *Lionheart*); for 2026, **106 yachts
  berthed in the port** plus ~180 more anchored offshore. The most sought-after
  berths "overlook sections of the circuit such as Tabac, the Swimming Pool
  complex and the harbour straight."
  (https://www.boatinternational.com/yachts/news/monaco-grand-prix-yachts-2026,
  https://www.yachtcharterfleet.com/news/monaco-grand-prix-2026-largest-charter-yachts-berthed-in-port-hercule-for-race-weekend)
- **Le Rocher (The Rock)** / **Monaco-Ville**: the historic old town "dramatically
  perched on a high promontory overlooking the Mediterranean," home to the
  **Prince's Palace** (a fortress dating to 1191, Grimaldi seat since 1297). It
  overlooks La Condamine and the main harbour from the **west** side of Port
  Hercule. (https://frenchmoments.eu/rocher-de-monaco/,
  https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince's_Palace_of_Monaco — via search synthesis)

## Skyline & architecture

- Monte-Carlo's character is **Belle Époque (1871–1914)**: "sumptuous villas and
  buildings divided into apartments and penthouses." Hallmark "coloured turrets,
  terraces and caryatids are distinct throughout Monte Carlo." Cream/pale stone,
  ornate cornices, mansard and green-copper roofs.
  (https://www.lxcollection.com/article/la-belle-epoque-penthouse-monaco-architecture/,
  https://www.britannica.com/topic/Casino-de-Monte-Carlo — via search synthesis)
- The principality climbs **steep hills from a narrow coastline**, so apartment
  blocks are **tiered up the hillside** behind the waterfront — dense, stacked,
  layered terraces. "The narrow coastline and steep hills have inspired the
  varying architectural styles."
  (https://blog.lacosta-properties-monaco.com/monaco-architecture-and-famous-buildings-through-the-ages/)
- Modern counterpoint: the **Tour Odéon** "redefined Monaco's skyline … rising
  prominently above the La Rousse–Saint-Roman district," Monaco's tallest
  residential tower (~170 m). A handful of modern glass towers punctuate an
  otherwise low-to-mid-rise cream Belle Époque mass.
  (https://www.lacosta-properties-monaco.com/en/monaco-buildings/belle-epoque-monaco/)
- Net palette: **cream / pale stone dominant**, terracotta and pink accents,
  **green copper roofs**, glass on modern towers, all stacked up green-and-grey
  hillsides. The harbour edge is a wall of waterfront apartment facades.

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Barriers**: steel **Armco everywhere**, sitting inches from the line for
  almost the whole lap. Run-off is essentially nil — walls, not gravel/grass.
  (https://www.total-motorsport.com/monaco-grand-prix-track-guide/)
- **Kerbs**: classic **red-and-white**. "The apexes at Monaco are tinged in
  vibrant white and red." Several kerbs are **permanent year-round** — notably the
  red-and-white apex at the **Fairmont hairpin** "never removed," unlike temporary
  kerbs elsewhere. The Swimming Pool chicanes have aggressive kerbs drivers attack
  with barriers tight on both sides.
  (https://monacolife.net/formula-one-walking-the-monaco-grand-prix-circuit/,
  https://www.total-motorsport.com/monaco-grand-prix-track-guide/)
- **Surface**: smooth Monte-Carlo public-road asphalt — "bumpy and offers minimal
  grip, particularly in warmer weather." Low-grip, undulating, with painted road
  markings and manhole covers visible.
  (https://www.total-motorsport.com/monaco-grand-prix-track-guide/)
- **Pit building**: semi-permanent **two-storey** pit structures (rebuilt 2004).
  Monaco is unique — the pits are **not facing the track**; the pit structure
  physically separates pit lane from the circuit.
  (https://monacolife.net/formula-one-walking-the-monaco-grand-prix-circuit/)
- **Grandstands**: temporary stands at Sainte-Dévote (A1), the harbour front,
  Tabac and the Swimming Pool; plus the famous **terraces, balconies and yacht
  decks** that double as viewing platforms. Tubular scaffold stands with covered
  roofs. (https://www.f1monaco.com/en/map-of-the-grandstands-29)

## Vegetation & water

- **Casino Square / Jardins des Casino**: formal gardens — clipped hedges,
  manicured lawns, **palm trees**, and a fountain (a new fountain installed on the
  Place du Casino in 1992). The Hermitage front is shaded by **century-old ficus
  trees**.
  (https://www.montecarlosbm.com/en/inspiration/hotel-hermitage-monte-carlo-hotel-connaisseurs)
- **Palms** line the harbour promenade and the boulevards — classic Mediterranean
  Riviera planting. Hillsides above the buildings carry **green/grey rocky scrub
  and pine/olive** vegetation.
- **Water**: **Port Hercule** is the central water feature — a rectangular harbour
  packed with **superyachts** (white hulls, dark glass, radar masts), with the
  open **Mediterranean** beyond a breakwater. The track runs right along the quay
  on the harbour side (Nouvelle Chicane → Tabac → Swimming Pool → Rascasse). The
  **Rainier III pool** is a distinct blue rectangular basin trackside at the
  Swimming Pool section.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Light**: bright Mediterranean sun, deep-blue sea, late-May low-humidity
  clarity. The defining lighting moment is the **tunnel dark→light transition** —
  cars plunge into shade then "explode back into Mediterranean sunlight" past the
  Yacht Club. Long shadows from the hillside; warm cream stone glowing against a
  saturated blue sky and sea. (Day race; no night running.)
- **National identity**: Monaco flag is **red over white** (horizontal bicolour,
  identical layout to Indonesia). Red/white motifs recur in kerbs, bunting, and
  Automobile Club de Monaco (ACM) branding. Grimaldi heraldry (red-and-white
  lozenges/diamonds) is the principality's signature pattern — a strong, authentic
  accent for armco livery and banners.
- **Signage**: ACM and F1 sponsor boards line the Armco; harbour-front and Casino
  Square carry luxury-brand hoardings. The start/finish straight runs along the
  harbour quay with the pit structure on the inland side.
