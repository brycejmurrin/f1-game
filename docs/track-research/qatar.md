# Lusail International Circuit (Qatar) — visual reference

Research for Apex 26 procedural scenery. Track: Lusail International Circuit
(a.k.a. Losail), Lusail, Qatar — the night-race desert venue ~20–30 km north of
Doha. Designed/upgraded by Tilke; F1 since 2021, fully remodelled for the 2023
Qatar GP. Key visual signature: **flat barren desert + the single largest
floodlit motorsport venue in the world** (raced entirely at night).

---

## Real-world surroundings (cited facts with URLs)

- **Location: isolated desert north of Doha.** The circuit sits "in the Qatari
  Desert north of Qatar's capital, Doha," just outside Lusail city, ~19–30 km
  from central Doha (sources vary; commonly cited "30 km from central Doha,"
  ~20 km north of West Bay). Flat sandy desert, barren and isolated — typical
  Arabian Peninsula plain, not dunes.
  ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit),
  [Rome2Rio](https://www.rome2rio.com/s/Doha/Lusail-International-Circuit),
  [worldinsport](https://worldinsport.com/qatar-grand-prix-the-desert-challenge-in-doha/))
- **Artificial grass border to hold back the sand.** "The track itself is
  surrounded by artificial grass designed to prevent sand from the neighbouring
  desert from blowing onto the circuit." A defining, distinctive feature — a
  green band of fake turf ringing the asphalt against pale desert.
  ([RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/qatar/lusail-international-circuit.html),
  [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit))
- **Night racing since 2008.** Permanent floodlighting installed 2007 (by Musco
  Lighting); the 2008 Qatar MotoGP was the first-ever MotoGP night race. F1 runs
  late afternoon into evening / full night to dodge desert heat.
  ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit),
  [Travel And Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/get-ready-for-the-2026-motogp-in-qatar-lusail-circuit-lights-up-the-desert-night-near-doha-know-more-about-it-now/))
- **Largest floodlit sports project at install.** "At the time, the lighting of
  the Lusail Circuit by Musco Lighting was the largest permanent venue sports
  lighting project in the world." Install took 175 days: 1,000+ structures,
  3 million kg of concrete, 500 km of wire.
  ([Travel And Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/get-ready-for-the-2026-motogp-in-qatar-lusail-circuit-lights-up-the-desert-night-near-doha-know-more-about-it-now/),
  [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit))
- **2023 full remodel by Tilke.** Between 2022–2023 paddock and race buildings
  were "completely replaced by a modern, contemporary ensemble" — all buildings
  "harmonize with each other and follow a coherent architectural design
  language." Client wanted "a prestigious and iconic landmark venue."
  ([Tilke portfolio](https://tilke.de/portfolio/lusail-race-track-qatar/) — via
  search summary; page 403s to direct fetch)
- **Record pit building.** Longest motorsport pitlane building in the world,
  **402.1 m**, **50 garages**, Guinness World Record (announced by Ashghal,
  4 Oct 2023). Includes race control tower, paddock, 5-star VIP areas.
  ([The Peninsula](https://thepeninsulaqatar.com/article/04/10/2023/ashghal-sets-guinness-world-record-for-longest-motorsport-pitlane-building-at-lusail-circuit),
  [iLoveQatar](https://www.iloveqatar.net/news/general/ashghal-achieves-new-guinness-world-records-longest-motorsport-pitlane-building-lusail-international-circuit))
- **Lusail city / Lusail Stadium are NEARBY but separate.** Lusail Stadium
  (Foster + Partners, 2022 World Cup final venue) is in Lusail city ~20 km north
  of Doha — a few km south/southeast of the circuit toward the marina, NOT
  adjacent to the track. Its **golden, bowl-shaped, perforated diagrid facade**
  is inspired by traditional Qatari **fanar lamps** with triangular patterns.
  It is a distant skyline element, not a trackside landmark.
  ([Lusail Stadium — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_Stadium),
  [Foster + Partners](https://www.fosterandpartners.com/projects/lusail-stadium/),
  [parametric-architecture](https://parametric-architecture.com/lusail-city-qatar-fifa-world-cup-2022/))

## Skyline & architecture / terrain

- **Terrain:** dead-flat pale sandy desert plain. Not rolling dunes — a low,
  barren, sun-bleached expanse. Sand colour is **pale tan / pale golden / buff**,
  bleaching lighter under floodlights at night.
  ([worldinsport](https://worldinsport.com/qatar-grand-prix-the-desert-challenge-in-doha/),
  [RacingCircuits.info](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/qatar/lusail-international-circuit.html))
- **Doha skyline:** the West Bay / Doha high-rise cluster is ~20–30 km south — at
  most a faint distant silhouette/glow on the horizon at night, not a prominent
  feature. Treat as a low, very distant skyline glow, not detailed towers.
  ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit))
- **Lusail city:** "city of the future" megaproject between circuit and Doha —
  marina towers, the gold Lusail Stadium, Katara-style towers. Visible as a
  distant lit cluster to the south/southeast, not at the trackside.
  ([CNN — Inside Qatar's city of the future](https://www.cnn.com/travel/article/lusail-qatar),
  [parametric-architecture](https://parametric-architecture.com/lusail-city-qatar-fifa-world-cup-2022/))
- **Architecture language (2023 Tilke):** modern, coherent, prestige; pale stone
  + glass + metal; paddock landscaping with **native plants** and **tree-branch
  metal shade canopies**; curved hospitality villas vs rectilinear pit slab. No
  literal Bedouin-tent peaked-canvas structures confirmed — the cultural cue is
  the organic "tree" canopies and (off-site) the fanar-lamp stadium, not tents.
  ([Tilke portfolio](https://tilke.de/portfolio/lusail-race-track-qatar/) — via
  search summary)

## Barriers, grandstands, kerbs, surface

- **Barriers:** major 2023 upgrade — **Tecpro barriers + tyre stacks installed
  around the entire venue** for F1, plus 500+ m of debris fencing for marshal
  protection. FIA Grade 1. So: blue Tecpro fronted by red/white tyre packs in
  places, white concrete walls behind, debris fencing on top.
  ([search summary, Qatar GP coverage](https://grokipedia.com/page/Qatar_Grand_Prix),
  [Geobrugg debris fencing](https://www.geobrugg.com/en/Losail-International-Circuit-194555.html))
- **Grandstands:** Main grandstand opposite the pits, 40,000 capacity, **only
  partially-covered stand** at the circuit (light canopy roof). Lusail Hill at
  T1 is a grassy general-admission mound, not a built stand.
  ([motorsporttickets grandstand guide](https://motorsporttickets.com/blog/qatar-grand-prix-grandstand-guide/),
  [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit))
- **Kerbs — the pyramid/sausage saga:** for F1, **sausage kerbs** were added at
  several corners to deter track-cutting. Their pointed **pyramid shape damaged
  tyres in 2023** (the infamous mandatory-stint/tyre-failure weekend). Afterward
  the tops of kerbs at **Turns 1, 2, 4, 10, 12, 13, 14 were rounded off** to
  remove the pointed pyramid profile. Standard red/white painted kerbs along the
  edges, with these raised sausage units at apex/exit lines.
  ([RaceFans — new kerbs & gravel](https://www.racefans.net/2024/11/26/new-kerbs-and-gravel-traps-in-response-to-2023-tyre-problems-at-losail/),
  [search summary](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/qatar/lusail-international-circuit.html))
- **Run-off & gravel:** generous tarmac run-off plus **added gravel strips** at
  several corner exits (post-2023) so drivers feel the track edge and can't gain
  advantage running wide. Pale-gold gravel traps beyond kerbs.
  ([RaceFans](https://www.racefans.net/2024/11/26/new-kerbs-and-gravel-traps-in-response-to-2023-tyre-problems-at-losail/),
  [search summary](https://www.racingcircuits.info/middle-east/qatar/lusail-international-circuit.html))
- **Surface:** resurfaced / upgraded for the 2023 F1 return — smooth, fresh dark
  asphalt; fast flowing layout (5.419 km, 1.068 km main straight). The new dark
  tarmac contrasts strongly with the pale desert and green artificial-grass
  border. ([search summary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit))

## Vegetation & water

- **Mostly barren.** The dominant ground cover is desert sand plus the
  **artificial-grass band** ringing the track. No natural lush vegetation.
- **Landscaped greenery is deliberate and sparse:** paddock landscaping uses
  **native (desert-adapted) plants** under the tree-branch canopies; **Lusail
  Hill** at T1 is a man-made green grassy mound. Expect a handful of **date
  palms / desert palms** as accent landscaping near buildings, not roadside
  forests. ([Tilke portfolio summary](https://tilke.de/portfolio/lusail-race-track-qatar/),
  [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusail_International_Circuit))
- **No natural water.** Desert venue; any "water" is the distant Lusail
  marina/Persian Gulf far to the east, not a trackside feature.

## Atmosphere / lighting / signage

- **Full-night race under floodlights — the headline visual.** Numbers from the
  lighting system: ~**1,000 light poles**, ~**3,600 light sources** originally
  (250 W / 1,500 W / 2,000 W metal-halide lamps), **~450 million lumens**, giving
  **~1,200–1,600 lux** at track level. Powered originally by 44 diesel
  generators (~13 MW each). Recently upgraded — **3,679 LED light sources** now
  illuminate the circuit.
  ([Travel And Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/get-ready-for-the-2026-motogp-in-qatar-lusail-circuit-lights-up-the-desert-night-near-doha-know-more-about-it-now/),
  [SpeedWeek — 3679 LED lights](https://www.speedweek.com/amp/formel1/news/184786/Formel-1-in-Losail-(Katar)-3679-LED-Lichtquellen.html?lang=en))
- **Light colour:** original metal-halide read **cool blue-white**; the new LED
  install is **crisp cool white** (high-CCT stadium LED). Track surface and pale
  sand glow bright; sky is a deep desert black with the lit ribbon visible from
  afar ("a glowing ribbon of speed on the edge of Doha").
  ([Travel And Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/get-ready-for-the-2026-motogp-in-qatar-lusail-circuit-lights-up-the-desert-night-near-doha-know-more-about-it-now/))
- **Signage / national identity:** title sponsor **Qatar Airways** (also the F1
  global airline partner) — maroon/burgundy + grey/oryx branding everywhere.
  **Ooredoo** (Qatari telco, red branding) is a long-standing Qatar-motorsport
  sponsor seen on boards/walls. Qatar national colours = **maroon and white**
  (the distinctive serrated maroon flag). Cultural motifs: pearl/dhow heritage,
  fanar-lamp (echoed by the gold stadium), oryx.
  ([Qatar Airways press release](https://www.qatarairways.com/press-releases/en-WW/228856-qatar-airways-the-official-global-airline-partner-of-formula-1-unveils-new-livery-ahead-of-formula-1-qatar-airways-qatar-grand-prix-20/),
  [Qatar GP coverage](https://grokipedia.com/page/Qatar_Grand_Prix))

## Gap analysis vs current game (specific)

Current build for Qatar (desert, night): *Lusail stadium bowl at s=0.55;
pearl-themed pale tower cluster + LEDs; mosque dome silhouette; dune rings;
floodlit grandstands.*

1. **Lusail Stadium at s=0.55 is misplaced and over-prominent.** The gold
   stadium is NOT adjacent to the track — it's a distant skyline object a few km
   away in Lusail city to the south. Placing a bowl trackside at 55% lap is
   wrong. If kept, it should be a **small distant gold bowl on the far horizon**,
   not a near landmark. Its facade is **gold diagrid / fanar-triangles**, not a
   plain pale bowl.
2. **Mosque dome silhouette is not characteristic of the circuit.** No mosque is
   a noted trackside landmark. The real signature buildings are the **long
   rectilinear record pit slab + race-control tower**, the **tree-branch metal
   paddock canopies**, and **curved hospitality villas** — none currently
   modelled.
3. **Dune rings are wrong terrain.** Real Lusail is a **flat barren plain**, not
   dunes. Replace dune silhouettes with a flat pale-sand horizon.
4. **Missing the artificial-grass border** — the most distinctive ground detail
   (green fake-turf band ringing dark asphalt against pale sand). Not represented.
5. **Floodlighting under-scaled.** Real venue is ~**1,000 masts / 3,679 LEDs** —
   "largest floodlit." Game floodlights should be unusually **dense and tall all
   the way around**, cool-white, and read as the dominant light source with a
   black desert sky.
6. **Pearl-tower cluster / generic city** doesn't match. The nearby built mass is
   **Lusail city** (marina towers, Katara towers, gold stadium) seen as a
   **distant lit cluster to one side**, plus a very faint **Doha skyline glow**
   far off — not a generic pearl-tower ring around the track.
7. **Pit building missing as the hero structure.** The 402 m record pit building
   on the start/finish straight is THE landmark and isn't called out.
8. **Kerbs/gravel/run-off** — no mention of the red/white kerbs, raised sausage
   kerbs, pale-gold gravel traps, or wide tarmac run-off that define the track
   edges.

## Concrete scenery recommendations (actionable)

1. **Flatten the terrain.** Replace `dune rings` with a flat, pale tan/buff
   sand plain extending to a low flat horizon. Bleach the sand slightly lighter
   under the floodlit night palette.
2. **Add the artificial-grass border.** Ring the asphalt with a continuous
   bright-green (slightly artificial, uniform) turf band 2–4 m wide between track
   edge / kerbs and the sand — Lusail's single most recognisable ground cue.
3. **Build the hero pit complex at s≈0.0 (start/finish straight).** A long
   (~400 m) low **rectilinear** pale-stone + glass slab with a glassy lit facade
   at night, topped mid-span by a taller **race-control tower**. Keep it straight,
   not wavy. Behind/beside it, a row of low pale **curved-front hospitality
   villas** and **tree-branch metal shade canopies** (organic branching struts)
   for the paddock — the distinctive Tilke 2023 motif.
4. **Main grandstand opposite the pits** with a light **partial canopy roof**
   (covered front rows, open back) facing the pit garages.
5. **Lusail Hill at Turn 1 (s≈0.05):** a raised green grassy spectator mound
   instead of a built stand.
6. **Max out the floodlights.** Make Qatar the densest floodlight stride of any
   track — tall slim grey masts every ~30 m all the way around, **cool-white
   LED** colour, strong HDR glow. This is the venue's identity ("largest
   floodlit"). Black desert sky; lit track ribbon visible into the distance.
7. **Distant skyline, not trackside city.** Put a **small distant cluster** off
   to one side (south/SE): a faint Doha/Lusail-city tower silhouette + a **small
   gold diagrid stadium bowl** (Lusail Stadium) low on the horizon, scaled tiny
   and far — replacing the trackside bowl at s=0.55 and the mosque dome. Optional
   faint horizon glow for Doha.
8. **Sparse desert palms** as accent landscaping near the pit/paddock buildings
   only (small clusters of date palms), not roadside forests; mostly barren sand
   elsewhere.
9. **Track edges:** red/white painted kerbs with occasional raised **sausage
   kerb** units at apexes/exits; **pale-gold gravel traps** beyond the kerbs at
   corner exits; wide light-tan tarmac run-off; fresh **dark smooth asphalt**
   (2023 resurface) contrasting the pale surroundings.
10. **Barriers & signage:** blue **Tecpro** barriers with **red/white tyre
    stacks** and white concrete walls + debris fencing. Sponsor/identity colours:
    **Qatar Airways maroon/grey**, **Ooredoo red**, Qatar **maroon-and-white**
    flag accents on armco and boards. Add pearl/dhow/oryx motif flourishes for
    national flavour.
